Convert small to large

When converting from a small unit \((\text{cm})\) to a larger unit \((\text{m})\) the number of units will decrease.

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{1\:\text{m}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}}=\:\PalAb{0\Ndp01\:\text{m}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{\text{cm}}=\PalAa{\text{cm}^2} )\\ \PalAb{0\Ndp01}\times\PalAb{0\Ndp01}&=\PalAb{0\Ndp0001}&&(\PalAb{\text{m}} \times \PalAb{\text{m}}=\PalAb{\text{m}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{0\Ndp0001\:\text{m}^2}\) or \(\PalAb{10^{-4}\:\text{m}^2}\).
 

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{mm}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{m}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{mm}}=\:\PalAb{0\Ndp001\:\text{m}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{mm}} \times \PalAa{\text{mm}}=\PalAa{\text{mm}^2} )\\ \PalAb{0\Ndp001}\times\PalAb{0\Ndp001}&=\PalAb{0\Ndp000001}&&(\PalAb{\text{m}} \times \PalAb{\text{m}}=\PalAb{\text{m}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{mm}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{10^{-6}\:\text{m}^2}\).
 

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{mm}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{cm}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{mm}}=\:\PalAb{0\Ndp1\:\text{cm}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{mm}} \times \PalAa{\text{mm}}=\PalAa{\text{mm}^2} )\\ \PalAb{0\Ndp1}\times\PalAb{0\Ndp1}&=\PalAb{0\Ndp01}&&(\PalAb{\text{cm}} \times \PalAb{\text{cm}}=\PalAb{\text{cm}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{mm}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{0\Ndp01\:\text{cm}^2}\).

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{km}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}}=\:\PalAb{10^{-5}\:\text{km}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{\text{cm}}=\PalAa{\text{cm}^2} )\\ \PalAb{10^{-5}}\times\PalAb{10^{-5}}&=\PalAb{10^{-10}}&&(\PalAb{\text{km}} \times \PalAb{\text{km}}=\PalAb{\text{km}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{10^{-10}\:\text{km}^2}\)

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{km}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}}=\:\PalAb{0\Ndp001\:\text{m}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{m}} \times \PalAa{\text{m}}=\PalAa{\text{m}^2} )\\ \PalAb{0\Ndp001}\times\PalAb{0\Ndp001}&=\PalAb{10^{-6}}&&(\PalAb{\text{km}} \times \PalAb{\text{km}}=\PalAb{\text{km}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{10^{-6}\:\text{cm}^2}\).

Notice that the use of the exponential form for writing small numbers improves readability:

\(0\Ndp0001=10^\PalAb{-4}\) and \(0\Ndp000001=10^\PalAb{-6}\)
