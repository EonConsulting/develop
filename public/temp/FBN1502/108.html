The relationship between formulas

Present value of annuity

Future value of annuity

\(\PalAa{P}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\)

\(\PalAb{S}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right]\)

where
\(\PalAa{P}\)
is the present value of the annuity
  \(\PalAb{S}\) is the future value of the annuity
  \(R\) is the regular payment
  \(i\) is the interest rate per payment interval
  \(n\)
 		 is the number of payment intervals
 

Relationship between present and future values of annuity

\(\PalAa{P}=\PalAb{S}\div(1+i)^n\)

and

\(\PalAb{S}=\PalAa{P}\times(1+i)^n\)


This relationship between the present and future values of an annuity can be illustrated as follows:
 

Present value of annuity

\(\PalAa{P}=\PalAb{S}\div(1+i)^n\)

Substitute \(\PalAb{S}\)

\(\PalAa{P}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right]\div(1+i)^n\)

Convert division to multiplication of fractions

\(\PalAa{P}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right]\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{(1+i)^n}\)

Multiply and simplify

\(\PalAa{P}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\)


and
 

Future value of annuity

\(\PalAb{S}=\PalAa{P}\times(1+i)^n\)

Substitute \(\PalAa{P}\)

\(\PalAb{S}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\times(1+i)^n\)

Convert to multiplication of fractions

\(\PalAb{S}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\times\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n}{1}\)

Multiply and simplify

\(\PalAb{S}=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right]\)
