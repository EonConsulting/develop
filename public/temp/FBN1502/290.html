Using a table to calculate marginal cost

The total cost of producing one pen is \(\Cur5\Cdp00\) and the total cost of producing two pens is \(\Cur9\Cdp00\).  

The marginal cost of expanding output by one unit is \(\Cur4\Cdp00\) only and is calculated as \(\Cur9\Cdp00-\Cur5\Cdp00=\Cur4\Cdp00\) (or \(9-5=4\)).  

The marginal cost of the second unit is the difference between the total cost of producing two units and the total cost of producing one unit.

Marginal cost \((\PalBb{MC})\) which is really an incremental cost can be written as

\(\PalBb{MC}=\displaystyle\frac{\text{change in } \PalAa {\text{total cost } }(\PalAa{TC})}{\text{change in } \PalAb{\text{number of units produced}}}\)

The total cost of producing four pens is \(\Cur16\Cdp00\) and the total cost of producing five pens is \(\Cur21\Cdp00\).

The marginal cost of the fifth unit is \(\Cur5\Cdp00\).  It is the difference between the total cost of producing five units and the total cost of producing four units and is calculated as

\(\begin{align} \PalBb{MC} &= \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{TC} \text{ of producing }5\text{ units}-\PalAa{TC} \text{ of producing }4\text{ units}}{\text{change in } \PalAb{\text{number of units produced}}} \\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\Cur21\Cdp00-\Cur16\Cdp00}{1}\\ &=\Cur5\Cdp00\text{.} \end{align}\)
