Example

Suppose you want a sample of \(9\) houses from a street with \(64\) houses.

Solution

Systematic sampling starts at a randomly chosen starting point in the population.
Then each subsequent \(\PalAb{k^{\text{th}}}\) element is chosen.  Use the following method for systematic sampling to determine the sample:

Step 1

Define the population.

The population is the group of \(\PalAa{64}\) houses.

Step 2

Determine the desired sample size.

The sample size is given as \(\PalAb9\).

Step 3

Obtain a list, preferably a randomised list of the population.

The list will be the group of \(\PalAa{64}\) houses on the street.

Step 4

To determine the value of \(\PalAb k\), divide the size of the population by the sample size.

\(\begin{align} \PalAb k&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{64}}{\PalAb9}\\&=\PalAb{7\Ndp11} \end{align}\)

So, each \(\PalAb{7^{\text{th}}}\)house will be chosen.

Step 5

Start at some random place at the top of the population list. 

Here we started at house number two.

Step 6

Starting at that point, take every \(\PalAb{k^{\text{th}}}\) element on the list until the desired sample size is reached.

Starting at the second house, take every \(\PalAb{7^{\text{th}}}\) house on the list until the desired sample size of \(\PalAb9\) is reached.
