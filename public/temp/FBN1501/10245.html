Visualization

When a number is a perfect square, the square root of that number can easily be determined.

  = =
     

Make two similar groups of factors:

  

Take one of the similar groups out of the root to obtain the answer:
 

\(\sqrt{x^2y^6}\)

\(=\)

\(\sqrt{\PalAa x\PalAb{y^3}\times \PalAa x\PalAb{y^3}}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa x\PalAb{y^3}\)

  

If a calculator is not used and you are calculating square roots of numbers that are not perfect squares, part of the answer can be given as a root.

  = =
     

Make two similar groups and a remainder:

  

Take one of the similar groups out of the root.  The remainder is left under the root:
 

\(\sqrt{x^3y^7}\)

\(=\)

\(\sqrt{\PalAa x\PalAb{y^3}\times \PalAa x\PalAb{y^3}\times x y}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa x\PalAb{y^3}\sqrt{xy}\)
