Recap

Qualitative data are data that are not given numerically, for example your favourite colour, place of birth, favourite food and type of car.

Quantitative data are numerical.  There are two types of quantitative data:

  • Discrete data can only be specific numeric values.  They include everything that can be considered as separate units because of their nature, for example shoe size, number of brothers and number of cars in a car park.
  • Continuous data can be any numerical values, for example height, mass and length.
