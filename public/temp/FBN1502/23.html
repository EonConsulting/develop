The \(y\)-intercept of a straight line

The point where the line cuts the \(\PalAa y\)-axis is called the \(\PalAa y\)-intercept.

At this point, where \(\PalAb {x = 0}\), the value of \(\PalAa y\) is the following in general and in this specific example case:

General case

Straight line equation

\(\PalAa y=a\PalAb x+b\)

Substitute \(\PalAb {x=0}\)

\(\begin{align} \PalAa y &=a(\PalAb 0)+b\\ &= a \times \PalAb 0 +b \\ & = 0+b\\&=\PalAa b \end{align} \)

Conclusion

Intercept on \(\PalAa y\)-axis is equal to constant term \(\PalAa b\) in equation for straight line.
The \(\PalAa y\)-intercept  is the point \(\PalAa{(0; b)}\).

Example case

Equation of line

\(\PalAa y=-2\PalAb x+4\)

Substitute \(\PalAb {x=0}\)

\(\begin{align} \PalAa y &=-2(\PalAb 0)+4\\ &= -2 \times \PalAb 0 +4 \\ & = 0+4\\&=4 \end{align} \)

Conclusion

The intercept on the \(\PalAa y\)-axis is equal to \(\PalAa4\).
The \(\PalAa y\)-intercept  is the point \(\PalAa{(0; 4)}\).

The straight line \(y = ax + b\), cuts the \(\PalAa y\)-axis at the point \(\PalAa{(0; b)}\).  
Therefore, the line \(y = -2x+4 \), cuts the \(\PalAa y\)-axis at \(\PalAa{y = b = 4}\).
