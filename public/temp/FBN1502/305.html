Farmer (Cont.)

A peanut farmer’s cost function in rand, is given by \(\PalAa {C(\PalAb x)=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+100}\)
where \(\PalAb x\) represents the number of kilograms of peanuts produced.  The cost is in rand.

The revenue function is given by \(\PalCd{R(\PalAb x)=31\PalAb x}\), that is each kilogram of peanuts produced is sold at \(\Cur31\Cdp00\).

The farmer's profit is the difference between income earned by selling (revenue) and the cost incurred for the production of the peanuts.
The profit function is given by \(\PalBc{P(\PalAb x)=-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+31\PalAb x-100}\).

If the farmer produces \({1\Tsp000}\) kilograms and he is interested in knowing how the profit will change if one extra kilogram is produced, then he wants to determine the marginal profit at a production level of \(\PalAb{1\Tsp000}\) kilograms.

He uses the following procedure:

The profit function is

\(\PalBc{P(\PalAb x)}\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+31\PalAb x-100\text.\)

Determine the derivative of the first term: 

\(\displaystyle\frac{d}{d\PalAb x}\left( -\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100} \right)\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{2\PalAb x^{2-1}}{100}\)

 

  

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x}{50}\)

Determine the derivative of the second term:

\(\displaystyle\frac{d}{d\PalAb x}(31\PalAb x)\)

\(=\)

\(31\)

Determine the derivative of the third term:

\(\displaystyle\frac{d}{d\PalAb x}(-100)\)

\(=\)

\(0\)

So, if the profit function is

\(\PalBc{P(\PalAb x)}\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+31\PalAb x-100\text,\)

then the derivative of the profit function is

\(\PalBa{P'(\PalAb x)}\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x}{50}+31\text.\)

Substitute

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb{1\Tsp000}\)

into the derivative

\(\PalBa{P'(\PalAb x)}\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x}{50}+31\)

to calculate the marginal profit at a production level of \(\PalAb{1\Tsp000}\) kilograms:

\(\PalBa{P'(\PalAb {1\Tsp000})}\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb {1\Tsp000}}{50}+31\)

 

 

\(=\)

\(\PalBa{11}\text.\)

This means, for an extra kilogram produced at a production level of \(\PalAb{1\Tsp000}\) kilograms, the profit will increase by \(\PalBa{\Cur11\Cdp00}\).  It is illustrated in the graphs of the profit and marginal profit functions drawn below.

Profit

\(\PalBc{P(x)=- \displaystyle\frac{x^2}{100}+31x-100}\)

Marginal Profit

\(\PalBa{P'(x)=-\displaystyle\frac{x}{50}+31}\)
