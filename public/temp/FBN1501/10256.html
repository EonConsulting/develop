Case 1

\( x\) percent of \( y\) is \( z\)

To determine the percentage of a number, we write "\(\PalAa x\) percent of \(\PalAb y\) is \(\PalBc z\)" as:

\(\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y=\PalBc z\)

What is \({80\%}\) of \({300}\)?  In this case \(\PalAa x\) equals \(\PalAa{80}\), \(\PalAb y\) equals \(\PalAb{300}\) and \(\PalBc z\) is unknown.  So, the answer is calculated as:

\(\PalBc z=\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y=\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{80}{100}}\times \PalAb {300}=\PalBc{240}\)

Example

A salesman receives a commission that is \(2\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}\%\) of his sales.  If his sales for the month total \(\Cur320\Tsp000\Cdp00\), what is his commission?  

So, we need to calculate \(2\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}\%\) of his sales of \(\Cur320\Tsp000\Cdp00\) to determine his commission.
In this case \(\PalAa x\) equals \(\PalAa{2\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}}\), \(\PalAb y\) equals \(\PalAb{320\Tsp000}\) and \(\PalBc z\) is the unknown commission.
His commission is calculated as:

\(\begin{align}\PalBc z&=\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y\\ &=\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{2\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}}{100}}\times \PalAb {320\Tsp000}\\ &=\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{2\Ndp5}{100}}\times \PalAb {320\Tsp000}\\ &=\PalAa{0\Ndp025}\times \PalAb {320\Tsp000}\\ &=\PalBc{8\Tsp000}\end{align}\)

His commission is \(\Cur8\Tsp000\Cdp00\).

What we basically did was to change the \(2\frac{1}{2}\%\) percentage to a decimal:

\(\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{2\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}}{100}}=\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{2\Ndp5}{100}}=\PalAa{0\Ndp025}\)

And then multiply the decimal with his total sales of \(\Cur320\Tsp000\Cdp00\) to determine his commission:

\(\PalAa{0\Ndp025}\times\PalAb{320\Tsp000}=\PalBc{8\Tsp000}\)
