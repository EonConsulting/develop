Example

This example illustrates the method of addition and subtraction of fractions.

Calculate the following:

\(5\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}+2\displaystyle\frac{5}{6}-7\displaystyle\frac{1}{3}\)

Method

 

Calculate the following:

\(\displaystyle5\frac{3}{4}+2\displaystyle\frac{5}{6}-7\displaystyle\frac{1}{3}\)

Step 1

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions.

\(=\displaystyle\frac{23}{4}+\displaystyle\frac{17}{6}-\displaystyle\frac{22}{3}\)

Step 2

Change each fraction into an equivalent fraction so all the fractions have the same denominator, the lowest common denominator.  The smallest number divisible by the denominators \(4\), \(6\) and \(3\), is \(\PalAa{12}\).

\(=\displaystyle\frac{23\PalAa{\times3}}{4\PalAa{\times3}}+\displaystyle\frac{17\PalAa{\times2}}{6\PalAa{\times2}}-\displaystyle\frac{22\PalAa{\times4}}{3\PalAa{\times4}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{69}{\PalAa{12}}+\displaystyle\frac{34}{\PalAa{12}}-\displaystyle\frac{88}{\PalAa{12}}\)

Step 3

Add or subtract the numerators.
Write the result above the line.
Keep the lowest common denominator below the line.

\(=\displaystyle\frac{{69}+{34}-{88}}{\PalAa{12}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{15}{12}\)

Step 4

Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and denominator by the GCF, in this case \(\PalAb3\).

\(=\displaystyle\frac{15\PalAb{\div3}}{12\PalAb{\div3}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{5}{4}\)

Step 5

Write the improper fraction as a mixed number.

\(=1\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\)
