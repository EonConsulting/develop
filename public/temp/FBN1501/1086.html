Formula

A simple index involves one product only.  A simple index is written as:

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{\text{number}}\text{ in }\PalAa{\text{given year}}}{\PalBc{\text{number}}\text{ in }\PalAb{\text{base year}}}\times100\)

 

The formula for a simple index is:

\(I_\PalAa n=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc n_\PalAa n}{\PalBc n_\PalAb0}\times100\)
where \(I_\PalAa n\) is the index for the \(\PalAa{n^{\text{th}}}\) period
  \(\PalBc n_\PalAa n\) is the number in the \(\PalAa{n^{\text{th}}}\) period
  \(\PalBc n_\PalAb0\) is the number in the base period

 

This number can represent   a price,
    a quantity,
    a value in rand,
or   a number of people.
