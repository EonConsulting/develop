How compound interest works 2

Suppose Paul invests \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) for three years at a compounded interest rate of \(10\%\) per annum.  Calculate the amount in his account after three years:

Original balance

 

\(\Cur 600 \Cdp 00\)

Balance end Year 1

  

\(\Cur 600 \Cdp 00+(\Cur 600 \Cdp 00\times{0\Ndp10})=\Cur 660 \Cdp 00\)

 

\(=\)

\(\Cur 600 \Cdp 00\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}=\Cur 660 \Cdp 00\)

Balance beginning Year 2

  

\(\Cur 660 \Cdp 00\)

Balance end Year 2

  

\(\Cur 660 \Cdp 00+(\Cur 660 \Cdp 00\times0\Ndp10)\)

 

\(=\)

\(\Cur 660 \Cdp 00\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}=\Cur 726 \Cdp 00\)

Balance beginning Year 3

 

\(\Cur 726 \Cdp 00\)

Balance end Year 3

 

\(\Cur 726 \Cdp 00+(\Cur 726 \Cdp 00\times0\Ndp10)\)

 

\(=\)

\(\Cur 726 \Cdp 00\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}=\Cur 798 \Cdp 60\)

To calculate the amount in his account in another way, let \( i\) denote the percentage interest the invested money earns each year, where \( i\) is expressed as a decimal. Then calculate the compounded interest. It can be concluded that each year the invested money is multiplied by \(\PalAb{(1+i)}\).

Since Paul invested his money at a compounded interest rate of \(10\%\) per annum, in this example \(i=0\Ndp10\) and \(\PalAb{(1+i)}=\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\).

In account after three years

To calculate the amount in his account after three years, we begin with our original deposit of \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) and multiply by \(\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\) a total of three times. The result is:

Original deposit

 

\(\Cur600\Cdp00\)

Multiply three times

\(\times\)

\(\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\)

Exponential form

\(=\)

\(\Cur600\Cdp00\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}^3\)

Calculate

\(=\)

\(\Cur798\Cdp60\)

In account after five years

So if we want to know how much he has in his account say after five years, we begin with our original deposit of \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) and multiply by \(1\Ndp10\) a total of five times. The result is:

Original deposit

 

\(\Cur600\Cdp00\)

Multiply five times

\(\times\)

\(\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}\)

Exponential form

\(=\)

\(\Cur600\Cdp00\times\PalAb{1\Ndp10}^5\)

Calculate

\(=\)

\(\Cur966\Cdp31\)
