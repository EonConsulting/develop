The slope of a straight line: Recap

The steepness at which straight lines ascend or descend is called
the slope.  The slope \(a\) is the ratio of the change in \(y\) values to
a given change in \(x\) values.

In terms of two arbitrary points \(\\ P_1\), with coordinates \((\PalAb {x_1};\PalAa{y_1})\), and \(P_2\), with coordinates \((\PalAb {x_2};\PalAa{y_2})\), on the straight line, we can write:

\(\text{slope} = a = \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{y_2-y_1}}{\PalAb{x_2-x_1}} \)

This is depicted in the following figure:

 

Now it is clear that:

   \(a\) is a measure of the steepness of a straight line. 
  The greater the change in \(y\) for a given change in \(x\), the steeper the line.

We also know that:

  If \(a>0\), then the line is ascending from left to right.
  If \(a<0\), then the line is descending from left to right.
