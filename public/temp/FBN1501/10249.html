Part to one

Consider the ratio \(\PalAa9 \text{ to }\PalAb{12}\) or \(\PalAa9:\PalAb{12}\) or \(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa9}{\PalAb{12}}\).

Note the last form of the ratio is nothing more than a fraction which can be reduced:

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa9}{\PalAb{12}}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa9\div3}{\PalAb{12}\div3}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa3}{\PalAb{4}}\)

Another way in which a ratio is reduced is by expressing the fraction in decimal form, in which case it is compared to one:

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa3}{\PalAb{4}}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa3\div4}{\PalAb{4}\div4}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{0\Ndp75}}{\PalAb{1}}\)   or    \(\PalAa{0\Ndp75}:\PalAb1\)

Be very careful that you place the quantity that you are comparing to, second.

In other words, the ratio of width to length is not the same as that of length to width:

\(\PalAa{\text{width}}:\PalAb{\text{length}}\ne \PalAb{\text{length}}:\PalAa{\text{width}}\)
