Negative powers

Using the negative power property, we know that:

\(x^{\PalAb{-4}}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb1}{x^\PalAb4}\)

From this, we can deduce the following:

If the numerator and denominator of a fraction have negative
 powers, you make them positive by moving them up or down.

\(\begin{align} \displaystyle\frac{x^{\PalAa{-a}}}{y^{\PalAb{-b}}}=\displaystyle\frac{y^\PalAb{b}}{x^\PalAa a}&&x\neq0 \end{align}\)
