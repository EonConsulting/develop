Population vs sample

A population is a collection of all items of interest or under investigation

Typically there are too many items in a population to consider every one.  
Examples of populations include the following:

  • Names of all registered voters in South Africa.
  • Incomes of all families living in Cape Town.
  • Annual returns of all stocks traded on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange.
  • Semester marks of all the students at Unisa.

A sample is the observed subset of a population.

It is very important that this sample must be representative of the population.  How does one manage this? There are several methods to choose a representative group.
