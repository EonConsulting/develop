Quartile deviation

Another measure of dispersion is the range of the middle half of the data.
The middle \(\PalAb{50\%}\) of the data lies between \(\PalAa{Q_1}\) and \(\PalBc{Q_3}\).

The measure \(\PalAb{Q_D}\) is called the quartile deviation and is the measurement of the dispersion of the data around the median.

\(\PalAb{Q_D}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{Q_3}-\PalAa{Q_1}}{2}\)

As with the median, the quartile deviation does not use all the observations.  It ignores outliers since the top \(\PalBc{25\%}\) and the bottom \(\PalAa{25\%}\) of the data values are not taken into account.

An outlier is a data point that is significantly different numerically from the other data points in a sample.  The term is used in statistical studies and can point to abnormalities in the data set studied, or errors in the measurements taken.

  • If a measurement falls to the right of \(\PalBc{Q_3}\) of a set of data, then we know that it is in the top \(\PalBc{25\%}\) of the data.
     
  • If a measurement falls to the left of \(\PalAa{Q_1}\) of a set of data, then we know that it is in the bottom \(\PalAa{25\%}\) of the data.
