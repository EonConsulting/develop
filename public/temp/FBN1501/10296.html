Conversion tables

To convert between units you need to multiply or divide by \(10\), \(100\), \(1\Tsp000\) and so on.

Second example

Tom is \(\PalAa{165\:\text{cm}}\) tall.  How tall is he in \(\PalAb{\text{m}}\)?

You are going from a smaller unit \((\PalAa{\text{cm}})\) to a larger one \((\PalAb{\text{m}})\).  The number of metres will be less than the number of \(\text{cm}\).  So it will be a division calculation.

There are \(100\:\PalAa{\text{cm}}\) in \(1\:\PalAb{\text{m}}\), so you need to divide by \(100\):

\(\begin{align}\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{165}}{100}=\PalAb{1\Ndp65}&&\left(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{\text{cm}}}{100}=\PalAb{\text{m}}\right) \end{align}\)

Tom is \(\PalAb{1\Ndp65\:\text{m}}\) tall.
