Fourth root (Perfect)

Let us consider the case when the numbers work out perfectly for determining the fourth root.

\(\sqrt[4]{ \PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}} \)

An alternative method to determine the fourth root can be used.

Consider \(x^4\).  Four is a multiple of four.  The fourth root of \(x^4\) is \(x^{4\div4}\) and that is equal to \(x^1\).

Also consider \(y^8\).  Eight is a multiple of four.  The fourth root of \(y^8\) is \(y^{8\div4}\) and that is equal to \(y^2\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^8}}=\PalAa{x}^{\PalAa4\div4}\PalAb y^{\PalAb8\div4}=\PalAa{x}\PalAb{y^2}\)
