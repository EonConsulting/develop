Calculate \(n\): Recap

How long will Lucy have to wait before her \(\Cur2\Tsp500\Cdp00\) invested at \(6\%\) simple interest per year accumulates to \(\Cur3\Tsp100\Cdp00\)?

We can manipulate the simple interest formula \(I=Pin\) and the accumulated sum formula \(S=P+I\).

The following is given in the question:

\(\begin{align} P&=\Cur2\Tsp500\Cdp00\\ S&=\Cur3\Tsp100\Cdp00\\ i&=6\%=\frac{6}{100}=0\Ndp06\text{ per year} \end{align}\)

We need to calculate the time period \(n\).  

First method

The time is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} n&=\frac{S-P}{Pi}\\ &=\frac{\Cur3\Tsp100\Cdp00-\Cur2\Tsp500\Cdp00}{\Cur2\Tsp500\Cdp00\times0\Ndp06}\\ &=\frac{\Cur600\Cdp00}{\Cur150\Cdp00}\\ &=4 \end{align}\)

So, the time period is \(4\) years.

Second method

Alternatively, the time can be calculated as in the following way.

The interest earned is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} I&=S-P\\ &=\Cur3\Tsp100\Cdp00-\Cur2\Tsp500\Cdp00\\ &=\Cur600\Cdp00 \end{align}\)

So, the interest earned is \(\Cur600\Cdp00\).

From the basic interest formula \(I=Pin\), we can change the subject of the formula to \(n=\displaystyle\frac{I}{Pi}\).

We can now calculate the time as:

\(\begin{align} n&=\frac{I}{Pi}\\ &=\frac{\Cur600\Cdp00}{\Cur2\Tsp500\Cdp00\times0\Ndp06}\\ &=\frac{\Cur600\Cdp00}{\Cur150\Cdp00}\\ &=4 \end{align}\)

Again, we find that the time is \(4\) years.

Conclusion

Note that these two methods do not differ much from each other.

In the first case the formula for simple interest \(S=P(1+in)\) is manipulated to \(n=\displaystyle\frac{S-P}{Pi}\), which is actually the same as \(n=\displaystyle\frac{I}{Pi}\).

In the second case the interest \((I)\) is calculated first and then substituted into the formula \(n=\displaystyle\frac{I}{Pi}\).
