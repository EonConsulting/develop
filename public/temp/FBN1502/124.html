Interest compounded quarterly and monthly

Suppose Paul invests \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) for three years at a compounded interest rate of \(10\%\) per annum. Compare the accumulated sum of his investment when the interest is compounded quarterly and monthly. 

Interest is compounded quarterly

If Paul's interest is compounded quarterly, then interest is compounded four times a year (every three months), so his periodic interest rate is the interest rate of \(10\%\) divided by four. 

Quarterly period interest rate

\(\displaystyle\frac{1}{\PalAa4}\text{year}\:I=I\div\PalAa4=10\%\div4=2\Ndp5\%=\PalAa{0\Ndp025}\)

Principal every quarter

\(= P\:\text{from previous quarter}\times(1+\PalAa{0\Ndp025})=1\Ndp025\)

Consequently, every three months (four times a year) his principal is multiplied by the factor \((1+\PalAa{0\Ndp025})=1\Ndp025\).

For interest compounded quarterly, the periodic
interest rate is a quarter of the annual interest rate.

Interest is compounded monthly

If Paul's interest is compounded monthly, then interest is compounded twelve times a year (every month), so his periodic interest rate is the interest rate of \(10\%\) divided by twelve. 

Monthly period interest rate

\(\displaystyle\frac{1}{\PalAb{12}}\text{year}\:I=I\div\PalAb{12}=10\%\div12=0\Ndp83\%=\PalAb{0\Ndp0083}\)

Principal every month

\(= P\:\text{from previous month}\times(1+\PalAb{0\Ndp0083})=1\Ndp0083\)

Consequently, every month (twelve times a year) his principal is multiplied by the factor \((1+\PalAb{0\Ndp0083})=1\Ndp0083\).

For interest compounded monthly, the periodic
interest rate is a twelfth of the annual interest rate.
