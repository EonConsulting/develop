Special case of straight line parallel to \(y\)-axis: Examples

When a line with undefined slope is drawn, it will look vertical, just like a steep cliff.  The sketch below shows three vertical lines, all with undefined (infinite) slope.

Notice that all the lines have similar equations in that they all look like \(x=\text{some value}\).  Just like a vertical cliff face, a vertical line does not progress, or change, in the horizontal, or \(x\), direction.  Every point on the line has the same \(x\) value.

Note the vertical line in the sketch above.  The \(x\)-coordinate of every point on the vertical line is \(2\).  The \(y\)-coordinate can have any value.
