Practical applications of quadratic functions

A parabola is the curve that is produced by graphing a quadratic or second-degree equation.  Parabolas have many practical applications.

Parabolas occur frequently in the real world.  Consider the following examples of parabolas we can find all around us.

Suspension bridge

The parabola can be seen most visibly when looking up at a suspension bridge.  The trace formed by the cables as they suspend from the highest point to the lowest is in the shape of a parabola.

Projectile motion

During a basketball game, the shots taken by the players trace out a parabola in the air.  In fact, this is probably one of the most common applications of the parabola, namely the projectile motion.

Satellite dish

In addition to the applications mentioned above, parabolic surfaces have technological applications. For example, satellite dishes are in the shape of parabolic surfaces.

Reflector

Another technological application of the parabolic surface can be found in reflectors.  The headlamps of your car, for example, are in this shape.  
When you are driving on a dark road and have your brights on, it is the parabolic surface of your headlamp reflectors that enable you to see further ahead.  The bulb is placed at a special point called the focus of the parabola.  The light is shone from the focus and the curved surface shoots the light out of the bottom of the parabola.
