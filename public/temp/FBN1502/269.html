Four types of inequalities

Greater than

With a greater than inequality, all numbers larger than \(x\), but not equal to \(x\), fit the inequality.

Example

\(\PalCa{x>2}\)

Any number larger than \(\PalCa 2\) fits the inequality, for example \(\PalCa{2\Ndp1};\PalCa{2\Ndp2};\PalCa{3};\PalCa{4};\PalCa{17};\dots\)

Less than

With a less than inequality, all numbers smaller than \(x\), but not equal to \(x\), fit the inequality.

Example

\(\PalCb{x<2}\)

Any number smaller than \(\PalCb 2\) fits the inequality, for example \(\PalCb{1\Ndp9};\PalCb{1\Ndp8};\PalCb{1};\PalCb{0};\PalCb{-1};\PalCb{-100};\dots\)

Greater than or equal to

With a greater than or equal to inequality, all numbers greater than \(x\) and equal to \(x\), fit the inequality.

Example

\(\PalCc{x\geq2}\)

Any number that is \(\PalCc2\) or is larger than \(\PalCc2\) fits the inequality, for example \(\PalCc{2};\PalCc{2\Ndp1};\PalCc{2\Ndp2};\PalCc{3};\PalCc{4};\PalCc{17}\dots \)

Less than or equal to

With a less than or equal to inequality, all numbers smaller than \(x\) and equal to \(x\), fit the inequality.

Example

\(\PalCd{x\leq2}\)

Any number that is \(\PalCd2\) or is smaller than \(\PalCd2\) fits the inequality, for example \(\PalCd2;\PalCd{1\Ndp9};\PalCd{1\Ndp8};\PalCd{1};\PalCd{0};\PalCd{-1};\PalCd{-100};\dots\)
