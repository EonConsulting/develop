Product of factors

The power of a product property states that a product raised to a power
 is equal to the product of its factors raised to the same power.

\((\PalAa a\PalAb b)^n=\PalAa a^n\PalAb b^n\)

You can use the power of a product property to separate factors or to group them together.

For example, consider the number:     \(12\)
Which can be written as the product of its factors:   \(=\) \(\PalAa3\times\PalAb4\)
Thus the square of \(12\) can be written as: \(12^2\) \(=\) \((\PalAa3\times\PalAb4)^2\)
Applying the power of a product property: \(12^2\) \(=\) \(\PalAa3^2\times\PalAb4^2\)
If we apply the exponents, we find that: \(12^2\) \(=\) \(144\)
  \(\PalAa3^2\) \(=\) \(\PalAa9\)
  \(\PalAb4^2\) \(=\) \(\PalAb{16}\)
And it is true that: \(144\) \(=\) \(\PalAa9\times\PalAb{16}\)
