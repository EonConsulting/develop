Example

The Fit For Life running club is trying to raise money for new equipment.
The men are selling water bottles for \(\PalAa{\Cur20\Cdp00}\) a bottle and the women are selling sweat bands for \(\PalAb{\Cur40\Cdp00}\) each.  The women expect to sell at least \(100\)
sweat bands.  Jointly they must raise more than \(\Cur8\Tsp000\Cdp00\).  

  Let \(\PalAa x\) represent the number of water bottles sold.
  Let \(\PalAb y\) represent the number of sweat bands sold.

Write a system of inequalities that represents this situation.

Solution

Write the inequality representing the income.

More than \(\Cur8\Tsp000\Cdp00\) implies \(>\): \(20\PalAa x+40\PalAb y\) \(>\) \(8\Tsp000\) or
  \(2\PalAa x+4\PalAb y\) \(>\) \(800\)

Write the inequality representing the sales of the sweat bands.

At least \(100\) sweat bands implies \(\geq100\): \(\PalAb y\) \(\geq\) \(100\)

Write the inequalities representing positive or zero sales.

Cannot sell a negative number of water bottles: \(\PalAa x\) \(\geq\) \(0\)
Cannot sell a negative number of sweat bands: \(\PalAb y\) \(\geq\) \(0\)
