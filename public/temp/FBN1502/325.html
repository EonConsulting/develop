Calculate \(P\): Changing the subject of the formula

Sometimes, we do not only consider the basic formula

\(I=Pin\)

but also rearrange it, in order to obtain formulas for each variable respectively in terms of the others.  We call this "changing the subject of the formula".

In the activity on the next page you will be given the formula \(I=Pin\) .The values of \(I\), \(i\) and \(n\), will be given and you will have to calculate the value of \(P\).
