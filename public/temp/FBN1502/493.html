Question 1

An appliance store knows that if it sells its new gadgets for \(\Cur50\Cdp00\) each it can sell \(200\) per month, and if it sells the same gadgets for \(\Cur40\Cdp00\), it will sell \(300\) per month.
Let \(p\) represent the price of the gadgets and let \(n\) represent the number of gadgets that is sold per month.  Assume that the relationship between price and sales is linear and that the sales are dependent on the price of the gadgets.  The linear equation you could use to predict sales for other prices, is:

  1. \(10p+n=700\)
  2. \(10p+n=600\)
  3. \(10p-n=700\)
  4. \(10p-n=200\)
  5. None of the above
