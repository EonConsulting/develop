Example

Convert the following statement to a mathematical expression:

"The sum of a man's age and half his son's age."

Solution

Step 1 There are two variables here, because we do not know the man's age nor his son's.  Let us use different variables than \(x\) and \(y\).  Let us call the man's age \(\PalAa m\) and the son's age \(\PalAb s\).
Step 2 The word "sum" means we are adding the two terms.
Step 3 The son's age \(\PalAb s\) needs to be divided in half before it gets into the expression.
Step 4 We can write this expression as \(\PalAa m\PalBc+\PalAb{\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}s}\).

So, the mathematical expression is:

\(m+{\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}s}\)
