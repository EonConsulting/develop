\(>\) and \(<\)

The concept greater than is denoted by the symbol \(\PalAa>\).
The concept less than is denoted by the symbol \(\PalAb<\).

We say \(b\) is greater than \(a\) if and only if the difference \(b-a\) is positive.
Thus  \(b\PalAa >a\)   So, \(4\PalAa>2\)  
means \(b-a\PalAa>0\) . gives \(4-2=2\) and \(2\PalAa>0\) .

We say \(b\) is less than \(a\) if and only if the difference \(b-a\) is negative.
Thus \(b\PalAb <a\)   So, \(2\PalAb<4\)  
means \(b-a\PalAb<0\) . gives \(2-4=-2\) and \(-2\PalAb<0\) .

If \(b=a\) or \(a=b\) then \(a\) and \(b\) are the same number.
