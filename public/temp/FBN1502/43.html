Determine the equation of a straight line using two points

The general equation for a straight line is \(y = \PalBb ax + \PalBc b \)
 with the slope as \(\PalBb a= \displaystyle\frac{\PalAb{y_2-y_1}}{\PalAa{x_2-x_1}} \) for any two points.

With this knowledge on straight lines, we are able to determine the specific equation for the straight line passing through two given points.

Method to determine equation

Step 1

Use general equation for a straight line \(y = \PalBb ax + \PalBc b \) and the slope \(\PalBb a= \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{y_2-y_1}}{\PalAb{x_2-x_1}} \) for any two points \((\PalAb {x_1};\PalAa{y_1})\) and \((\PalAb {x_2};\PalAa{y_2})\).

Step 2

Find the value of \(\PalBb a\), the slope of the straight line.

Step 3

Find the value of \(\PalBc b\).

Step 4

Substitute \(\PalBb a\) and \(\PalBc b\) into the general equation \(y = \PalBb ax + \PalBc b \) to get the specific equation of the straight line.

Example

Determine the equation for the straight line passing through the points \((1; 3)\) and \((3;7)\).

Find the value of slope \(\PalBb a\)

      

Use the two points on the line

\((\PalAb {x_1};\PalAa{y_1})\)

\(=\)

\((\PalAb1; \PalAa3)\)

and

\((\PalAb {x_2};\PalAa{y_2})\)

\(=\)

\((\PalAb3;\PalAa7)\)

Formula for slope of line

\(\PalBb a\)

\(=\)

\( \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{y_2-y_1}}{\PalAb{x_2-x_1}} \)

Substitute \((\PalAb {x_1};\PalAa{y_1})\) and \((\PalAb {x_2};\PalAa{y_2})\)

 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{7-3}} {\PalAb{3-1}}\)

Calculate and simplify

 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{4}{2}=2\)

Value of slope \(\PalBb a\) is

\(\PalBb a\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBb 2\)

 

General equation for straight line

\(y\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb ax + \PalBc b \)

Substitute \(\PalBb a=\PalBb2\)

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(( \PalBb 2)x + \PalBc b\)

General equation reduces to

\(y\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb 2x + \PalBc b\)

 

Find the value of \(\PalBc b\)

      

Use either point on the line

\((\PalAb {x_1};\PalAa{y_1})\)

\(=\)

\((\PalAb1; \PalAa3)\)

or

\((\PalAb {x_2};\PalAa{y_2})\)

\(=\)

\((\PalAb3;\PalAa7)\)

Use for example first point, i.e.

\((\PalAb {x};\PalAa{y})\)

\(=\)

\((\PalAb1; \PalAa3)\)

Equation with slope \(\PalBb a=\PalBb2\)  

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb 2\PalAb x + \PalBc b\)

Substitute point in equation

\((\PalAa 3)\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb 2(\PalAb 1) + \PalBc b\)

Calculate and simplify

\(\PalAa3\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb2\times\PalAb1 + \PalBc b\)

 

\(3\)

\(=\)

\(2+\PalBc b\)

 

\(2+\PalBc b\)

\(=\)

\(3\)

Substract \(-2\) both sides

\(2-2+\PalBc b\)

\(=\)

\(3-2\)

Value of \(\PalBc b\) is

\(\PalBc b\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBc1\)

Substitute \(\PalBc b=\PalBc 1\) into the equation

\(y\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb 2x + (\PalBc 1)\)

So, the equation of the line is

\(y\)

\(=\)

\( \PalBb 2x + \PalBc 1\)

The equation for the line passing through the two points \((1;3)\) and \((3;7)\) is therefore \(y=2x+1\).

Note

It does not matter which point we call \((x_1 ; y_1)\) and which \((x_2 ; y_2)\).  Had we numbered them the other way around above we would have found that \((x_1 ; y_1) = (3; 7)\) and \((x_2 ; y_2) = (1; 3)\).  The slope would be still be calculated as before:

\(\PalBb a=\displaystyle\frac{3-7}{1-3}=\displaystyle\frac{-4}{-2}=\PalBb2\)

We could also just as well have used the point \((3; 7)\) instead of \((1;3)\) to find the value of \(\PalBc b\).  Using point \((3; 7)\), we would calculate the value of \(\PalBc b\) as follows:

\(\begin{align} 7&=2\times3+\PalBc b \\ 7&=6+\PalBc b \\ 7-6&=6+\PalBc b-6 \\ \PalBc b&=\PalBc1 \end{align}\)
