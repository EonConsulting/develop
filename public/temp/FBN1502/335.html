Rules

We can formulate two simple rules regarding moving money forwards and backwards:

Rule 1

To move money forwards (determine a future value), inflate the relevant sum by multiplying by the accumulation factor \((1+i)^\PalAb n\).

Rule 2

To move money backwards (determine a present value), deflate the relevant sum by dividing by the accumulation factor \((1+i)^\PalAb n\).  That is equivalent to multiplying by a
discount factor of \((1+i)^{-\PalAb n}\).

The point is that the mathematics of finance deals with dated values of money.  This fact is fundamental to any financial transaction involving money due on different dates.  In principle every sum of money specified should have an attached date.  Fortunately in practice it is often clear from the context what the implied date is.
