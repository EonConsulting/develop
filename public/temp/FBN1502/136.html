What is a formula?

What exactly is a formula?

A formula is a special type of equation that shows the relationship
between different variables.  It is in fact a recipe for calculating the
value of some desired variable, called the dependent variable, from
the values of the relevant independent variables.

Example using chart

Suppose Mary has a house cleaning business, getting paid \(\Cur285\Cdp00\) per house in her neighbourhood.  She wants to calculate the amount of money she can make cleaning \(3\) houses as opposed to \(5\) houses. She might start by creating a chart: 

Number of houses Amount of money earned
  \(1\)     \(\Cur285\Cdp00\)  
  \(2\)     \(\Cur570\Cdp00\)  
  \(3\)     \(\Cur855\Cdp00\)  
  \(4\)     \(\Cur1\Tsp140\Cdp00\)  
  \(5\)     \(\Cur1\Tsp425\Cdp00\)  

So, she can earn a total amount of \(\Cur855\Cdp00\) cleaning \(3\) houses and \(\Cur1\Tsp425\Cdp00\) cleaning \(5\) houses in her neighbourhood.

Example using formula

Since Mary multiplies the number of houses by \(\Cur285\Cdp00\) each time, she can write a formula in words to make this process of calculating her income easier:

\(\PalAa{\text{income}} = \Cur285\Cdp00 \:\text{times} \PalAb{\:\text{the number of houses cleaned}}\)

She can rewrite this formula, using variables instead of words:

  Let the variable \(\PalAb h\) represent the number of houses cleaned.  
  Let the variable \(\PalAa i\) represent income earned.  

Her income is therefore calculated using the following formula:

\(\PalAa i=\Cur285\Cdp00\times \PalAb h\)

Using this formula, she can substitute any number for \(\PalAb h\) (the number of houses cleaned) and determine the amount of her earnings.  In this way, she can always change \(\PalAb h\) and substitute the number of houses that she cleaned that week and easily determine her earnings.  

So, to calculate the amount of money she can make cleaning \(\PalAa3\) houses as opposed to \(\PalAb5\) houses, use the formula in the following way:

Number of houses cleaned \(\PalAb h=\PalAb 3\) \(\PalAb h=\PalAb5\)
Income formula \(\PalAa i\) \(=\) \(\Cur285\Cdp00\times \PalAb h\) \(\PalAa i\) \(=\) \(\Cur285\Cdp00\times \PalAb h\)
Substitute \(h\)   \(=\) \(\Cur285\Cdp00\times \PalAb3\)   \(=\) \(\Cur285\Cdp00\times \PalAb5\)
Income earned   \(=\) \(\Cur855\Cdp00\)   \(=\) \(\Cur1\Tsp425\Cdp00\)

So, using the formula we get the same result as using the chart, namely she can earn a total amount of \(\Cur855\Cdp00\) cleaning \(\PalAb3\) houses and \(\Cur1\Tsp425\Cdp00\) cleaning \(\PalAb5\) houses in her neighbourhood.

Notice how the variables \(\PalAa i\) and \(\PalAb h\) change based on the number of houses cleaned.  Using a formula is an easy way to calculate her earnings without the added work of creating a chart.
