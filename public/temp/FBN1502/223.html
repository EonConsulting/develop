Shape of the graph of a quadratic function

The graph of a quadratic function is called a parabola.

A parabola contains a point called a vertex. This is the point at which the curve turns, and is thus also known as the turning point.

A parabola can:

open upward (smiling)

or

open downward (sad).

The two forms of a parabola

Compare the two forms of a parabola illustrated below and note the similarities and differences in characteristics:

Parabola opens upward
\(y=\PalAa2x^2-2\)

\(\PalAa{a>0}\)

The vertex is the lowest point.

At vertex the \(y\)-coordinate is the minimum value.

It is the point at which the curve turns.

The function has a minimum value at this point

Parabola opens downward
\(y=\PalAb{-2}x^2+2\)

\(\PalAb{a<0}\)

The vertex is the highest point.

At vertex the \(y\)-coordinate is the maximum value.

It is the point at which the curve turns.

The function has a maximum value at this point.

Identifying the form of a parabola without graphing

How do we know which one of the two forms we are dealing with without actually drawing the curve?

The answer lies in the value of \(a\), the coefficient of \(x^2\) (the number in front of the \(x^2\)), as we can see illustrated below:

 

\(\PalAa{a>0}\)

  

\(\PalAb{a<0}\)
 

For \(y=\PalAa2x^2+0x-2\)
which is actually \(y=\PalAa2x^2-2\),
\(\PalAa{a=2}\), \(b=0\) and \(c=-2\).

  

For\(y=\PalAb{-2}x^2+0x+2\)
which is actually \(y=\PalAb{-2}x^2+2\),
\(\PalAb{a=-2}\), \(b=0\) and \(c=2\).
 

So, the term \(\PalAa a\) is positive.

  

So, the term \(\PalAb a\) is negative.

So, if \(\PalAa a\) is positive \((\PalAa{a>0})\), then the parabola opens upward, and if \(\PalAb a\) is negative \((\PalAb{a<0})\), then the parabola opens downward.

Symmetry of a parabola

A parabola is symmetric with respect to a vertical line called the axis of symmetry.  The vertex of the parabola will lie on the axis of symmetry.  In the two graphs above, the axis of symmetry is the \(y\)-axis.
