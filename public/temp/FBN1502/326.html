Calculate \(P\): Present Value

It is possible to obtain the present value \(P\) or \(PV\) from the basic formula for the accumulated sum or future value \(S\) in the following way:

Basic formula for the accumulated sum

\(S\)

\(=\)

\(P(1+in)\)

Divide by the factor \((1+in)\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{S}{(1+in)}\)

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{P(1+in)}{(1+in)}\)

 

\(\displaystyle\frac{S}{(1+in)}\)

\(=\)

\(P\)
 

\(P\)

\(=\)

\(S\div(1+in)\)

How do we interpret this result?

\(P\) is the amount which must be borrowed now to accrue to the sum \(S\) after a term \(n\) at interest rate \(i\) per time unit.  As such it is known as the present value of the sum \(S\).

Stated formally, the present value of a debt \(S\) on a date prior to the due date is the value \(P\) or \(PV\) of the debt on the date in question, and is given by the formula

\(P=S\div(1+in)\)

where \(i\) is the rate of interest and \(n\) "the time to run to maturity".
