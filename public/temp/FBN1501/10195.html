Fractions to decimals

Method

Follow these steps to convert a fraction to a decimal:

Step 1

Find a number you can multiply by the denominator (bottom) of the fraction to make it \(10\), \(100\), \(1\Tsp000\), or any \(1\) followed by zeros.

Step 2

Multiply both the numerator and the denominator (top and bottom) by that number.

Step 3

Then write down just the numerator (top number), putting the decimal comma in the correct place, that is one space from the right-hand side for every zero in the denominator (bottom number).

Example

Express \(\displaystyle\frac{3}{16}\) as a decimal:

Step 1

We have to multiply the denominator \(16\) by \(\PalAa{625}\) for it to become \(\PalBc{10\Tsp000}\).  

  

\(16\PalAa{\times625}=\PalBc{10\Tsp000}\)

Step 2

Multiply both the numerator and the denominator (top and bottom) by \(\PalAa{625}\).

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{3\PalAa{\times626}}{16\PalAa{\times625}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{1\Tsp875}{\PalBc{10\Tsp000}}\)

Step 3

Write down the numerator (top number) \(1\Tsp875\) with the decimal comma four spaces from the right, because \(\PalBc{10\Tsp000}\) has four zeros.

  

\(=0\PalBc\Ndp1875\)

So, the fraction \(\displaystyle\frac{3}{16}\) expressed as a decimal is \(0\Ndp1875\).

Calculator method

The example above was given to explain to you the mathematics behind the scenes.  The good news is that there is an easier way to express a fraction as a decimal number: just use your calculator.

Using your calculator, key in

\(3\div16=\)

and \(0\Ndp1875\) will be displayed.
