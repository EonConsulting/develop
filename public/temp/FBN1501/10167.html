Definitions

Associative law of addition

The sum of three numbers does not depend on
which two are added first, the result is unique.

For variables \(\PalAa A\), \(\PalAb B\) and \(\PalBc C\) this law can be written as:

\(\begin{align} (\PalAa A+\PalAb B)+\PalBc C&=\PalAa A+(\PalAb B+\PalBc C) \end{align} \)

\(\begin{align} (\PalAa 5+\PalAb 2)+\PalBc 3&=\PalAa 5+(\PalAb 2+\PalBc 3) \end{align} \)

Associative law of multiplication

The product of three numbers does not depend on
which two are multiplied first, the result is unique.

For variables \(\PalAa A\), \(\PalAb B\) and \(\PalBc C\) this law can be written as:

\(\begin{align} (\PalAa A\times\PalAb B)\times\PalBc C&=\PalAa A\times(\PalAb B\times\PalBc C) \end{align} \)

\(\begin{align}(\PalAa 3\times\PalAb 5)\times\PalBc 2&=\PalAa 3\times(\PalAb 5\times\PalBc 2) \end{align} \)
