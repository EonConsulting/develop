Summation

The summation symbol is an important symbol when working with numbers. It is a very useful sign, because it is the mathematical shorthand sign for adding.

The Greek capital letter for S is \(\sum\) and is pronounced as "sigma".

Sign for sum

The summation symbol is the mathematical shorthand sign for adding.

  

\(\displaystyle\sum\)

What must we add?

Whatever appears after the sigma is added.  Here we will sum \(\PalBc n\).

  

\(\displaystyle\sum \PalBc n\)

What is the value of \(\PalBc n\)?

The values are shown below and above the sigma.  In this case \(\PalBc n\) starts at \(\PalAa1\) and ends at \(\PalAb4\), so that is \(\PalAa1\), \(2\), \(3\) and \(\PalAb4\).

\(\)

\(\)\(\displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc n=\PalAa1}^{\PalAb 4}{\PalBc n}\)

Have a look

So now we add up \(\PalAa1\), \(2\), \(3\) and \(\PalAb4\).

\(\begin{align} &\displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc n=\PalAa1}^{\PalAb 4}{\PalBc n}\\ =\:&\PalAa1+2+3+\PalAb4\\ =\:&10 \end{align}\)

Instead of adding numbers, we can also add variables.

Example

Suppose we have the following variables:

\(x_1=4\), \(x_2=9\), \(x_3=2\), \(x_4=5\) and \(x_5=8\)

Calculating the value of \(\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^5{x_i}\) gives:

\(\begin{align} \displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa1}^\PalAb5{x_\PalBc i}&=x_\PalAa1+x_2+x_3+x_4+x_\PalAb5\\ &=4+9+2+5+8\\ &=28 \end{align}\)

For \(\displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa2}^\PalAb4{x_\PalBc i}\) you will calculate \(x_\PalAa2+x_3+x_\PalAb4\).  Substitute the value of \(\PalBc i\) in \(x_\PalBc i\) for the specified range.  The variable \(\PalBc i\) starts at \(\PalAa2\) and ends at \(\PalAb4\):

\(\begin{align} \displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa2}^\PalAb4{x_\PalBc i} &=x_\PalAa2+x_3+x_\PalAb4\\ &= 9+2+5\\ &=16 \end{align}\)

 

For \(\displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa1}^\PalAb2{x_\PalBc i}\) you will calculate \(x_\PalAa1+x_\PalAb2\).  Substitute the value of \(\PalBc i\) in \(x_\PalBc i\) for the specified range.  The variable \(\PalBc i\)starts at \(\PalAa1\) and ends at \(\PalAb2\):

\(\begin{align} \displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa1}^\PalAb2{x_\PalBc i}&=x_\PalAa1+x_\PalAb2\\ &=4+9\\ &=13 \end{align}\)

 

For \(\displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa1}^\PalAb3{x_\PalBc i}^2\) you will calculate \(x_\PalAa1^2+x_2^2+x_\PalAb3^2\).  Substitute the value of \(\PalBc i\) in \(x_\PalBc i^2\) for the specified range.  The variable \(\PalBc i\) starts at \(\PalAa1\) and ends at \(\PalAb3\):

\(\begin{align} \displaystyle\sum_{\PalBc i=\PalAa1}^\PalAb3{x_\PalBc i}^2&=x_\PalAa1^2+x_2^2+x_\PalAb3^2\\ &=4^2+9^2+2^2\\ &= 16+81+4\\ &=101\end{align}\)
