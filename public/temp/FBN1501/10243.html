Fractional exponents 2

A fractional exponent with one in the numerator is a root of some sort, for example a number to the power of a third is the cube root.

But what would an exponent of \(\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\) mean?

The number \(y^{\frac{\PalAa 2}{\PalAb3}}\)can also be written as \(\sqrt[\PalAb3]{y^\PalAa2}\).

What would an exponent of \(\displaystyle\frac{5}{2}\) mean?

The number \(x^{\frac{\PalAa 5}{\PalAb2}}\) can also be written as \(\sqrt{x^\PalAb 5}\).

In a fractional exponent, the numerator is the power to which the number should be taken and the denominator is the root that should be taken.

Example

Calculate \(64^{\frac{2}{3}}\).

\(64^{\frac{\PalAa2}{\PalAb3}}\) means you need to square \(64\) and take the cube root of the result.
This works out to \(16\).
