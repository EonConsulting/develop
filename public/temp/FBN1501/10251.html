Example

Statements of proportionality are used all the time to scale up, or down, the values for a known or accepted situation to new values or new situations.

Property tax is often assessed on a proportional basis.  Suppose a certain municipality charges \(\PalAa{\Cur350\Cdp00}\) per year for every \(\PalAb{\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) assessed valuation.  What would the yearly tax be on a property valued at \(\PalAb{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)?

Solution using the comparison to one ratio

The ratio of tax to value is

\(\PalAa{\text{tax}}\)

\(:\)

\(\PalAb{\text{value}}\)
 

\(\PalAa{\Cur350\Cdp00}\)

\(:\)

\(\PalAb{\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

 

\(\PalAa{350}\)

\(:\)

\(\PalAb{10\Tsp000}\)

Rewrite it as a ratio to one
by dividing both sides by \(10\Tsp000\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{350}}{10\Tsp000}\)

\(:\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb{10\Tsp000}}{10\Tsp000}\)

So, the comparison to one ratio is

\(\PalAa{0\Ndp035}\)

\(:\)

\(\PalAb1\)

Calculate the tax on the
property valued at \(\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00\)
by multiplying both sides of
the ratio to one with \(950\Tsp000\)

\(\PalAa{0\Ndp035}\times950\Tsp000\)

\(:\)

\(\PalAb{1}\times950\Tsp000\)

Which gives the ratio of tax to value as

\(\PalAa{33\Tsp250}\)

\(:\)

\(\PalAb{950\Tsp000}\)

Check that the result is true

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{33\Tsp250}}{\PalAb{950\Tsp000}}\)

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{350}}{\PalAb{10\Tsp000}}\)

So, the annual tax on this property valued at \(\PalAb{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) is \(\PalAa{\Cur33\Tsp250\Cdp00}\).

Rate as the comparison to one ratio

So, in this method the basic trick is to determine the comparison to one ratio.  This gives us the rate.

But how do you know to which number you must compare?  In other words, which number comes second in the ratio?  The answer is simple: write down the information that is given.  In this case:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{\text{tax}}&:\PalAb{\text{value}}\\ \PalAa{350}&:\PalAb{10\Tsp000}\\ \PalAa{\text{?}}&:\PalAb{950\Tsp000} \end{align}\)

The second number in the ratio is always the one with two given values.
In this case it is the valuation (with \(\PalAb{\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) and \(\PalAb{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)).

Remember to always check your answer as shown above in the example.
