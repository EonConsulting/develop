The equation of a quadratic function

Parabolas are described by quadratic equations.

A quadratic equation is one in which the highest power of the independent variable \(x\) is a square.  The general equation of a quadratic function is where the relationship between the dependent variable, \(\PalAa y\), and the independent variable, \(\PalAb x\), has the form:

\(\PalAa y=f(\PalAb x)=a\PalAb x^2+b\PalAb x+c\)

where   \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) are constants
and   \(a\neq0\).

The three values \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) are also called coefficients.

A coefficient is a number used to multiply with a variable.  For example, \(3x\) means '\(3\) times \(x\)', so \(3\) is a coefficient.  In the same way, \(ax^2\) means
'\(a\) times \(x^2\)', so \(a\) is a coefficient.

The coefficient \(c\) in the quadratic equation is also called the constant.

The term \(a\neq0\) means \(a\) may not be zero and is read as '\(a\) not equal to \(0\)'.  If \(a=0\), the expression becomes:

Quadratic equation \(y\) \(=\) \(\PalBa ax^2+bx+c\)
If \(\PalBa a=\PalBa 0\) \(y\) \(=\) \((\PalBa 0)x^2+bx+c\)
    \(=\) \(\PalBa 0\times x^2+bx+c\)
    \(=\) \(\PalBa 0 x^2+bx+c\)
    \(=\) \(bx+c\)
Straight line equation \(y\) \(=\) \(bx+c\)

So, the term \(a\neq0\), because if \(a=0\), the quadratic equation becomes the equation for a straight line.
