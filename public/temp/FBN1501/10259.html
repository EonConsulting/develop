\(\%\) Change

Percentage change is calculated as follows:

\(\%\text{ change}=\displaystyle\frac{\text{change}}{\text{original}}\times 100\)

The change can be an increase or a decrease.

Example

Mandile buys a handbag on a sale.  It is marked down from \(\Cur780\Cdp00\) to \(\Cur655\Cdp20\).
Calculate the percentage decrease in price on the handbag.

The original price was \(\PalAb{\Cur780\Cdp00}\) and the new price is \(\PalBc{\Cur655\Cdp20}\).  The decrease in price is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{\text{decrease}}&=\PalAb{\text{original price}}-\PalBc{\text{new price}}\\ &=\PalAb{\Cur780\Cdp00}-\PalBc{\Cur655\Cdp20}\\ &=\PalAa{\Cur124\Cdp80} \end{align}\)

The decrease in price is \(\PalAa{\Cur124\Cdp80}\).

The percentage decrease in price is calculated as:

\(\begin{align}\PalAa{\%\text{ decrease}}&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{\text{decrease}}}{\PalAb{\text{original}}}\times 100\\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{\Cur124\Cdp80}}{\PalAb{\Cur780\Cdp00}}\times 100\\ &=\PalAa{16\%} \end{align}\)

The handbag was marked down \(16\%\).
