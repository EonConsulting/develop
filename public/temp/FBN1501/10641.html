The prices and quantities sold at William's Wardrobe for some items of clothing for May 2007 and May 2013 are:

Item \(\textrm{Prices in 2007:}\\~~~p_{\,2007}~\textrm{(R)}\\~~\textrm{(base year)}\) \(\textrm{Quantities sold in 2007:}\\~~~~~~~~~~q_{\,2007}\\~~~~~\textrm{(base year)}\) \(\textrm{Prices in 2013:}\\~~~p_{\,2013}~\textrm{(R)}\\~~\textrm{(given year)}\) \(\textrm{Quantities sold in 2013:}\\~~~~~~~~~~q_{\,2013}\\~~~~~\textrm{(given year)}\)
Ties (each) \(~\,100\) \(1\,000\) \(~\,200\) \(900\)
Suits (each) \(1\,200\) \(~\,100\) \(1\,600\) \(120\)
Shirts (each) \(~\,250\) \(~\,500\) \(~\,220\) \(500\)

Complete the following table:

Item \(p_{\,2007}\) \(q_{\,2007}\) \(p_{\,2013}\) \(q_{\,2013}\) \(p_{\,2007} \times q_{\,2007}\) \(p_{\,2013} \times q_{\,2013}\)
Ties (each) \(~\,100\) \(1\,000\) \(~\,200\) \(900\)
Suits (each) \(1\,200\) \(~\,100\) \(1\,600\) \(120\)
Shirts (each) \(~\,250\) \(~\,500\) \(~\,220\) \(500\)
Total        

 

Item \(p_{\,2007}\) \(q_{\,2007}\) \(p_{\,2013}\) \(q_{\,2013}\) \(p_{\,2007} \times q_{\,2007}\) \(p_{\,2013} \times q_{\,2013}\)
Ties (each) \(~\,100\) \(1\,000\) \(~\,200\) \(900\) \(100\,000\) \(180\,000\)
Suits (each) \(1\,200\) \(~\,100\) \(1\,600\) \(120\) \(120\,000\) \(192\,000\)
Shirts (each) \(~\,250\) \(~\,500\) \(~\,220\) \(500\) \(125\,000\) \(110\,000\)
Total         \(345\,000\) \(482\,000\)

 

The value index is

\( V=~\) \(\sum{p_nq_n}\) \( ~\times 100\)
\(\sum{p_0q_0}\)
\( =~\) \(\sum{p_{2013}q_{2013}}\) \( ~\times 100\)
\(\sum{p_{2007}q_{2007}}\)
\( =~\) \( ~\times 100\)
\( =~\)

 

The correct answer is

\(\begin{eqnarray*} V &=& \displaystyle\frac{\sum{p_nq_n}}{\sum{p_0q_0}} \times 100 \\[2mm] &=& \displaystyle\frac{\sum{p_{2013}q_{2013}}}{\sum{p_{2007}q_{2007}}} \times 100 \\[2mm] &=& \displaystyle\frac{482\,000}{345\,000} \times 100 \\[2mm] &=& 139,71. \end{eqnarray*}\)
