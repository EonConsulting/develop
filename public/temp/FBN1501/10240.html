Cube root (Normal)

Let us look at roots with higher indexes when the numbers do not work out perfectly.

The cube root

= =
   

Make three similar groups and a remainder:

  

Take one of the similar groups out of the root.  The remainder is left under the root:

\(\sqrt[3]{x^{10}y^7}\)

\(=\)

\(\sqrt[3]{\PalAa {x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa {x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa {x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\times xy}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa {x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\sqrt[3]{ xy}\)

  

We can also use the following different method to determine the cube root.

Consider \(x^{10}\).  Ten is not a multiple of three, therefore we will factor it in two powers of \(x\).  The largest factor which is a multiple of three, is nine.
The two factors therefore are \(x^9\) and \(x^1\).

Also consider \(y^7\).  Seven is not a multiple of three, therefore we will factor it in two powers of \(y\).  The largest factor which is a multiple of three, is six.
The two factors therefore are \(y^6\) and \(y^1\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{10}}\PalAb{y^7}}=\sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{9+1}}\PalAb{y^{6+1}}} = \sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{9}x^1}\PalAb{y^6y^1}}\)

The cube root of \(x^9\) is \(x^{9\div3}\) and that is equal to \(x^3\).  And the cube root of \(y^6\) is \(y^{6\div3}\) and that is equal to \(y^2\).

This gives:

\( \sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{9}x^1}\PalAb{y^6y^1}}=\PalAa {x^3}\PalAb{y^2} \sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x}\PalAb{y}} \)

So, the complete calculation can be written as:

\(\sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{10}}\PalAb{y^7}}=\sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{9+1}}\PalAb{y^{6+1}}} = \sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^{9}x^1}\PalAb{y^6y^1}}=\PalAa {x^3}\PalAb{y^2} \sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x}\PalAb{y}} \)
