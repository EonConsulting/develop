The \(x\)-intercept of a straight line

The point where the line cuts the \(\PalAb x\)-axis is called the \(\PalAb x\)-intercept or root.

At this point, where \(\PalAa {y = 0}\), the value of \(\PalAb x\) can be determined by following the steps given below:

General case

Straight line equation

\(\PalAa y=a\PalAb x+b\)

Substitute \(\PalAa{y=0}\) 

\(\PalAa0=a\PalAb x+b\)

Subtract \(b\) both sides 

\(\begin{align} \PalAa0-b & = a\PalAb x +b -b \\ -b & = a\PalAb x \end{align} \)

Assuming \(a\neq0\), divide by \(a\) both sides

\(\begin{align} -\displaystyle\frac{b}{a}&=\displaystyle\frac{a\PalAb x}{a}\\ -\displaystyle\frac{b}{a}&=\PalAb x\\ \PalAb x&=-\displaystyle\frac{b}{a} \end{align} \)

Conclusion

Intercept on \(\PalAb x\)-axis is equal to \(\PalAb{-\displaystyle\frac{b}{a}}\) in equation for straight line.
The \(\PalAb x\)-intercept  is the point \(\PalAb{(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{a};0)}\).

Example case

General case

\(\PalAa y=a\PalAb x+b\)

Values of variables

\(a=-2\) and \(b=4\)

Substitute for equation

\(\PalAa y=-2\PalAb x+4\)

Substitute \(\PalAa{y = 0}\) 

\(\PalAa0=-2\PalAb x+4\)

Subtract \(-4\) both sides

 \(\begin{align} \PalAa0-4&=-2\PalAb x+4-4 \\-4&=-2\PalAb x \end{align}\)

Divide by \(-2\) both sides

\(\begin{align} \displaystyle\frac{-4}{-2} & = \displaystyle\frac{-2\PalAb x}{-2} \\ 2 & = \PalAb x \\ \PalAb x & =2 \end{align} \)

Conclusion

The intercept on the -axis is equal to \(\PalAb2\).
The \(\PalAb x\)-intercept  is the point \(\PalAb{\left(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{a};0\right)}=\PalAb{(\displaystyle\frac{-4}{-2};0)}=\PalAb{(2;0)}\).\(\)

The straight line \(y = ax + b\), cuts the \(\PalAb x\)-axis at the point \(\PalAb{(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{a};0)}\).  
Therefore, the line \(y = -2x+4 \), cuts the \(\PalAb x\)-axis at \(\PalAb{x = -\displaystyle\frac{b}{a} = \displaystyle\frac{-4}{-2}=2}\).
