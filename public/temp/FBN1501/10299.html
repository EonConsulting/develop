Number line

You can visualise positive and negative numbers using the number line:

It is important to understand the number line because it shows you that every number has an opposite.

Why do we need negative numbers?  As it turns out, there are many everyday problems where negative numbers are useful, for example:

  • We can both gain and lose weight.
  • Temperature can rise or fall.
  • Locations on the earth can be above or below sea level.

A number that is greater than \(0\) is called positive.  If it is less than \(0\) it is called negative.  Zero is neither positive nor negative.  Two integers that are the same distance from the origin in opposite directions are called opposites, for example, negative \(\PalAa5\) is the opposite of positive \(\PalAb5\).

The arrows on each end of the number line show us that the line stretches to infinity in both the negative and positive direction.  We do not have to include a positive sign \((\PalAb+)\) when we write positive numbers. However, we do have to include the negative sign \((\PalAa-)\) when we write negative numbers.
Zero is called the origin and is neither negative nor positive.
