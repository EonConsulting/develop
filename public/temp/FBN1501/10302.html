Summary

Mean

The mean is a good summary for values that represent magnitudes, such as test marks and the cost of something.

  • Mean for raw data

\(\overline{x}=\displaystyle\frac{\sum_{i=1}^\PalAa nx_i}{\PalAa n}\) where \(\PalAa n\) is the number of observations in the data set.
  • Mean for data in frequency distribution

\(\overline{x}=\displaystyle\frac{\sum_{i=1}^\PalAb k f_ix_i}{\PalAa n}\) where \(\PalAa n=\sum_{i=1}^\PalAb kf_i\) and \(\PalAb k\) is the number of intervals.

Median

The median is best used when ranking people or things, like heights or when extreme values might affect the mean.

The position of the median \((Me)\) is the \(\left(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa n+1}{2}\right)^\text{th}\) value in a
data set of size \(\PalAa n\) sorted in ascending order.

Mode

The mode is best used when finding out the most popular dress size or the most popular brand of chocolate.

The mode of a data set is that value which occurs most often.

Distribution

  • Normal distribution

Symmetrical with a single central peak at the mean (average) of the data.

The mean, median and the mode are the same.

  • Positively skewed distribution

mode \(<\) median \(<\) mean

  • Negatively skewed distribution

mean < median < mode
