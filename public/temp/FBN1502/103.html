Calculate \(S\) and \(R\)

Four variables are needed when determining the future value of an annuity, namely \(S\), \(R\), \(i\) and \(n\).  If you know any three of these variables, the fourth can be determined.

The following two types of calculations are relevant:

  • Calculate \(S\) when \(R\), \(i\) and \(n\) are known
  • Calculate \(R\) when \(S\), \(i\) and \(n\) are known

 

Calculate \(S\) when \(R\), \(i\) and \(n\) are known

Use the formula for the future value of an annuity:

\(S=R\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right]\)

where \(S\) is the future value of the annuity
  \(R\) is the regular payment
  \(i\) is the interest rate per payment interval
  \(n\) is the number of payment intervals

 

Calculate \(R\) when \(S\), \(i\) and \(n\) are known

Manipulate the formula for the future value of an annuity to solve for \(R\):

Future value of annuity

 \(S\) \(=\) \(R\times\left[[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right]\)

Multiply both sides

 \(S \times\left[\displaystyle\frac{i}{(1+i)^n-1}\right] \) \(=\) \(R\times\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}\right] \times\left[\displaystyle\frac{i}{(1+i)^n-1}\right] \)

\(R\) alone on right

 \(S \times\left[\displaystyle\frac{i}{(1+i)^n-1}\right]\) \(=\) \(R\)

\(R\) on left

 \(R\) \(=\) \(S \times\left[\displaystyle\frac{i}{(1+i)^n-1}\right]\)

Simplify

 \(R\)\(\) \(=\) \(S \left[\displaystyle\frac{i}{(1+i)^n-1}\right]\)
