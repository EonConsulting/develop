Formulas

Let us look at a summary of the formulas.

Permutations

If you have \(\PalBc n\) objects and you want to arrange \(\PalAb r\) objects from the \(\PalBc n\) objects, then you use permutations:

Permutations - order is important

\(\PalBc{_n}P\PalAb{_r}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc n!}{(\PalBc n-\PalAb r)!}\)    and    \(\PalBc{_n}P\PalBc{_n}=\PalBc n!\)
 

\(\PalBc{_n}P\PalAb{_r}\) can also be written as \(\PalBc{^n}P\PalAb{_r}\) or \(P(\PalBc n,\PalAb r)\).

\(\PalBc{_n}P\PalAb{_r}\) is the number of permutations of \(\PalBc n\) objects taken \(\PalAb r\) at a time.

Combinations

If you have \(\PalBc n\) objects and you want to select \(\PalAb r\) objects from the \(\PalBc n\) objects, then you use combinations:

Combinations - order is not important

\(\PalBc{_n}C\PalAb{_r}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{_n}P\PalAb{_r}}{\PalAb r!}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc n!}{(\PalBc n-\PalAb r)!\PalAb r!}\)
 

\(\PalBc{_n}C\PalAb{_r}\) can also be written as \(\PalBc{^n}C\PalAb{_r}\) or \(C(\PalBc n,\PalAb r)\) or \(\left( \begin{array}{c} \PalBc n\\ \PalAb r \end{array} \right)\).

\(\PalBc{_n}C\PalAb{_r}\) is the number of combinations of \(\PalBc n\) objects taken \(\PalAb r\) at a time and is sometimes read as "\(\PalBc n\) choose \(\PalAb r\)".
