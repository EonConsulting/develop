\(0\) and \(1\) exponents

\(0\) exponent property

Any power having a non-zero base and an exponent of zero, is equal to one.

\(\begin{align}a^\PalAa0=\PalAa1,&&a\ne0\end{align}\)

Example

Simplify the following expression:

\(\left[\left(3x^4y^7z^{12} \right)^5\left(-5x^9y^3z^4 \right)^2 \right]^\PalAa0\)

We do not have to worry about the complicated expression inside the square brackets, because the zero exponent on the outside means that the value of the entire expression is one\(\).

So, this expression simplified is:

\(\left[\left(3x^4y^7z^{12} \right)^5\left(-5x^9y^3z^4 \right)^2 \right]^\PalAa0=\PalAa1\)

\(1\) exponent property

Any power with exponent of one, is equal to the base.

\(a^\PalAb1=\PalAb a\)

Example

Simplify the following expression:

\(\left[\left(3x^4y^7z^{12} \right)^5\left(-5x^9y^3z^4 \right)^2 \right]^\PalAb1\)

We do not have to worry too much about the complicated expression inside the square brackets, because the one exponent on the outside means that the value of the entire expression is the same as the base.

So, this expression simplified is:

\(\left[\left(3x^4y^7z^{12} \right)^5\left(-5x^9y^3z^4 \right)^2 \right]^\PalAb1=\PalAb{\left(3x^4y^7z^{12} \right)^5\left(-5x^9y^3z^4 \right)^2 }\)
