Example - More than one

A data set may have more than one mode if the most frequently occurring scores occur the same number of times.

A survey of \(14\) employees' performance scores out of \(50\) had these results, ordered from lowest to highest:

There are two modes, namely \(\PalAb{33}\) and \(\PalAb{39}\).  Such distributions are called bimodal.
