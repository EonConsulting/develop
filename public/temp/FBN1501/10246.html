Introduction

A ratio compares values.

A ratio says how much of one thing there is compared to another thing.

\(\PalAa4:\PalAb1\)

\(\PalAa{4\text{ squares}}:\PalAb{1\text{ square}}\)

In this case, \(4:1\) means there are four blue squares to one orange square.

Writing ratios

A ratio can be written in different ways.  Note the following three ways:

  • Use the colon \(:\) to separate the values

 

\(4:1\)
  • Use the word \(\text{to}\) instead of the colon \(:\)

 

\(4\text{ to }1\)
  • Write the ratio as a fraction

 

\(\displaystyle\frac{4}{1}\)

Scaling ratios

A ratio can be scaled up.  See below how \(4:1\) is scaled up.

\(\PalAa4:\PalAb1\)

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{16\text{ squares}}&:\PalAb{4\text{ squares}}\\ \PalAa{4\text{ big squares}}&:\PalAb{1\text{ big square}} \end{align}\)

Here the ratio is still four blue squares to one orange square, even though there are more squares.  There are \(16\) small blue squares to \(4\) small orange squares, which is the same ratio as \(4\) big orange squares to \(1\) big blue square.
