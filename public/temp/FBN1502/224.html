Vertex and the \(y\)-coordinate

The \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex tells us
how high or low the parabola sits.

To find the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex, we substitute the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex into the original general equation for the quadratic function, as illustrated below:

Quadratic function equation

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(f(x)=ax^2+bx+c\)

 \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex

\(x_m\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\)

\(y\)-coordinate of the vertex

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(f\left(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\right)\)
   

\(=\)

\(a\left(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2+b\left(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\right)+c\)
   

\(=\)

\(a\times\left(\displaystyle\frac{-b}{2a}\right)^2+b\times\left(\displaystyle\frac{-b}{2a}\right)+c\)
   

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-b^2+4ac}{4a}\)

So, the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex is calculated as \(y=f\left(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\right)=\displaystyle\frac{-b^2+4ac}{4a}\).

Example

For the quadratic function or parabola

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(f(\PalAb x)=\PalAb x^2+2\PalAb x-3\)

the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is

\(\PalAb{x_m}\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}=-\displaystyle\frac{2}{2\times1}=\PalAb{-1}\).

Substitute the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex 

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb{-1}\)

into the quadratic function

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(f(\PalAb x)\)

to calculate the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex:

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(f(\PalAb{-1})\)
   

\(=\)

\((\PalAb{-1})^2+2(\PalAb{-1})-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(-1\times-1+2\times-1-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(1-2-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(\PalAa{-4}\)

So, the vertex is the point

    

\((\PalAb{-1};\PalAa{-4})\)

 

The vertex is the point \(\left( -\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a} ; f\left( -\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a} \right) \right)\).
