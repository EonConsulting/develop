Simple Interest Formula

When and how interest is calculated gives way to different types of interest.
Simple interest is interest which is computed on the principal for the entire term for which it is borrowed or saved.  It is therefore due at the end of the term.  

It is given as:

\(I=Pin\)

There are four variables needed when determining simple interest.

 If you know any three of these, the fourth can be determined.

where

\(I\)

is the simple interest (in rand) paid for the use of the money at the end of the term;
 

\(P\)

is the principal, original or total amount borrowed (in rand) which is subject to interest.  This is also known as the present value \((PV)\) of the loan;
 

\(i\)

is the interest rate \((\%)\), divided by \(100\) to get a decimal fraction.  It is the fraction of the principal which must be paid at the end of each period ( for example, each year) for the use of the principal; and
 

\(n\)

is the term or time, that is the number of periods for which the principal is borrowed.
