Example

There are a few steps to follow when multiplying fractions.  Let us use the following example to illustrate these steps.

Calculate the following:

\(2\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{9}{10}\)

First method

Simplify the fractions by dividing a numerator and a denominator by the same number.  Then multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.

 

Calculate the following:

  

\(2\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{9}{10}\)

Step 1

Convert any mixed numbers to improper fractions.

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{8}{3}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{9}{10}\)

Step 2

Simplify by dividing a numerator and a denominator by the same number.
Note that \(3\PalAb{\div3}=\PalAb1\) and \(9\PalAb{\div3}=\PalAb3\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{8}{\PalAb\not3_\PalAb1}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb\not9^\PalAb3}{10}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{8}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{3}{10}\)

Step 3

Simplify again by dividing a numerator and a denominator by the same number.  Note that \(4\PalAb{\div4}=\PalAb1\) and \(8\PalAb{\div4}=\PalAb2\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb\not8^\PalAb2}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{\PalAb\not4_\PalAb1}\times\displaystyle\frac{3}{10}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{2}{1}\times\frac{1}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{3}{10}\)

Step 4

Simplify again by dividing a numerator and a denominator by the same number.  Note that \(2\PalAb{\div2}=\PalAb1\) and \(10\PalAb{\div2}=\PalAb5\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb\not2^\PalAb1}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{3}{\PalAb\not{10}_\PalAb5}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{1}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{1}\times\displaystyle\frac{3}{5}\)

Step 5

Multiply the numerators and place the answer above the line.  Then multiply the denominators and place the answer below the line.

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa1\times\PalAa1\times\PalAa3}{\PalAa1\times\PalAa1\times\PalAa5}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{3}{5}\)

Second method

Alternatively you could simply multiply the numerators together and place the answer above the line; then multiply the denominators together and place the answer below the line.  Then simplify the fraction.

 

Calculate the following:

 

\(2\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{9}{10}\)

Step 1

Convert any mixed numbers to improper fractions.

 

\(=\displaystyle\frac{8}{3}\times\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}\times\displaystyle\frac{9}{10}\)

Step 2

Multiply the numerators and place the answer above the line.  Then multiply the denominators and place the answer below the line.

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa8\times\PalAa1\times\PalAa9}{\PalAa3\times\PalAa4\times\PalAa{10}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{72}{120}\)

Step 3

Simplify the fraction.  Note that \(72\) and \(120\) are both divisible by \(\PalAb4\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{72\PalAb\div\PalAb4}{120\PalAb\div\PalAb4}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{18}{30}\)

Step 4

Simplify the fraction again.  Note that \(18\) and \(30\) are both divisible by \(\PalAb6\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{18\PalAb\div\PalAb6}{30\PalAb\div\PalAb6}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{3}{5}\)
