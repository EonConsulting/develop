Cube root (Perfect)

Let us look at roots with higher indexes.  Let us consider the case when the numbers work out perfectly.

The cube root

Make three similar groups:

   Take one of the similar groups out of the root to obtain the answer:
 

\(\sqrt[3]{x^9y^6}\)

\(=\)

\(\sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa{x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa{x^3}\PalAb{y^2}}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa{x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\)

  

We can also use the following different method to determine the cube root.

Consider \(x^9\).  Nine is a multiple of three, therefore \(x^9\) is a perfect cube.  The cube root of \(x^9\) is \(x^{9\div3}\) and that is equal to \(x^3\).

Also consider \(y^6\).  Six is a multiple of three, therefore \(y^6\) is a perfect cube.  The cube root of \(y^6\) is \(y^{6\div3}\) and that is equal to \(y^2\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[3]{\PalAa{x^9}\PalAb{y^6}}=\PalAa{x}^{\PalAa9\div3}\PalAb y^{\PalAb6\div3}=\PalAa{x^3}\PalAb{y^2}\)
