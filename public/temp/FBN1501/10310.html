Add and subtract 1

Let us meet teenager Steve.  His parents have a marks system to help them decide if he can go to a party on Friday night:

  "If you do a chore we will add \(\PalAb4\) points \((\PalAb{+4})\)."
"If you do not behave, we take away \(\PalAa4\) points \((\PalAa{-4})\)."
"If you reach \(30\) points by Friday, you can go the party."

Steve starts on Wednesday with \(20\) points.

Event Mom's
calculations		 List of Steve's points
  Steve starts on Wednesday with \(20\) points.     \(20\)     \(20\)      
  Steve's mom discovers his bicycle lying in the driveway in front of the garage.     \(20\PalAa{-4}=16\)     \(20\PalAa{-4}=16\)   (undo)  
  His little brother confesses that he used Steve's bicycle and left it there.  Mom writes (undo) on the list and subtracts the previous calculation value.     \(\begin{align} 16\PalAa-(\PalAa{-4})&=16\PalAb{+4}\\ &=20 \end{align}\)     \(20\)      

If you subtract a negative, you gain points, like adding points; 
  Mom adds \(\PalAb4\) points because Steve takes out the garbage.     \(20\PalAb{+4}=24\)     \(20\PalAb{+4}=24\)   (undo)  
  Dad says that he took out the garbage and writes (undo) on the list.     \(\begin{align} 24\PalAa-(\PalAb{+4})&=24\PalAa{-4}\\ &=20 \end{align}\)     \(20\)      

If you subtract a positive, you lose points, like subtracting points; 
  Dad sees Steve brushing the dog and writes \(\PalAb{(+4)}\) on the list.     \(\begin{align} 24\PalAb+(\PalAb{+4})&=20\PalAb{+4}\\ &=24 \end{align}\)     \(20\PalAb{+4}=24\)   \(\PalAb{(+4)}\)  

If you add a positive, you gain points, like adding points; 
  Steve throws a piece of wood at his brother.  Dad writes \(\PalAa{(-4)}\) on the list.     \(\begin{align} 24\PalAb+(\PalAa{-4})&=24\PalAa{-4}\\ &=20 \end{align}\)     \(24\PalAa{-4}=20\)   \(\PalAa{(-4)}\)  

If you add a negative, you lose points, like subtracting points; 

Do you think Steve will reach \(30\) points by Friday?
