The borrower

Interest is paid by the party who uses or borrows the money to the party who lends money.  Interest is calculated as a fraction of the amount borrowed (principal amount) in a time period.  The fraction, also known as the interest rate, is usually expressed as a percentage per year, but must be reduced to a decimal fraction for calculation purposes.

Example

Jane borrowed \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) from the bank for a period of one year at a simple interest rate of \(\PalAa{12\%}\) per year. So to borrow the \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for one year will cost:

\(\begin{align}\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\times\displaystyle\PalAa{\frac{12}{100}}&=\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\times\PalAa{0\Ndp12}\\ &=\Cur600\Cdp00 \end{align} \)

The interest is \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) and the simple interest rate is \({12\%}\).

Of course Jane will have to pay back the original \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) after one year, so this is what happens:

  Today Jane borrows \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) from the bank.
  Next year Jane pays back the bank \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) plus \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) interest.
  So, Jane borrows \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\), but has to pay back \(\Cur5\Tsp600\Cdp00\).

 In this case Jane is the borrower and the bank is the lender.
