Calculate \(i\): Recap

A client borrows money from a micro lender who offers short-term loans.
The client borrows \(\Cur2\Tsp175\Cdp00\) and has to repay \(\Cur2\Tsp349\Cdp00\) after six months.
What yearly simple interest rate does the micro lender use to calculate the interest?

We can manipulate the simple interest formula \(I=Pin\) and the accumulated sum formula \(S=P+I\).

The following is given in the question:

\(\begin{align} P&=\Cur2\Tsp175\Cdp00\\ S&=\Cur2\Tsp349\Cdp00\\ n&=6\text{ months}=0\Ndp5\text{ year} \end{align}\)

We need to calculate the interest rate \(i\).  

Calculate interest

The interest \(I\) is calculated as: 

\(\begin{align} I&=S-P\\ &=\Cur2\Tsp349\Cdp00-\Cur2\Tsp175\Cdp00\\ &=\Cur174\Cdp00 \end{align}\)

Calculate interest rate

Note that \(I=S-P\) and therefore the formula \(i=\displaystyle\frac{I}{Pn}\) can be rewritten as \(i=\displaystyle\frac{S-P}{Pn}\) in the following way:  

\(\begin{align} S&=P(1+in)\\ S&=P+Pin\\ S-P&=Pin\\ \frac{S-P}{Pn}&=\frac{Pin}{Pn}\\ \frac{S-P}{Pn}&=i\\ i&=\frac{S-P}{Pn} \end{align}\)

 

So, the interest rate \(i\) is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} i&=\frac{S-P}{Pn}\\ &=\frac{\Cur2\Tsp349\Cdp00-\Cur2\Tsp175\Cdp00}{\Cur2\Tsp175\Cdp00\times0\Ndp5}\\ &=\frac{\Cur174\Cdp00}{\Cur1\Tsp087\Cdp50}\\ &=0\Ndp16 \end{align}\)

Therefore, the interest rate is \(16\%\) per year.
