Decimals to fractions

Method

Follow these steps to convert a decimal to a fraction:

Step 1

Write down the decimal divided by one, like this: \(\displaystyle\frac{\text{decimal}}{1}\).

Step 2

Multiply both the numerator and denominator (top and bottom) by \(10\) for every number after the decimal comma.
For example, if there are two numbers after the decimal comma, use \(100\); if there are three then use \(1\Tsp000\), and so on.

Step 3

Simplify (or reduce) the fraction.

Example

Express \(0\Ndp625\) as a fraction:

Step 1

Write down the decimal divided by one.

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{0\Ndp625}{1}\)

Step 2

Multiply both the numerator and the denominator by \(1\Tsp000\).  There were three digits after the decimal comma, so that is \(\PalBc{10}\times\PalBc{10}\times\PalBc{10}=\PalBc{1\Tsp000}\).

 

 

\(\displaystyle\frac{0\Ndp625}{1}\PalBc{\times}\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{1\Tsp000}}{\PalBc{1\Tsp000}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{625}{1\Tsp000}\)

Step 3

Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and denominator by the common factor \(\PalAb{25}\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{625\PalAb{\div25}}{1\Tsp000\PalAb{\div25}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{25}{40}\)
 

Simplify the fraction again by dividing both the numerator and denominator by the common factor \(\PalAb5\).

  

\(=\displaystyle\frac{25\PalAb{\div5}}{40\PalAb{\div5}}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{5}{8}\)

So, the decimal \(0\Ndp625\) expressed as a fraction is \(\displaystyle\frac{5}{8}\).
