Example

Apart from systems of two inequalities in two unknowns, it is quite feasible and often necessary in practice, to consider systems with a greater number of inequalities than the number of unknowns.  This is illustrated below, where we consider a system of five inequalities in two unknowns.

The procedure for solving the system is exactly as before, namely solve each inequality separately and then determine which region is common to all solutions.

Solve the following system of inequalities graphically:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{-x+y-3} & \PalAa \leq \PalAa 0\\ \PalAb{x+y-5} & \PalAb \leq \PalAb0 \\ \PalBc{x-3} & \PalBc \leq \PalBc 0 \\ x &\geq0\\ y&\geq0 \end{align}\)

We look at the last two of these inequalities first.  The inequality \(x\geq0\) simply means the region to the right of and including the \(y\)-axis whereas \(y\geq0\) means the region above and including the \(x\)-axis.  Taken together, these two inequalities imply the first quadrant of the Cartesian plane so we can restrict our considerations to this region.

The first inequality is satisfied by all points on, below and to the right of the line

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{-x+y-3} & \PalAa = \PalAa0& \text{or}\\ \PalAa{y} & \PalAa = \PalAa{x+3} \end{align} \)

That is the blue shaded region.

The second inequality is satisfied by all points on, below and to the left of the line

\(\begin{align} \PalAb{x+y-5} & \PalAb = \PalAb0& \text{or}\\ \PalAb{y} & \PalAb = \PalAb{-x+5} \end{align} \)

That is the red shaded region.

Finally, the third inequality

\(\begin{align} \PalBc{x-3} & \PalBc \leq \PalBc 0& \text{or} \\ \PalBc{x} & \PalBc \leq \PalBc 3 \end{align}\)

is satisfied by all points on and to the left of the vertical line (PalBc{x=3}).  That is the yellow shaded region.

The solution is the overlap region of all these inequalities, that is the brown shaded region shown in the graph below.

Note that the solution is called a closed or bounded solution because there are lines on all sides of the solution (i.e. the overlap region as indicated on the graph).
