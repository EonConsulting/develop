Terminology

In a fraction, the denominator tells us how many parts the whole is divided into and the numerator tells us how many of those parts we are dealing with, for example:

Take note of some other important fraction terms to remember:

Proper fraction

The numerator is less than the denominator, for example \(\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}\) or \(\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\).

Improper fraction

The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator, for example \(\displaystyle\frac{5}{3}\) or \(\displaystyle\frac{9}{9}\).

Mixed number/fraction

A whole number and a fraction, for example \(2\displaystyle\frac{1}{3}\) or \(5\displaystyle\frac{4}{9}\).

Equivalent fractions

Fractions that represent the same number, for example \(\displaystyle\frac{4}{6}\) and \(\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\).

Reciprocal

The multiplicative inverse of a number. For a fraction it means 'turn the fraction around', for example \(\displaystyle\frac{1}{10}\) and \(10\).
