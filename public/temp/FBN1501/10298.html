Converting

The pictures below illustrate the following two interesting facts about volume:

  • The volume of a cube with sides \(\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{1\:\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{1\:\text{cm}} \) \((\PalAa{\text{cm}^3})\) is equal to one millilitre:

  • You can pour one litre of liquid into a cube with sides \(\PalAa{10\:\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{10\:\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{10\:\text{cm}} \) \((\PalAa{1\Tsp000\:\text{cm}^3}\text{ or } \PalAa{10^3\:\text{cm}^3})\):

Convert between litre and \(\text{cm}^3\)

The volume of \(\PalAb{1\:\ell}\) is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} \PalAb V&=l\times w\times h\\ &=10\times10\times10&& ( \PalAa{\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{\text{cm}}\times \PalAa{\text{cm}}) \\ &=\PalAa{1\Tsp000}&&(\PalAa{\text{cm}^3}) \end{align}\)

Therefore, \(\PalAb{1\:\ell}\) equals \(\PalAa{1\Tsp000\:\text{cm}^3}\) or \(\PalAa{10^3\:\text{cm}^3}\).

\(\begin{align} 1\:\PalAa{\text{cm}^3} &=1\:\PalAb{\text{m}\ell}\\ 1\Tsp000\:\PalAa{\text{cm}^3} =10^3\:\PalAa{\text{cm}^3} &= 1\Tsp000\:\PalAb{\text{m}\ell}=1\:\PalAb{\ell}\\ \end{align}\)
  • To change from \(\PalAb{\ell}\) to \(\PalAa{\text{cm}^3}\) (or \(\text{m}\ell\)) multiply by \(10^3\).
  • To change from \(\PalAa{\text{cm}^3}\) (or \(\text{m}\ell\)) to \(\PalAb{\ell}\) divide by \(10^3\) or multiply with \(10^{-3}\).

Convert between litre and \(\text{m}^3\)

The volume of \(\PalAb{1\:\ell}\) is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} \PalAb V&=l\times w\times h&&(10\:\text{cm}=0\Ndp10\:\text{m})\\ &=0\Ndp10\times0\Ndp10\times0\Ndp10&& ( \PalAa{\text{m}} \times \PalAa{\text{m}}\times \PalAa{\text{m}}) \\ &=\PalAa{0\Ndp001}&&(\PalAa{\text{m}^3}) \end{align}\)

Therefore, \(\PalAb{1\:\ell}\) equals \(\PalAa{0\Ndp001\:\text{m}^3}\) or \(\PalAa{10^{-3}\:\text{m}^3}\).

  • To change from \(\PalAb{\ell}\) to \(\PalAa{\text{m}^3}\) divide by \(10^3\) or multiply with \(10^{-3}\).
  • To change from \(\PalAa{\text{m}^3}\) to \(\PalAb{\ell}\) multiply by \(1\Tsp000\) or \(10^3\).
