Dependent and independent variables

The independent variable is the variable you change, and the
dependent variable changes because of it (cause and effect).

Suppose Mary has a house cleaning business, getting paid \(\Cur285\Cdp00\) per house.  She wants to calculate the amount of money she can make.  She can use the formula:

 \(i=\Cur285\Cdp00\times h\)

where the variable \(i\) represents her income in rand and the variable \(h\) the number of houses cleaned.  

A change in the value of \(\PalAb h\) will change the value of \(\PalAa i\). Therefore, \(\PalAa i\) is dependent on \(\PalAb h\).

\(\PalAa i=\Cur285\Cdp00\times \PalAb h\)

\(\PalAa i\) is the dependent variable   \(\PalAb h\) is the independent variable
