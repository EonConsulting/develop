Of fractions

Calculate the square root of a fraction without using a calculator.  

For example, calculate the following:

\(\sqrt{\displaystyle\frac{225}{16}}\)

When \(225\) is divided by \(16\), the answer rounded to two decimal places, is \(14\Ndp06\).  How do we take the square root of \(14\Ndp06\)?  The calculation can be done as follows:

\(\begin{align} \sqrt{\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{225}}{\PalAb{16}}}&=\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{225}}}{\sqrt{\PalAb{16}}}\\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{15}\times\PalAa{15}}}{\sqrt{\PalAb{4}\times\PalAb{4}}}\\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{15}}{\PalAb{4}}\\ &=3\displaystyle\frac{3}{4} \end{align}\)

From this example, we can formulate a property of square roots related to division, namely:

\(\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa a}}{\sqrt{\PalAb b}}=\sqrt{\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa a}{\PalAb b}}\)
