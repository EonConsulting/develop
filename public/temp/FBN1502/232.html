Example

Draw the graph of the quadratic function

\(y=-2x^2+8x-6\).

Step 1

From the function

we have that 

\(y\)

\(\PalCa a\)

\(=f(x)=-2x^2+8x-6\)

\(=\PalCa{-2}\), \(b=8\) and \(\PalCd{c}=\PalCd{-6}\).

Step 2

The function has a maximum value because

\(\PalCa a\)

\(\PalCa{<0}\).

Step 3

\(x\)-coordinate of vertex is

\(x\)

\(=-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\)

\(=-\displaystyle\frac{(8)}{2(-2)}=-\displaystyle\frac{8}{2 \times-2}\)

\(=-\displaystyle\frac{8}{-4}=\displaystyle\frac{8}{4}\)

\(=2\)

Step 4

\(y\)-coordinate of vertex is

\(y\)

\(=f(x)=-2x^2+8x-6\)

\(=f(2)=-2(2)^2+8(2)-6\)

\(=-2 \times2^2+8 \times2-6\)

\(=-8+16-6\)

\(=2\)
 

So, vertex is the point

  

\(\PalCa{(2;2)}\).

Step 5

Value of discriminant

\(\PalCb{b^2-4ac}\)

\(=(8)^2-4(-2)(-6)\)

\(=8^2-4 \times-2 \times-6\)

\(=64-48\)

\(=\PalCb{16}\).

Step 6

The two \(\PalCc x\)-intercepts exist because

\(\PalCb{b^2-4ac}\)

\(\PalCb{>0}\).
 

The two \(\PalCc x\)-intercepts are

\(\PalCc{x_1}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-(8)-\sqrt{(16)}}{2(-2)}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-8-\sqrt{16}}{2 \times-2}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-8-4}{-4}=\displaystyle\frac{-12}{-4}\)

\(=\PalCc3\)
 

and

\(\PalCc{x_2}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-(8)+\sqrt{(16)}}{2(-2)}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-8+\sqrt{16}}{2 \times-2}\)

\(=\displaystyle\frac{-8+4}{-4}=\displaystyle\frac{-4}{-4}\)

\(=\PalCc1\)
 

So, the \(\PalCc x\)-intercepts are

  

\(\PalCc{(3;0)}\) and \(\PalCc{(1;0)}\).

Step 7

The \(\PalCd y\)-intercept is

\(\PalCd y\)

\(=f(0)=\PalCd c\)

\(=\PalCd {-6}\)
 

So, the \(\PalCd y\)-intercept is

   \(\PalCd{(0;-6)}\).
