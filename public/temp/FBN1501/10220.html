Multiply exponents

Consider the simple exponential expression:   \(\left(2^\PalAa3 \right)^\PalAb2\)
Follow the order of operations (BEMDAS).  Start by doing what is in the brackets.  Raise two to the third power: \(=\) \((8)^\PalAb2\)
And remove the brackets: \(=\) \(8^\PalAb2\)
Apply the exponent: \(=\) \(64\)
Which can also be written as: \(=\) \(2^6\)
Which can furthermore be written as: \(=\) \(2^{\PalAa3\times\PalAb2}\)

So, the simplified form of \(\left(2^\PalAa3 \right)^\PalAb2\) is \(64\).  And \(64\) can be written as \(2^6\), which is the same as \(2^{\PalAa3\times\PalAb2}\).

There is a quicker method:

The power of a power property states that to simplify a power raised
to a power, multiply the exponents and remove the brackets.

\(\left(a^\PalAa m\right)^\PalAb n=a^{(\PalAa m\times\PalAb n)}=a^{\PalAa m\PalAb n}\)
