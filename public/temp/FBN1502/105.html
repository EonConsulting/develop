A formula for calculating the present value of annuity

We have seen that if \(R\) is the payment in rand made at each payment interval in respect of an annuity at interest rate \(i\) per payment interval, then the present value of the annuity after \(n\) intervals is given by the formula:

\(P=R\left[[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\)

where \(P\) is the present value of the annuity
  \(R\) is the regular payment
  \(i\) is the interest rate per payment interval
  \(n\) is the number of payment intervals
