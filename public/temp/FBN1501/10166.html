Examples

Commutative law of addition

\(\begin{align} \PalAa6+\PalAa2&=\PalAb2+\PalAb6&\text{because}&&\PalAa8&=\PalAb8 \\ \PalAa{3\Ndp1}+\PalAa{9\Ndp3}&=\PalAb{9\Ndp3}+\PalAb{3\Ndp1}&\text{because}&&\PalAa{12\Ndp4}&=\PalAb{12\Ndp4}\\ \PalAa{-7\Ndp6}+\PalAa{2\Ndp3}&=\PalAb{2\Ndp3}+\PalAb{-7\Ndp6}&\text{because}&&\PalAa{-5\Ndp3}&=\PalAb{-5\Ndp3} \\ \PalAa{-11\Ndp1}+\PalAa{-4\Ndp4}&=\PalAb{-4\Ndp4}+\PalAb{-11\Ndp1}&\text{because}&&\PalAa{-15\Ndp5}&=\PalAb{-15\Ndp5} \end{align}\)

Commutative law of multiplication

\(\begin{align} \PalAa5\times\PalAa2&=\PalAb2\times\PalAb5&&\text{because}&\PalAa{10}&=\PalAb{10} \\ \PalAa{2\Ndp2}\times\PalAa{3\Ndp5}&=\PalAb{3\Ndp5}\times\PalAb{2\Ndp2}&&\text{because}&\PalAa{7\Ndp7}&=\PalAb{7\Ndp7}\\ \PalAa{-8\Ndp1}\times\PalAa{9\Ndp4}&=\PalAb{9\Ndp4}\times\PalAb{-8\Ndp1}&&\text{because}&\PalAa{-76\Ndp14}&=\PalAb{-76\Ndp14} \\ \PalAa{-11}\times\PalAa{-13}&=\PalAb{-13}\times\PalAb{-11}&&\text{because}&\PalAa{143}&=\PalAb{143} \end{align}\)

Therefore, the commutative laws say you can swop numbers around and still get the same answer.
