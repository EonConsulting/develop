The Laspeyres price index

The Laspeyres price index for 2014 with 2013 as the base year is \(\PalAa{98\Ndp85}\).  What is the meaning of this?

The index is less than a \(100\).  Subtract \(100\Ndp00\) from \(\PalAa{98\Ndp85}\):

\(\PalAa{98\Ndp85}-100\Ndp00=-1\Ndp15\)

The result is a negative number, we have a decrease.

Consider the following explanation: You bought a basket of goods in the base period.
If you bought this basket in the given period, how much would it have cost?

The Paasche price index

The Paasche price index for 2014 with 2013 as the base year is \(\PalAa{97\Ndp84}\).  What is the meaning of this?

The index is less than a \(100\).  Subtract \(100\Ndp00\) from \(\PalAa{97\Ndp84}\):

\(\PalAa{97\Ndp84}-100\Ndp00=-2\Ndp16\)

The result is also a negative number, we have a decrease.

Consider the following explanation: You bought a basket of goods in the given period.
If you bought this basket in the base period, how much would it have cost then?

Example

Consider the same table given above that deals with the prices of magazine advertisement pages.

In 2014 advertisers bought \(2\Tsp270\:(1\Tsp320+950)\) advertisement pages from the two magazines together.  If they bought the same number of pages in 2013, it would have cost them \(2\Ndp16\%\) less than what they paid in 2014.
