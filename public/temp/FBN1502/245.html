Example

Evaluate the expression \(\log_{10}100\) without using a calculator.  Note that the base is equal to \(\PalBa{10}\).

Step 1

Set the log equal to \(\PalBb x\).

\(\log_{\PalBa{10}}\PalBc{100}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBb x\)

To what power must \(10\) be raised to get \(100\)?

Step 2

Use the definition of log to write the equation in exponential form.

\(\PalBa{10}\PalBb{^x}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBc{100}\)

Definition of log: If \(\PalBc y=\PalBa a \PalBb{^x}\) with \(a>0\) and \(a \neq1\), then \(\log\PalBa{_a} \PalBc y= \PalBb x\).

Step 3

\(\PalBa{10} \PalBb{^x}\)

\(10^x\)

\(10^x\)

\(=\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBc{100}\)

\(10 \times10\)

\(10^2\)

 
 

From this

\(\PalBb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBb 2\).

  

Therefore, \(\log_{10}100=2\).
