Example - Even

When computing the median of an even number of data values in a sample, the first step is to order the data values from the smallest to largest.

Refer to the example below to discover the rest of the procedure to compute the median of an even number of data values.

A survey of \(14\) employees' performance scores out of \(50\) gave the following results, ordered from lowest to highest:

\(32\) \(35\) \(36\) \(36\) \(37\) \(38\) \(\PalAb{38}\) \(\PalAb{39}\) \(39\) \(39\) \(40\) \(40\) \(42\) \(45\)

The median is the midpoint between the two middle scores.  In this case the median is the value \(\PalAb{38\Ndp5}\).  It was found by adding the two middle scores together and dividing by two:

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb{38}+\PalAb{39}}{2}=\PalAb{38\Ndp5}\)

If the two middle scores are the same value, then the median is that value.

How do we know where the middle number is?

Given a data set of size \(\PalAa{n}\) sorted in ascending sequence,
the position of the median \((\PalAb{Me})\) is the \(\PalBc{\left(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa n+1}{2}\right)^{\text{th}}}\) value.

Our data set has \(\PalAa{14}\) values, so the position of the median is the following value:

\(\PalBc{\left(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa n +1}{2}\right)^{\text{th}}\text{value}}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa {14} +1}{2}=\frac{15}{2}=\PalBc{7\Ndp5^\text{th} \text{ value}}\)

The median is the \(\PalBc{7\Ndp5^{\text{th}}}\) value in the ordered data set.  So, the median is \(\PalAb{38\Ndp5}\).

  \(\PalBc{7\Ndp5^{\text{th}}}\)  
\(\PalAa{1^{\text{st}}}\) \(\PalAa{2^{\text{nd}}}\) \(\PalAa{3^{\text{rd}}}\) \(\PalAa{4^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{5^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{6^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{7^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{8^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{9^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{10^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{11^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{12^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{13^{\text{th}}}\) \(\PalAa{14^{\text{th}}}\)
\(32\) \(35\) \(36\) \(36\) \(37\) \(38\) \(\PalAb{38}\) \(\PalAb{39}\) \(39\) \(39\) \(40\) \(40\) \(42\) \(45\)
  \(\PalAb{38\Ndp5}\)  

Note that it is not taken into account whether there is a duplication of scores around the median.
