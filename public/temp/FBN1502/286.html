Example

A factory manufactures office desks.  The manufacturing costs for a month are given below:

  cost of wood and other materials amount to \(\Cur105\Cdp00\) per desk,
  direct labour costs \(\Cur175\Cdp00\) per desk,
  variable factory overheads amount to \(\Cur70\Cdp00\) per desk and
  fixed factory overheads amount to \(\Cur100\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for the month.

Calculate the total monthly cost of the factory.

Solution

The variable cost per month is calculated from three costs:

  wood and other materials per desk \(\Cur105\Cdp00\)  
  labour per desk \(\Cur175\Cdp00\)  
  variable factory overheads per desk \(\Cur70\Cdp00\)  
    \(\overline{\Cur350\Cdp00}\)  

Let \(\PalAb q\) represent the number of desks (quantity) the factory manufactures per month.

The variable cost is therefore \(\Cur350\Cdp00\) per desk or 

\(350\times \PalAb q = \PalAb{350q}\).

The fixed cost per month is \(\PalBc{\Cur100\Tsp000\Cdp00}\).

The total cost \((\PalAa{TC})\) is calculated as

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{TC}&=\PalBc{\text{fixed cost}}+\PalAb{\text{variable cost}}\\ &=\PalBc{100\Tsp000}+\PalAb{350q}. \end{align} \)
