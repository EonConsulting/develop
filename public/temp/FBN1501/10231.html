Of numbers

If we use numbers instead of variables, part of the answer can be given as a root.

Calculate \(\sqrt{50}\) as follows without a calculator:

\(\begin{align} \sqrt{50}&=\sqrt{\PalAa{25}\times\PalAb2}\\ &=\sqrt{\PalAa{25}}\times\sqrt{\PalAb2}\\ &=\sqrt{\PalAa5\times\PalAa5}\times\sqrt{\PalAb2}\\ &=\sqrt{\PalAa5}\times\sqrt{\PalAa5}\times\sqrt{\PalAb2}\\ &=\PalAa5\sqrt{\PalAb2} \end{align}\)

From this example, we can formulate two properties of square roots related to multiplication, namely:

\(\sqrt{\PalAa a\PalAb b}=\sqrt{\PalAa a}\times \sqrt{\PalAb b}\)

For example:

\(\sqrt{50}=\sqrt{\PalAa{25}\times\PalAb2}\)

\(\sqrt{\PalAa a}\times \sqrt{\PalAa a}=\PalAa a\)

For example: 

\(\sqrt{\PalAa 5}\times \sqrt{\PalAa 5}=\PalAa 5\)
