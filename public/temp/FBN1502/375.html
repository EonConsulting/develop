How compound interest works 1

After one year he has earned \(10\%\) of \(\Cur 600\) as interest, which is \(\Cur 60.\)

His new principal for the second year is

  \(100\%\) of \(600 + 10\%\) of \(600=110\%\) of \(600\)
or \(1 \times 600 + \PalAb{0,10} \times 600 = \PalAa{1,10} \times 600\)
or \(600+60=660\)

We can do this calculation in one step with the multiplication

\(\PalAa{110\% \times 600 = 1,10 \times 600 = 660}\)

During the second year he earns interest on his new principal of \(\Cur660\).

He earns \(10\%\) interest on \(\Cur660\) which is \(\Cur66\).

After two years he will have \(110\%\) of \(\Cur660\), or

\(\PalAa{110\% \times 660 = 1,10 \times 660 = 726}\)
