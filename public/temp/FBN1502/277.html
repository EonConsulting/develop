Activity 1 recap

Making sense of the importance of the shaded region in an inequality is difficult without assigning any context to it.  The following example shows one instance where the shaded region helps us understand a range of possibilities.

Cecile and Julia want to donate some money to a local crèche.  To raise funds, they are selling necklaces and earrings that they have made themselves.  Necklaces cost \(\Cur80\Cdp00\) and a pair of earrings cost \(\Cur50\Cdp00\).

What is the range of possible sales they could make in order to donate a minimum of \(\Cur1\Tsp000\Cdp00\)?

The range of possible sales is given by the inequality

\(80\PalAa x+50\PalAb y\geq1\Tsp000\)

which can also be written as

\(\PalAb y\geq-\displaystyle\frac{8}{5}\PalAa x+20\).

The variable \(\PalAa x\) represents the number of necklaces sold and \(\PalAb y\) the number of pairs of earrings sold.

The shaded region represents all the possible combinations of necklaces and earrings that Cecile and Julia could sell in order to make at least \(\Cur1\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for the crèche.  It is quite a wide range.

Note that while all points will satisfy the inequality, not all points will make sense in this context.  

Take for example the point \((\PalAa{6\Ndp5};\PalAb{15\Ndp75})\).  It satisfies the inequality because

\(\begin{align} \PalAb y&\geq-\displaystyle\frac{8}{5}\PalAa x+20\\ \PalAb{15\Ndp75}&\geq-\displaystyle\frac{8}{5} \times \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{6\Ndp5}}{1}+20\\15\Ndp75&\geq 9\Ndp6 \end{align} \)

is true.  But while this point does fall within the shaded region, it is hard to expect them to sell \(\PalAa{6\Ndp5}\) necklaces and \(\PalAb{15\Ndp75}\) pairs of earrings.  How could they sell half a necklace?

The women can look for whole number combinations in the shaded region to plan how much jewellery to produce.

The point \((\PalAa{8};\PalAb{15})\) also falls within the shaded region.  If they sell \(\PalAa{8}\) necklaces and \(\PalAb{15}\) pairs of earrings, their sales are calculated as

\(\begin{align}80\PalAa x+50\PalAb y&=80 \times \PalAa 8+50 \times \PalAb {15}\\ &=1\Tsp390 \end{align}\)

Their sales will be \(\Cur1\Tsp390\Cdp00\) which is indeed greater than or equal to \(\Cur1\Tsp000\Cdp00\).
