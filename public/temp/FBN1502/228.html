The discriminant and two \(x\)-intercepts

The \(x\)-coordinates of the \(x\)-intercepts of a parabola are

 \(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b-\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\) and \(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b+\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\).

The quantity \(\PalAa{b^2-4ac}\) in the equations above is known as the discriminant since it discriminates between the different types of \(x\)-intercepts.

Discriminant \(=b^2-4ac\)

There are three different types of \(x\)-intercepts, namely:

  • Parabola with two \(x\)-intercepts
  • Parabola with one \(x\)-intercept
  • Parabola with no \(x\)-intercepts

Parabola with two \(x\)-intercepts

When the discriminant is positive, the parabola has two \(x\)-intercepts, as is illustrated below.

\(b^2-4ac>0\)

We can evaluate the square root of the discriminant and determine the two separate \(x\)-intercepts using the given equations.  Note that the \(x\)-intercepts are equally far from the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex.  In fact they are often written in the form

\(\PalAb x=\displaystyle\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}=-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)

to emphasise this fact.  This means, when \(\PalAa{b^2-4ac}>\PalAa{0}\) that there are always two \(\PalAb x\)-values which give the same \(y\)-value (the vertex excluded).

Example

For the parabola

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(x^2+2x-3\),

 

\(a\)

\(=\)

\(1\), \(b=2\) and \(c=-3\).

In the equations

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\)

the discriminant is

 

 

\(\PalAa{b^2-4ac}\)
   

\(=\)

\((2)^2-4(1)(-3)\)
   

\(=\)

\(2^2-4\times1\times-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(4+12\)
   

\(=\)

\(\PalAa{16}\).

The discriminant is positive

\(16\)

\(>\)

\(0\).

Note value of \(\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a} \)

 

 

\(\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a} =\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{(\PalAa{16})}}{2(1)}=\displaystyle\frac{4}{2} = 2\).

So, distance from \(x\)-intercepts to vertex is

    

\(2\) units.

Note value of \(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\)

     \(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}=-\displaystyle\frac{(2)}{2(1)}=-\displaystyle\frac{2}{2}=-1\).

So, \(x\)-coordinate of vertex is

     \(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}=-1\).

Determine the two
\(\PalAb x\)-intercepts

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\)

Substitute the discriminant

  

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{(\PalAa{16})}}{2a}\)
Substitute \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\)  

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{(2)}{2(1)}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{16}}}{2(1)}\)
    \(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{2}{2\times1}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{4}{2\times1}\)

 

 

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{2}{2}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{4}{2}\)
   

\(=\)

\(-1\PalAb\pm2\)

So, the two \(\PalAb x\)-coordinates are

\(\PalAb{x_1}\)

\(=\)

\(-1+2=\PalAb1\)

and

\(\PalAb {x_2}\)

\(=\)

\(-1-2=\PalAb{-3}\).

The \(\PalAb x\)-intercepts are \((\PalAb{-3};0)\) and \((\PalAb 1;0)\).
