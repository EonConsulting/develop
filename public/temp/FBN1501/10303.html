Spread around mean

The standard deviation is the measure of spread most commonly used in statistical practice when the mean is used to calculate central tendency.

The standard deviation can be difficult to interpret as a single number on its own.
Basically, a small standard deviation means that the values in a statistical data set are close to the mean of the data set, on average.  A large standard deviation means that the values in the data set are farther away from the mean, on average.

The standard deviation measures how concentrated the data is around the mean; the more concentrated, the smaller the standard deviation.

The standard deviation measures the spread of data around the mean.
