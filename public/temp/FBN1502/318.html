The investor

It can also work the other way around.

Example

Jane invests \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) in a bank that pays \(\PalAa{12\%}\) simple interest per year for a period of one year.  So to invest the \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for one year, the interest she will earn is:

\(\Cur5 \ 000,00\times0\Cdp12=\Cur600\Cdp00\)

The interest earned is \(\Cur600\Cdp00\).

Of course the bank will have to pay back the original \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) after one year, so this is what happens:

  Today Jane invests \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) in the bank.
  Next year the bank pays back Jane \(\Cur5\Tsp000\Cdp00\) plus \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) interest.
  Jane receives \(\Cur5\Tsp600\Cdp00\) from the bank after one year.

In this case the bank is the borrower and the Jane is the investor.
