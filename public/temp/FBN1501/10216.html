What is the difference between a bar graph and a histogram?

There are two differences; one is in the type of data that is presented and the other is the way in which they are drawn.

Bar Graph

Displays categorical data, that is data that fits into categories.

The bars are usually drawn with spaces between them.

 

Histogram

Presents continuous data, that is data that represents a measured quantity.  The numbers can take on any value in a certain range.

The bars are never drawn with spaces between them because there should not be any gaps between the intervals.
