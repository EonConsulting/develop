Slope

The general equation for a straight line is \(y = ax + b\).  When the slope of the line is zero, the value of \(a\) is zero.  This causes the \(x\) to drop out of the equation altogether.  The equation of the line then becomes \(y=b\).

Equation of a straight line with slope \(0\)

The expression for \(a\) becomes:

 

\(a\)

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \)
   

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{0}{x_2-x_1} \)
   

\(=\)

\(0\)

The numerator (top part) of the fraction can only be equal to zero if \(y_2=y_1\).  That is, if the function values (values for \(y\)) are the same.  In this case the value of \(y\) is not dependent on \(x\).  It is a constant.  The equation of the line becomes:

  \(y\) \(=\) \(ax+b\)
    \(=\) \(0\times x+b\)
    \(=\) \(b\)

This is represented by a straight line parallel to the \(x\)-axis, which is a horizontal line.

The expression for this line is \(y = b\) where \(b\) is the intercept
 on the \(y\)-axis.  There is no intercept on the \(x\)-axis.
