The \(x\)- and \(y\)-intercepts of a straight line

For the straight line \(\PalAa y=a\PalAb x+b\) the \(\PalAb x\)-intercept is where \(y=0\), so then  \(\PalAb{x= -\displaystyle\frac{b}{a}}\), and the \(\PalAa y\)-intercept is where \(x=0\), so then \(\PalAa{y= b}\).

Thus, we can conclude that the line  \(\PalAa y=a\PalAb x+b\) cuts the \(\PalAb x\)-axis at the point \(\PalAb{(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{a};0)}\) and the \(\PalAa y\)-axis at the point \(\PalAa {(0 ; b)}\).
