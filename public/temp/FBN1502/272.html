Example

A linear inequality can be formed by converting a word statement to a linear inequality.

Jacob has \(\Cur2\Tsp000\Cdp00\) in his savings account at the beginning of the holiday.
He wants to have at least \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) in his account by the end of the holiday.
He withdraws \(\Cur280\Cdp00\) each week for snacks and entertainment.  For how many weeks can he withdraw money from his account?

Solution

Write an inequality that represents Jacob’s situation.

Let \(\PalAa n\) be the number of weeks.

Unravel the inequality from the information given:

Information Inequality
  He started with \(\Cur2\Tsp000\Cdp00\).     \(2\Tsp000\)    
  He withdraws money from \(\Cur2\Tsp000\Cdp00\).     \(2\Tsp000-\)    
  He withdraws \(\Cur280\Cdp00\) every week.     \(2\Tsp000-280\times \PalAa n\)    
  He wants to have \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) left in his account by the end of the holiday.     \(2\Tsp000-280\times \PalAa n\)   \(600\)
  It must be at least \(\Cur600\Cdp00\).  There must be \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) or more than \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) left in his account.     \(2\Tsp000-280\times \PalAa n\) \(\geq\) \(600\)

How many weeks can Jacob withdraw money from his account?

Solve the inequality to find the value of \(\PalAa n\):

Solve inequality for \(\PalAa n\)

\(2\Tsp000-280\PalAa n\)

\(\geq\)

\(600\)

Add \(-2\Tsp000\) both sides

\(-280\PalAa n\)

\(\geq\)

\(600-2\Tsp000\)

 

\(-280\PalAa n\)

\(\geq\)

\(-1\Tsp400\)

 Divide by \(\PalAb{-280}\), so reverse \(\PalAb\geq\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-280\PalAa n}{\PalAb{-280}}\)

 \(\PalAb{\leq}\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-1\Tsp400}{\PalAb{-280}}\)
 

\(\PalAa n\)

\(\PalAb\leq\)

\(5\)

Therefore, Jacob can withdraw money from his account for five or less weeks and will still have \(\Cur600\Cdp00\) or more left in his account.
