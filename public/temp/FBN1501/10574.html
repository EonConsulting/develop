Relative frequency

We might not just be interested in the absolute frequencies, but instead in how these numbers relate to each other.  In other words we might want to know the proportion of distances in each interval in order to understand how many distances from each interval make up the whole.  This is called the relative frequency.

For example, \(4\) out of \(80\) distances are in the first interval:

\(\displaystyle\frac{4}{80}=0\Ndp0500\)

\(0\Ndp05\) is the proportion of distances from the first interval.
