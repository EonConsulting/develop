Example

Use substitution to solve the system of linear equations:

\(\begin{align} 5x-y&=1\\ 4x-y&=-1 \end{align}\)

Method

Step 1

Isolate a variable in an equation.

Step 2

Substitute the result of Step 1 into the other equation and solve for one variable.

Step 3

Substitute the result of Step 2 into the result of Step 1 and solve for the other variable.

Step 4

State the solution.

Solution

Use substitution to solve the following system of linear equations:

\(\begin{align} \PalCc{5x-y}&\PalCc=\PalCc1\\ \PalCd{ 4x-y}&\PalCd{=-1} \end{align}\)

Step 1

Isolate \(\PalCa y\) in the first equation:

\(\PalCc{5x-}\PalCa y\)

\(\PalCc=\)

\(\PalCc1\)

 

(subtract \(5x\) both sides)

\(-\PalCa y\)

\(=\)

\(-5x+1\)
 

(divide both sides by \(-1\))

\(\PalCa y\)

\(=\)

\(5x-1\)

Step 2

Substitute result of Step 1 \(\PalCa {y=\PalCa5x-1}\) into second equation and find value of \(\PalCb x\):

\( \PalCd{ 4\PalCb x-\PalCa y}\)

\(\PalCd=\)

\(\PalCd{-1}\)
 

(substitute \(\PalCa {y=\PalCa5x-1}\) in)

\( 4\PalCb x-(\PalCa {5x-1})\)

\(=\)

\(-1\)
   

\( 4\PalCb x-5\PalCb x+1\)

\(=\)

\(-1\)
 

(subtract \(1\) both sides)

\(-\PalCb x+1-1\)

\(=\)

\(-1-1\)
   

\(-\PalCb x\)

\(=\)

\(-2\)
 

(divide both sides by \(-1\))

\(\PalCb x\)

\(=\)

\(2\)

Step 3

Substitute result of Step 2 \(\PalCb{x=2}\) into result of Step 1 \(\PalCa {y=\PalCa5x-1}\) and find value of \(\PalCa y\):

\(\PalCa {y}\)

\(\PalCa {=}\)

\(\PalCa{5\PalCb x-1}\)
 

(substitute \(\PalCb{x=2}\) in)

\(\PalCa {y}\)

\(=\)

\(5(\PalCb2)-1\)
 

(calculate and simplify)

\(\PalCa {y}\)

\(=\)

\(10-1\)
   

\(\PalCa {y}\)

\(=\)

\(9\)

Step 4

Since \(\PalCb{x=2}\) and \(\PalCa{y=9}\), the solution is \((2;9)\).

    

When the lines are drawn you can see that they intersect at \((2;9)\).
