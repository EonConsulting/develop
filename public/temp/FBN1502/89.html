Present value (\(P\))

Sometimes you have to determine the present value of the loan before you can calculate the payments. 

The present value of the loan is the amount that is
borrowed and has to be repaid in equal payments.

Example 1

Sithuli wants to buy a flat screen television for \(\Cur 8 \Tsp 000 \Cdp 00\).  He agrees to pay a deposit of \(\Cur 600 \Cdp 00\) and a monthly instalment for two years at an interest rate of \(12\%\) per annum, compounded monthly.  What is the amount he borrows? In other words, what is the present value of the loan?

Price of television is

\(\Cur 8 \Tsp 000 \Cdp 00\)

Deposit is

\(\Cur 600 \Cdp 00\)

Present value of loan is

\(\begin{align} &\text{price of television}\:-\:\text{deposit}\\ &=\:\Cur 8 \Tsp 000 \Cdp 00-\Cur 600 \Cdp 00\\ &=\:\Cur 7 \Tsp400 \Cdp 00 \end{align}\)

The present value of the loan is \(\Cur 7 \Tsp400 \Cdp 00\).  This is the amount that he borrows after the deposit is subtracted.

Example 2

Jonathan purchases an apartment by making a down payment of \(\Cur 40 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\) and obtains a \(20\) year loan for the balance of \(\Cur 280 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\) at \(15\%\) per annum, compounded monthly.  What is the price of the apartment and the present value of the loan?

Deposit is

\(\Cur 40 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\)

Price of apartment is

\(\begin{align} &\text{present value of loan}\:+\:\text{deposit}\\ &=\:\Cur 280 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00+\Cur 40 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\\ &=\:\Cur 320 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00 \end{align}\)

The price of the apartment is \(\Cur 320 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\).  He has \(\Cur 40 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\) which he uses as a deposit and he borrows the rest, namely \(\Cur 280 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\).  The present value of the loan is \(\Cur 280 \Tsp000 \Cdp 00\).  This is the amount that he borrows after the deposit is subtracted.

Example 3

Sam wants to buy a new fridge for \(\Cur 6 \Tsp500 \Cdp 00\).  He agrees to pay a deposit of \(15\%\) of the price and a monthly instalment for one year at an interest rate of \(11\%\) per annum, compounded monthly.  What is the amount he borrows? In other words, what is the present value of the loan?

Price of fridge is

\(\Cur 6 \Tsp500 \Cdp 00\)

Deposit is

\(\begin{align} &15\%\: \text{of}\:\Cur 6 \Tsp500 \Cdp 00\\ &=\:\frac{15}{100}\times\Cur 6 \Tsp500 \Cdp 00\\ &=\:0.15\times\Cur 6 \Tsp500 \Cdp 00\\&=\:\Cur 975 \Cdp 00 \end{align}\)

He will pay a deposit of

\(\Cur 975 \Cdp 00\)

Present value of loan is

\(\begin{align} &\text{price of fridge}\:-\:\text{deposit}\\ &=\:\Cur 6 \Tsp500 \Cdp 00-\Cur 975 \Cdp 00\\ &=\:\Cur 5 \Tsp525 \Cdp 00 \end{align}\)

The present value of the loan is \(\Cur 5 \Tsp525 \Cdp 00\).  This is the amount that he borrows after the deposit is subtracted.
