Case 3

\(x\) percent of \( y\) is \(z\)

The percentage and final number is known.  What is the base or initial number?  To determine the base number, we write "\(\PalAa x\) percent of \(\PalAb y\) is \(\PalBc z\)" as:

\(\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y=\PalBc z\)

And solve for \(\PalAb y\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y&=\PalBc z\\ \PalAb y&=\PalBc z\times \PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{100}{x}}\\ \PalAb y&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc z}{\PalAa x}\times \PalAa{100} \end{align}\)

\(60\) is \({30\%}\) of what number?  In this case \(\PalAa x\) equals \(\PalAa{30}\), \(\PalBc z\) equals \(\PalBc{60}\) and \(\PalAb y\) is unknown.  So, the answer can be calculated as:

\(\PalAb y=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc z}{\PalAa x}\times \PalAa{100} =\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{60}}{\PalAa{30}}\times \PalAa{100}=2\times{100}=\PalAb{200}\)

Example

A DVD player is offered as a special for \(\Cur3\Tsp600\Cdp00\) and it states that this is \(75\%\) of the usual price.  What was the price before the discount?

So, we need to calculate the usual price of the DVD player if \(75\%\) of this price is \(\Cur3\Tsp600\Cdp00\).  In this case \(\PalAa x\) equals \(\PalAa{75}\), \(\PalBc z\) equals \(\PalBc{3\Tsp600}\) and \(\PalAb y\) is the unknown price.  The price can be calculated using the formula as above, or by dividing with the percentage written as a decimal number.

Writing the percentage as a decimal number gives \(\PalAa{0\Ndp75}\).  Divide \(\PalBc{3\Tsp600}\) by \(\PalAa{0\Ndp75}\):

\(\begin{align}\PalAa{75\%}\text{ of the} \PalAb{\text{ price}}&=\PalBc{\Cur3600\Cdp00}\\ \PalAa{0\Ndp75}\times\PalAb{\text{ price}}&=\PalBc{\Cur3600\Cdp00}\\ \PalAb{\text{ price}}&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{\Cur3600\Cdp00}}{\PalAa{0\Ndp75}}\\ &=\PalAb{\Cur4\Tsp800\Cdp00} \end{align}\)

The normal price is \(\Cur4\Tsp800\Cdp00\).

To check the answer, calculate the ratio of \(3\Tsp600:4\Tsp800\) which reduces to \(0\Ndp75:1\).
Multiplying by \(100\) gives \(75\%\).
