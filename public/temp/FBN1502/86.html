Formula

The example in the video illustrates the typical amortisation problem, namely:

A loan of present value \(P\) rand must be amortised over payments at interest rate \(i\) per payment interval. What is the amount of the payment \(R\)?

As pointed out in the video example, the \(n\) payments form an ordinary annuity, so we can use the formula for the present value of such an annuity to calculate \(R\):

Present value formula

\(P=R\times\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\)

therefore

\(R=P\div\left[\displaystyle\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]\)  \(\)

or simplified

\(R=P\times\left[\displaystyle\frac{i(1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n-1}\right]\)

We have also seen that

Amortisation is the process of gradually reducing a debt
through instalment payments of principal and interest.
