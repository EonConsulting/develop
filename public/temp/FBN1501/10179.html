Greatest Common Factor

We reduce a fraction to lowest terms by finding an equivalent fraction in which the numerator and denominator are as small as possible.

So, this means that when a fraction has been reduced, there is no number except one, that can be divided exactly into both the numerator and the denominator.

To reduce a fraction to its lowest terms, divide the numerator and denominator by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF).

The Greatest Common Factor is the largest number that divides two or more numbers evenly.

This is also called simplifying the fraction.

Example

Simplify the fraction \(\displaystyle\frac{20}{60}\) by reducing it to its lowest terms:

\(\displaystyle\frac{20}{60}=\displaystyle\frac{20\div\PalAb{20}}{60\div\PalAb{20}}=\displaystyle\frac{1}{3}\)

The GCF of \(20\) and \(60\) is \(\PalAb {20}\).
