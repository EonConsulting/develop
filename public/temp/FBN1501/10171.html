Definition

Distributive law of multiplication over addition

The product of a number with the sum of two other
numbers is equal to the sum of the products of the
first number with each of the other two numbers.

For variables \(\PalAa A\), \(\PalAb B\) and \(\PalBc C\) this law can be written as:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa A\times(\PalAb B+\PalBc C)&=\PalAa A\times\PalAb B+\PalAa A\times\PalBc C \end{align} \)

\(\begin{align} \PalAa 3\times(\PalAb 2+\PalBc 4)&=\PalAa 3\times\PalAb 2+\PalAa 3\times\PalBc 4 \end{align} \)

This means that \(\PalAa3\) lots of \((\PalAb2+\PalBc4)\) is the same as \(\PalAa3\) lots of \(\PalAb2\) plus \(\PalAa3\) lots of \(\PalBc4\).

So, the \(\PalAa3\times\) can be distributed across the \(\PalAb2+\PalBc4\) into \(\PalAa 3\times\PalAb 2\) and \(\PalAa 3\times\PalBc 4\).
