Compare data sets

Another reason for studying the dispersion in a set of data is to compare the spread in two or more sets of data.

When comparing the spread of two separate data sets, named A and B, that have approximately the same mean, data set A has a smaller standard deviation.  It has a narrower spread of measurements around the mean and therefore usually has comparatively fewer high or low data values.  Data set B has a larger standard deviation.  It has a wider spread of measurements around the mean and therefore usually has more high and low data values.

An item selected at random from data set A whose standard deviation is low has a better chance of being close to the mean than an item from data set B whose standard deviation is higher.
