Introduction

Not many people can afford to buy a house or brand new car for cash.  Most of us have to borrow money from a bank to make such a big purchase.  We usually repay such a loan to the bank over a long period of time in fixed payments which include interest on the amount still owed to the bank.

The loan is said to be amortised when all liabilities, that is both the principal (the amount of the loan) and interest, are paid by a sequence of equal payments made at equal intervals of time.

Typical questions that we may ask about this process are:

  • What should the amount of the equal payments be?
  • What is the remaining value of the loan still to be repaid at any stage during its repayment?  
  • What is the total interest payable on the loan?

These may sound like difficult questions to answer, but if you consider that this way of paying back a loan is just an ordinary annuity, the good news is that you already have the tools to answer these questions!
