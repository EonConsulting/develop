Recap

Consider an obligation of \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) which falls due in three years.  Interest is credited quarterly at an interest rate of \(12\Ndp5\%\) per annum.

Moving backwards

Suppose you want to repay the \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) at the end of month six (two quarters from now).

If you move \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) backwards from quarter twelve to quarter two, you will pay less than \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\).  You will save on interest because you pay back the debt earlier than originally planned.

The amount of \(\PalBc{\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) at quarter twelve is the future value \(\PalBc S\).  You want to determine the amount at quarter two which is the present value \(\PalAa P\).
The value of \(\PalAb n\) is ten because the \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) is moved backwards by ten quarters.

You calculate the present value using \(\PalAa P=\PalBc S(1+i)^{-\PalAb n}\).

Moving forwards

Suppose you do not have money to repay the \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) after three years and want to repay it after four years.

If you move \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) forwards, from quarter 12 to quarter 16, you will pay more than \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\).  You will pay interest on the \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) because you pay back the debt later than originally planned.

The amount of \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) at quarter 12 is the present value \(\PalAa P\).  You want to determine the amount at quarter 16 which is the future value \(\PalBc S\).  The value of \(\PalAb n\) is four because the \(\Cur50\Tsp000\Cdp00\) is moved forwards by four quarters.

You calculate the future value using \(\PalBc S=\PalAa P(1+i)^\PalAb n\).
