Representation

Written as a whole number and fractions

A decimal number can be written as a whole number plus tenths, hundredths and so forth.

For example, what is \(3\Ndp24\)?

On the left side of the decimal comma is \(3\), that is the whole number part.
There are two digits on the right side of the decimal comma.  The \(2\) is in the tenths position, meaning '\(2\) tenths', and the \(4\) is in the hundredths position, meaning '\(4\) hundredths'.

So, \(3\Ndp24\) is '\(3\) and \(2\) tenths and \(4\) hundredths'.

Written as a decimal fraction

A decimal number can also be written as a decimal fraction.

A decimal fraction is a fraction where the denominator
(the bottom number) is a number such as \(10\), \(100\), \(1\Tsp000\)
and so forth, in other words a power of ten.

The decimal number \(3\Ndp24\) would look like \(\displaystyle\frac{324}{100}\).

And the decimal number \(24\Ndp752\) would look like \(\displaystyle\frac{24\Tsp752}{1\Tsp000}\).

Written as a whole number and a decimal fraction

A decimal number can be written as a whole number and a decimal fraction.  Or, you could think of a decimal number as a whole number plus a decimal fraction.

The decimal number \(3\Ndp24\) would look like \(3\) and \(\displaystyle\frac{24}{100}\) or \(3\displaystyle\frac{24}{100}\).

And the decimal number \(24\Ndp752\) would look like \(24\) and \(\displaystyle\frac{752}{1\Tsp000}\) or \(24\displaystyle\frac{752}{1\Tsp000}\).
