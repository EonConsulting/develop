Multiply and divide

What happens to plus signs and minus signs in multiplication and division?

\(\begin{align}\PalAb+\times\PalAb+&=\PalAb+\\ \PalAb +\div \PalAb+&=\PalAb+ \end{align}\)

  

Two positives make a positive.

\(\begin{align}\PalAa-\times\PalAa-&=\PalAb+\\ \PalAa-\div \PalAa-&=\PalAb+ \end{align}\)

  

Two negatives make a positive.

\(\begin{align} \PalAa-\times\PalAb+&=\PalAa-\\ \PalAa-\div \PalAb+&=\PalAa- \end{align}\)

  

A negative and a positive make a negative.

\(\begin{align} \PalAb+\times\PalAa-&=\PalAa-\\ \PalAb +\div \PalAa-&=\PalAa- \end{align}\)

  

A positive and a negative make a negative.

Examples

\(\begin{align} \PalAb3\times\PalAb2&=\PalAb6\\ \PalAa{-3}\times\PalAa{-2}&=\PalAb6\\ \PalAa{-3}\times\PalAb2&=\PalAa{-6}\\ \PalAb3\times\PalAa{-2}&=\PalAa{-6} \end{align}\)

What about multiplying three or more numbers together?  That is easy, you multiply two at a time and follow the rule.  (Keep in mind, dividing is like multiplying with the inverse.)
