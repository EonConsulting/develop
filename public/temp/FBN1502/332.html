Example

You want to have an amount of \(\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00\) in your account one year from now.  You can earn \(8\%\) interest per year, compounded monthly.  Calculate the amount that you need to invest now.

Invested for one year

We have that the future value \(\PalBc{S=\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) and the interest rate written as a decimal \(i=0\Ndp08\) compounded monthly, for a one year term of  \(\PalAb{n=12}\) time periods.  We need to calculate the present value \(\PalAa P\).

The money needed now is:

\( \PalAa P\)

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc S}{(1+i)^{\PalAb n}}\)

or

\( \PalAa P\)

\(=\)

\(\PalBc S(1+i)^{-\PalAb n}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc {\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}}{\left(1+\displaystyle\frac{0\Ndp08}{\PalAb{12}}\right)^{\PalAb {12}}}\)

  

 

\(=\)

\({\PalBc {\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}}\times{\left(1+\displaystyle\frac{0\Ndp08}{\PalAb{12}}\right)^{-\PalAb {12}}}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\PalAa{\Cur9\Tsp233\Cdp61}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\PalAa{\Cur9\Tsp233\Cdp61}\)

You need to invest \({\Cur9\Tsp233\Cdp61}\) now.

Invested for two years

Now suppose you were offered \(\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00\) after one year or \(\Cur10\Tsp830\Cdp00\) after two years.  The interest you can earn is \(8\%\) per year, compounded monthly.  Which one would you choose?  Most people would probably take the \(\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00\) after one year because it is money in their hands.

Take the \(\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00\) after one year and invest it for another year at \(8\%\):

\(\PalBc S\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa P(1+i)^\PalAb n\)
 

\(=\)

\({\PalAa {\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00}}\times{\left(1+\displaystyle\frac{0\Ndp08}{\PalAb{12}}\right)^{\PalAb {12}}}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\PalBc{\Cur10\Tsp830\Cdp00}\)

At the end of two years you will have \({\Cur10\Tsp830\Cdp00}\).  

We see that the two values are the same, since you have taken the \(\Cur10\Tsp000\Cdp00\) and invested it for another year at \(8\%\) after which it had also accumulated to \({\Cur10\Tsp830\Cdp00}\).

Note that the dated values are not equivalent at some other rate of interest.
