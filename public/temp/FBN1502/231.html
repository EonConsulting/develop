Draw the graph of a quadratic function: Steps

Note that when asked to draw the graph of a quadratic function it is not necessary to plot it in great detail.  Just indicate the intercepts and vertex and the approximate shape.  Parabolas can be drawn in eight easy steps:

Step 1

Write the function in the form

and determine the values of

\(y=ax^2+bx+c\)

\(a\), \(b\) and \(c\).

Step 2

Determine minimum or maximum value

by using \(a\) value.

Step 3

Determine \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex:

\(x_m=-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\)

Step 4

Determine \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex:

\(f \left( \displaystyle\frac{-b}{2a}\right)\)

Step 5

Determine the value of the discriminant:

\(b^2-4ac\)

Step 6

Determine \(x\)-coordinates of
\(x\)-intercepts, if any:

and

\(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)

\(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)

Step 7

Determine the \(y\)-coordinate of the
\(y\)-intercept:

\(y=f(0)=c\)

Step 8

Draw the graph.
