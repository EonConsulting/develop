Lengths in SI system

The standard unit for length is the metre.
The SI abbreviation for metre is \(\text{m}\).

Therefore we write \(10\) metres as \(10\text{m}\).

Lengths in the SI system:
  \(10\) millimetre \((\text{mm})\) \(=\) \(1\) centimetre \((\text{cm})\)
  \(10\) centimetre \((\text{cm})\) \(=\) \(1\) decimetre \((\text{dm})\)
  \(10\) decimetre \((\text{dm})\) \(=\) \(1\) metre \((\text{m})\)
  \(10\) metre \((\text{m})\) \(=\) \(1\) decametre \((\text{dam})\)
  \(10\) decametre \((\text{dam})\) \(=\) \(1\) hectometre \((\text{hm})\)
  \(10\) hectometre \((\text{hm})\) \(=\) \(1\) kilometre \((\text{km})\)

We normally use \(\text{km}\), \(\text{m}\), \(\text{cm}\) and \(\text{mm}\):

  \(1\:\text{km}\) \(=\) \(1\Tsp000\:\text{m}\)   \(\)
  \(1\:\text{m}\) \(=\) \(100\:\text{cm}\) \(=\) \(1\Tsp000\:\text{mm}\)
  \(1\:\text{cm}\) \(=\) \(10\:\text{mm}\)    

In the textile industry where, among other things, clothes are manufactured, centimetres are used for technical reasons.  Due to this all body lengths are measured in centimetres.

This ruler measures in \(\text{cm}\).

This ruler measures in \(\text{mm}\).

A metre is about the length of a person's stride.  You could measure longer things like a room or a garden using metres.

Distance is another word for length and it is convention that distances between places are measured in kilometres.
