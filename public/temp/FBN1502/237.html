Introduction

Exponential functions look somewhat similar to quadratic functions in that they involve exponents, but there is a big difference in that the variable is now the exponent, rather than the base.

Quadratic functions

Quadratic functions are functions such as \(f(\PalAa x)=\PalAa x^2\)where the variable \(\PalAa x\) is the base and a fixed number, such as \(2\), is the exponent.

Exponential functions

Exponential functions, however, are functions such as \(f(\PalAb x)=2^\PalAb x\)where the base is a fixed number, such as \(2\), and the exponent is the variable \(\PalAb x\).
