Rounding

Rounding means reducing the digits in a number while trying to keep
its value similar.  The result is less accurate, but easier to use.

Method to round numbers

Step 1

Decide which is the last digit to keep.

Step 2

Leave it the same if the next digit is less than \(\PalAb5\).  This is called rounding down.

But increase it by one if the next digit is \(\PalAa5\) or more.  This is called rounding up.

Step 3

If necessary, replace removed digits with zeros, for example when rounding whole numbers.

Example of rounding whole numbers

Round \(74\) to the nearest \(10\).

Step 1

We want to keep the \(\PalBc7\) as it is in the tens' position.

  

\(\PalBc74\)

Step 2

The next digit is \(\PalAb4\) which is less than \(\PalAb5\), so no change is needed to \(\PalBc7\).

  

\(\PalBc7\PalAb4 \)

\(\approx\PalAb{70}\)

Step 3

Replace the removed digits with zeros, so the \(\PalAb4\) is replaced by a \(\PalAb0\).

  

\(\PalAb7\PalAb0\)

So, \(74\) was rounded down and the answer is \(70\).

Example of rounding to units

Round \(8\Ndp6\) to the nearest unit.

Step 1

We want to keep the \(\PalBc8\) as it is in the units' position.

  

\(\PalBc8\Ndp6\)

Step 2

The next digit is \(\PalAa6\) which is \(\PalAa5\) or more, so increase the \(\PalBc8\) by one to \(\PalAa9\).

  

\(\PalBc8\Ndp\PalAa6\)

\(\approx\PalAa9\)

Step 3

Since the removed digits came after the decimal comma, it is not necessary to replace them with zeroes.

  

\(\PalAa9\)

So, \(8\Ndp6\) was rounded up and the answer is \(9\).

Example of rounding to different digits

  Rounding \(238\Ndp7\PalBc2\PalAb48\)   to the nearest hundredth gives \(238\Ndp7\PalAb2\).  
  Rounding \(238\Ndp\PalBc7\PalAb248\)   to the nearest tenth gives \(238\Ndp\PalAb7\).  
  Rounding \(23\PalBc8\Ndp\PalAa7248\)   to the nearest unit gives \(23\PalAa9\).  
  Rounding \(2\PalBc3\PalAa8\Ndp7248\)   to the nearest ten gives \(2\PalAa{40}\).  
  Rounding \(238\Ndp72\PalBc4\PalAa8\)   to three decimal places gives \(238\Ndp72\PalAa5\).  
  Rounding \(\PalBc2\PalAb38\Ndp7248\)   to hundred gives \(\PalAb{200}\).
