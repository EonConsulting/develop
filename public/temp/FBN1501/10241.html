Fourth root (Normal)

Let us consider the case when the numbers do not work out perfectly for determining the fourth root.

The fourth root

= =
   

Make four similar groups and a remainder:

  

Take one of the similar groups out of the root.  The remainder is left under the root:

\(\sqrt[4]{x^4y^9}\)

\(=\)

\(\sqrt[4]{\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\times y}\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\sqrt[4]{y}\)

  

An alternative method to determine the fourth root can be used.

Consider \(x^4\).  Four is a multiple of four, so leave it for now.

Also consider \(y^9\).  Nine is not a multiple of four, therefore we will factor it in two powers of \(y\).  The largest factor which is a multiple of four, is eight.  The two factors therefore are \(y^8\) and \(y^1\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^9}}=\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^{8+1}}}=\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^8y^1}}\)

The fourth root of \(x^4\) is \(x^{4\div4}\) and that is equal to \(x^1\).  The fourth root of \(y^8\) is \(y^{8\div4}\) and that is equal to \(y^2\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^8y^1}}=\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\sqrt[4]{\PalAb y}\)

So, the complete calculation can be written as:

\(\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^9}}=\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^{8+1}}}=\sqrt[4]{\PalAa{x^4}\PalAb{y^8y^1}}=\PalAa x\PalAb{y^2}\sqrt[4]{\PalAb y}\)
