Formula

The tyre company Radial advertised that their XXX tyres will travel at least \(65\Tsp000\) 
kilometres before one of the four tyres will no longer meet the minimum safety requirements.  A frequency table has been drawn up, providing data about intervals.

The midpoints for the intervals of Radial's frequency table were calculated, given below.  So, for each interval we have the number of values, frequency
and a midpoint:

Interval Frequency \(\PalAb f_\PalBc i\) Midpoint \(x_\PalBc i\) Percentage \(\%\)
  \(13\Ndp5 - 24\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb4\)     \(\PalBc{19}\)     \(5\Ndp00\)  
  \(24\Ndp5 - 35\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb5\)     \(\PalBc{30}\)     \(6\Ndp25\)  
  \(35\Ndp5 - 46\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb9\)     \(\PalBc{41}\)     \(11\Ndp25\)  
  \(46\Ndp5 - 57\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb{11}\)     \(\PalBc{52}\)     \(13\Ndp75\)  
  \(57\Ndp5 - 68\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb{25}\)     \(\PalBc{63}\)     \(31\Ndp25\)  
  \(68\Ndp5 - 79\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb{17}\)     \(\PalBc{74}\)     \(21\Ndp25\)  
  \(79\Ndp5 - 90\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb8\)     \(\PalBc{85}\)     \(10\Ndp00\)  
  \(90\Ndp5 - 101\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb1\)     \(\PalBc{96}\)     \(1\Ndp25\)  

If we multiply each midpoint by its frequency, add them all and then divide by the total number of values in the frequency table, we have an estimate of the mean.  

Method to determine estimated mean

So, the method to determine an estimate of the mean is as follows:

Step 1 Determine the total number of values by finding the sum of the frequency values.
Step 2 Multiply each midpoint by its frequency.
Step 3 Find the sum of all these products.
Step 4 Divide the sum of these products by the total number of values.

Step 1

Determine the total number of values:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa n&= \sum \PalAb f_\PalBc i\\ &=\PalAb4+\PalAb5+\PalAb9+\PalAb{11}+\PalAb{25}+\PalAb{17}+\PalAb8+\PalAb1\\&=\PalAa{80} \end{align}\)

Step 2

Multiply each midpoint by its frequency:

Interval Frequency \(\PalAb f_\PalBc i\) Midpoint \(x_\PalBc i\) \(\PalAb f_\PalBc i\times x_\PalBc i\)
  \(13\Ndp5 - 24\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb 4\)     \(\PalBc{19}\)   \(\PalAb{4}\times\PalBc{19}=\)\(\) \(76\)\(\)  
  \(24\Ndp5 - 35\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb5\)     \(\PalBc{30}\)   \(\PalAb{5}\times\PalBc{30}=\) \(150\)  
  \(35\Ndp5 - 46\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb9\)     \(\PalBc{41}\)   \(\PalAb{9}\times\PalBc{41}=\) \(369\)  
  \(46\Ndp5 - 57\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb{11}\)     \(\PalBc{52}\)   \(\PalAb{11}\times\PalBc{52}=\) \(572\)  
  \(57\Ndp5 - 68\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb{25}\)     \(\PalBc{63}\)   \(\PalAb{25}\times\PalBc{63}=\) \(1\Tsp575\)  
  \(68\Ndp5 - 79\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb{17}\)     \(\PalBc{74}\)   \(\PalAb{17}\times\PalBc{74}=\) \(1\Tsp258\)  
  \(79\Ndp5 - 90\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb8\)     \(\PalBc{85}\)   \(\PalAb{8}\times\PalBc{85}=\) \(680\)  
  \(90\Ndp5 - 101\Ndp5\)     \(\PalAb1\)     \(\PalBc{96}\)   \(\PalAb{1}\times\PalBc{96}=\) \(96\)  

Step 3

Find the sum of the products of the midpoints and their frequencies.  Add these products together:

\(\begin{align} \sum \PalAb f_\PalBc ix_\PalBc i &=76+150+369+572+1\Tsp575+1\Tsp258+680+96\\&=4\Tsp776 \end{align}\)

The sum of the products is \(4\Tsp776\).

Step 4

Divide this sum of the \(\PalAb f_\PalBc i\times x_\PalBc i\) products by the total number of values:

\(\begin{align} \bar{x} &= \frac{\displaystyle\sum \PalAb f_\PalBc i x_\PalBc i}{\PalAa n}\\ &=\frac{4\Tsp776}{\PalAa{80}}\\ &=59\Ndp70 \end{align}\)

Therefore, the estimate of the mean is \(59\Ndp70\).

The mean calculated for the raw data (the original \(80\) values) of Radial is \(59,79\).  We did not lose too much information by classifying the data into a frequency table.
