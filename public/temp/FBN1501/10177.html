Mixed number to improper fraction

We can convert a mixed number to an improper fraction, using the following method:

Step 1

Multiply the whole number by the denominator of the fraction.

Step 2

Add the numerator of the fraction to the product.

Step 3

Write the sum over the original denominator.

Example

Convert \(1\displaystyle\frac{1}{3}\) to an improper fraction:

 

To convert the mixed number,

  

\(\PalBc1\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa1}{\PalAb3}\)

Step 1

multiply number \(\PalBc1\) with denominator \(\PalAb3\)

\(\)

\((\PalBc1\times\PalAb3)\)

Step 3

and add numerator \(\PalAa1\) to product.

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{(\PalBc1\times\PalAb3)+\PalAa1}{\PalAb3}\)

Step 3

Write sum over original denominator \(\PalAb3\).

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{4}{\PalAb3}\)

In this example, since three thirds is a whole, the whole number one is three thirds, plus one more third equals four thirds.
