Example

There are many situations or problems in the business environment where the values of some unknown quantities have to be found.  We can represent the unknown quantities by the names of two variables and form two linear equations involving the variables.

A company publishes two magazines, namely Fresh Food and Big Bite.  It costs \(\Cur4\Cdp00\) to print a copy of Fresh Food and \(\Cur6\Cdp00\) to bind it.  It costs \(\Cur5\Cdp00\) to print a copy of Big Bite and \(\Cur3\Cdp00\) to bind it.  The budget of the company only allows \(\Cur21\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for binding and \(\Cur20\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for printing.

Set up two linear equations that will determine how many copies of each magazine can be published.

Solution

Let \(\PalCa x\) represent the number of Fresh Food magazines that are published.
Let \(\PalCb y\) represent the number of Big Bite magazines that are published.

The budget of the company only allows \(\PalCc{\Cur20\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) for printing.  It costs \(\PalCc{\Cur4\Cdp00}\) to print a copy of Fresh Food and \(\PalCc{\Cur5\Cdp00}\) to print a copy of Big Bite.

The equation that represents the cost and budget for printing is:

\(\PalCc{\Cur4\Cdp00}\times\) Fresh Food printed \(+\) \(\PalCc{\Cur5\Cdp00}\times\) Big Bite printed \(=\) \(\PalCc{\Cur20\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)
\(\PalCc 4\PalCa x\) \(+\) \(\PalCc 5\PalCb y\) \(=\) \(\PalCc{20\Tsp000}\)

So, the equation that represents the cost and budget for printing is \(\PalCc {4x+5y=20\Tsp000} \). Name this equation one.

The budget of the company only allows \(\PalCd{\Cur21\Tsp000\Cdp00}\) for binding.  It costs \(\PalCd{\Cur6\Cdp00}\) to bind a copy of Fresh Food and \(\PalCd{\Cur3\Cdp00}\) to bind a copy of Big Bite.

The equation that represents the cost and budget for binding is:

\(\PalCd{\Cur6\Cdp00}\times\) Fresh Food bound + \(\PalCd{\Cur3\Cdp00}\times\) Big Bite bound \(=\) \(\PalCd{\Cur21\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)
\(\PalCd 6\PalCa x\) \(+\) \(\PalCd 3\PalCb y\) \(=\) \(\PalCd{21\Tsp000}\)

So, the equation that represents the cost and budget for binding is \(\PalCd {6x+3y=21\Tsp000} \). Name this equation two.
