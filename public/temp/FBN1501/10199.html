Sampling

Sampling is the process of choosing a number of individuals for a study in such a way that the individuals represent the population from which they were chosen.

The theory of sampling is as follows:

  1. Researchers want to gather information about a whole group of people - the population.
  2. Researchers can only observe a part of the population - the sample.
  3. The findings from the sample are generalised, or extended, back to the population.
