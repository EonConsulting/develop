Constant change

Sweet Tooth, which specialises in home-made chocolates, packs chocolates in \(100\text{g}\) gift packets.  The fixed production cost is \(\Cur400\Cdp00\) per day, while the production cost per \(100\text{g}\) packet amounts to a further \(\Cur9\Cdp00\) per packet.
Sweet Tooth cannot produce more than \(200\) packets per day.

Let \(x\) represent the number of packets of chocolates produced per day.

Determine the total cost function \(\PalAa{C(x)}\) and the marginal cost function \(\PalBb{C'(x)}\)of Sweet Tooth.

Solution

The variable cost is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} &\text{variable cost to produce one packet } \times \text{ number of packets produced per day} \\ &=9 \times x\\ &=\PalAb{9x} \end{align}\)

The fixed cost per day is \(\PalBc{\Cur400\Cdp00}\).

The total cost, as a function of \(x\), is given by:

\(\begin{align}\PalAa{C(x)}&= \PalBc{\text{fixed cost}} + \PalAb{\text{variable cost}}\\ &=\PalBc{400}+\PalAb{9x} \end{align} \)

The marginal cost is given by the derivative \(\PalBb {C'(x)=9}\).

Conclusions

We determined the total cost and marginal cost function of Sweet Tooth, which specialises in home-made chocolates, as:

\(\begin{align}\PalAa{C(x)}&\PalAa{=400+9x}&\text{and}\\ \PalBb{C'(x)}&\PalBb{=9}. \end{align}\)

The cost function is a straight line.  The derivative of the function is \(9\), which is a constant.  For a straight line the slope remains constant.
Therefore the marginal cost remains constant.

Remember, Sweet Tooth cannot produce more than \(200\) packets of chocolates per day.  If it produces \(150\) packets per day, the marginal cost is \(\PalBb{\Cur9\Cdp00}\).  The marginal cost is always \(\PalBb{\Cur9\Cdp00}\) a day no matter how many packets of chocolates they produce (as long as it is not more than \(200\)).

Marginal cost implies that the change in the total cost, resulting from producing one additional packet of chocolates, is \(\PalBb{\Cur9\Cdp00}\).

Note that the fixed cost reduced to zero when the marginal cost was calculated.

The marginal cost is only influenced by the variable cost
and is unaffected by the fixed cost.

This is because fixed costs, such as rent or other overheads, generally remain the same, while variable costs will correlate with the number of products manufactured.  The number of products manufactured will in turn affect the marginal cost.
