Introduction

Problems can occur when we work with a measure of central tendency only.
Without knowing something about how data is spread out, measures of central tendency may be misleading.

For example, a residential street, say Street A, with \({20}\) homes on it having a mean value of \(\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00\) with little variation from the mean, would be very different from a street, say Street B, with the same mean home value, but with three homes having a value of \(\Cur3\Tsp500\Tsp000\Cdp00\) and the other \(17\) clustered around \(\Cur500\Tsp000\Cdp00\).

Measures of dispersion provide a more complete picture.

Street A Street B

\(\PalAa{20}\) homes at around \(\PalAa{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

\(\PalAa{20}\times\PalAa{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00} = \PalAa{\Cur19\Tsp000\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

\(\PalAb{17}\) homes at \(\PalAb{\Cur500\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

\(\PalAb{17}\times\PalAb{\Cur500\Tsp000\Cdp00} = \PalAb{\Cur8\Tsp500\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

\(\PalAb3\) homes at \(\PalAb{\Cur3\Tsp500\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

\(\PalAb{3}\times\PalAb{\Cur3\Tsp500\Tsp000\Cdp00} = \PalAb{\Cur10\Tsp500\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

Total value of \(\PalAa{20}\) homes is \(\PalAa{\Cur19\Tsp000\Tsp000\Cdp00}\).

\(\PalAb{8\Tsp500\Tsp000}+\PalAb{10\Tsp500\Tsp000}= \PalAb{19\Tsp000\Tsp000}\)

Total value of \(\PalAb{20}\) homes is \(\PalAb{\Cur19\Tsp000\Tsp000\Cdp00}\). \(\)

\( \PalAa{\Cur19\Tsp000\Tsp000\Cdp00}\div\PalAa{20}=\PalAa{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

Mean value of homes is 
\(\PalAa{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\).

\( \PalAb{\Cur19\Tsp000\Tsp000\Cdp00}\div\PalAb{20}=\PalAb{\Cur950\Tsp000\Cdp00}\)

Mean value of homes is
