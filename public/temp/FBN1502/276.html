Boundary line

Using the Cartesian plane is especially helpful for understanding the range of possible solutions for inequalities with two variables.  When a linear inequality with two variables is graphed in the Cartesian plane, the full range of possible solutions is represented as a shaded area in the plane.

The boundary line for the inequality is drawn as a solid line if the points on the line itself do satisfy the inequality, as in the cases of \(\PalAa \leq\) and \(\PalAa \geq\).

The boundary line for the inequality is drawn as a dashed line if the points on the line do not satisfy the inequality, as in the cases of \(\PalAb<\) and \(\PalAb>\).
