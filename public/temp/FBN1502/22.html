Values of \(a\) and \(b\)

The equation for the straight line is

\(y=f(x)=\PalAb ax+\PalAa b\)

where \(\PalAb a\) and \(\PalAa b\) are constants.  By constants, we mean that \(\PalAb a\) and \(\PalAa b\) are numbers.  

Example

The equation for this straight line is

\(y=f(x)=\PalAb {-2}x+\PalAa 4\)

From this, we see that if the equation for a straight line is

\(y=f(x)=\PalAb ax+\PalAa b\)

then the value of \(\PalAb a\) is equal to \(\PalAb {-2}\) and the value of \(\PalAa b\) is equal to \(\PalAa 4\).
