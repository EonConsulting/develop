The intercept on the \(y\)-axis

The \(y\)-intercept is the point where the parabola crosses the y-axis. The \(y\)-coordinate of this point is simply the value of the quadratic function for \(x=0\), namely \(f(0)\).

So, the intercept on the \(y\)-axis is calculated as:

Quadratic function equation

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(f(x)=ax^2+bx+c\)

The \(y\)-intercept is where

\(x\)

\(=\)

\(0\)

The intercept on the \(y\)-axis is

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(f(0)\)

 

 

\(=\)

\(a(0)^2+b(0)+c\)
   

\(=\)

\(a\times0+b\times0+c\)
   

\(=\)

\(0+0+c\)
   

\(=\)

\(c\)

 

The \(y\)-intercept of the parabola is \((0;c)\).

Example

For the parabola

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(f(\PalAb x)=\PalAb x^2+2\PalAb x-3\)

the \(y\)-intercept is where

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb0\).

Substitute the value of 

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb0\)

into the quadratic function

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(f(\PalAb x)\)

to calculate the \(y\)-intercept:

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(f(\PalAb0)\)
   

\(=\)

\((\PalAb0)^2+2(\PalAb0)-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(0\times0+2\times0-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(0+0-3\)
   

\(=\)

\(\PalAa{-3}\)

So, the \(y\)-intercept is

    

\((\PalAb0;\PalAa{-3})\)
