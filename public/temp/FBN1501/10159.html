Method 1

The annual growth rate reflects the changes
in the annual GDP between different years.

The formula for calculating the annual real growth rate is:

\(\text{Annual growth rate}= \left( \left( \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP current year}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP base year}}}\right)^\frac{1}{\PalBc n}-1 \right) \times 100\)

where \(\PalBc n\) is the number of years between the current and the base year.  

If you calculate the annual growth rate between two consecutive years, the value of \(\PalBc n\) is one and the formula reduces to:

\(\text{Annual growth rate}= \left( \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP current year}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP base year}}}-1 \right) \times 100\)
