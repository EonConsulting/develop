Summary

Total cost, fixed cost and variable cost

  • Cost is a very important factor in determining the success of a company.  Suppose you have the linear total cost function:

\(\PalAa{TC}=\PalBc{40}+\PalAb{5q}\).  

  • The total cost is calculated as:

\(\PalAa{\text{total cost}}=\PalBc{\text{fixed cost}}+\PalAb{\text{variable cost}}\).

  • The variable cost is calculated as:

\(\begin{align}\text{variable cost to produce single unit}\times\text{number of units produced}= 5\times q\end{align}\)

Marginal cost

  • If the total cost is a function of \(x\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{TC}=\PalAa{f(x)} && \text{or}&&\PalAa{TC}=\PalAa{C(x)} \end{align}\)

  • Then the marginal cost is equivalent to the derivative of the total cost function:

\(\begin{align} \PalBb{MC}=\PalBb{f'(x)} && \text{or}&&\PalBb{MC}=\PalBb{C'(x)} \end{align}\)

When to use marginal costing

  • Marginal cost will be used to determine the change in the total cost which results from producing one additional unit.
     
  • Through marginal costing we can ascertain whether our cost of production is rising, falling or constant and thus it helps in formation of a strategic plan for the business.

Marginal cost and exact cost

  • If \(\PalAa{C(x)}\) is the total cost of producing \(x\) items, the marginal cost function approximates the exact cost of producing the \((x+1)^{\text{st}}\) item:

\(\begin{align}\PalBb{\text{marginal cost}}&\approx \text{exact cost}\\ \PalBb{C'(x)}&\approx C(x+1)-C(x) \end{align}\)
