Calculate \(n\) and \(i\)

The formulas for calculating \(i\) or \(n\) when the other variables are given are rather difficult to derive and will therefore just be given.

Formula to calculate the term

To calculate the term when the present value, future value and interest rate are given, use the formula:

\(n=\displaystyle\frac{ln\left(\displaystyle\frac{S}{P}\right)}{ln(1+i)}\)

where \(ln\) (pronounced as 'Lynn') is the logarithm to the base \(e\). Use the \(ln\) key on your calculator.

Formula to calculate the interest rate

To calculate the interest rate when the present value, future value and term are given, use the formula:

\(i=\left(\dfrac{S}{P}\right)^{\dfrac{1}{n}}-1\)
