Special case when \(b=0\)

Consider the special case for which the constant term is zero, that is \(b=0\). 

A straight line \(y=ax+b\) has slope \(a\) and \(y\)-intercept \(b\). If a straight line passes through the origin, then its \(y\)-intercept is \(0\), that means \(b=0\). 

The equation of a straight line passing through the origin
is \(y=ax\) where \(a\) is the slope of the line.

 This means that the intercept on the \(x\)-axis is also zero
