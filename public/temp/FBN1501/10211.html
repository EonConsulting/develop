Consider the tyre company Radial again.  It took a sample of \(80\) out of the population of \(2\:600\) sets of tyres that were sold during the previous six months.
Radial is happy that its sample is representative.  The variable in this sample is the number of thousands of kilometres that a customer had driven until one of the four tyres no longer met the minimum safety requirements.  In short, the variable is the number of kilometres driven.  The sample is given below.

What do we do now with these numbers?

Unfortunately, we cannot make any deductions about the
data from the form in which the numbers are presented.

If we could now envisage the data, we would be able to form a better idea of what is going on.

Before we try doing something with these numbers, let us first consider the different types of data one may get.
