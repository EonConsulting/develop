Part to whole

A part to whole ratio means a ratio can show a part compared to the whole lot.

Example

There are five children, three are girls and two are boys.  Determine the part to part ratios and the part to whole ratios.

The part to part ratios compare the number of boys and number of girls with each other:

The ratio of boys to girls is

\(\PalAa2:\PalAb3\)

or

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa2}{\PalAb3}\).

  

The ratio of girls to boys is

\(\PalAb3:\PalAa2\)

or

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb3}{\PalAa2}\).

  

The part to whole ratios compare the number of boys or girls with the number of children:

The ratio of boys to children is

\(\PalAa2:\PalBc5\)

or

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa2}{\PalBc5}\).

  

The ratio of girls to children is

\(\PalAb3:\PalBc5\)

or

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb3}{\PalBc5}\).
