Introduction

A proportion states that two ratios are equal.

The ratios are the same, so they are in proportion.

When things are in proportion, then their relative sizes are the same.

In the two drawings below you can see that the ratios of the horse's head length to head width are the same in both drawings.  So they are proportional.  Both have the width to length ratio of \(\PalAa1:\PalAb2\), meaning the length is double the width of the head.
Making the head too long or too short would look distorted.
