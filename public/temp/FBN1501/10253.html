Introduction

The word percent comes from the Latin Per Centum.  The Latin
word Centum means \(100\), for example a century is \(100\) years.

When you say "percent" you are really saying "per \(100\)".

For example, a percentage of \(25\%\) means \(25\text{ per }100\).  Therefore, we can say \(\PalAa{25\%}\) (or a quarter) of the block below is shaded blue.

Here are three ways to write the same thing:

  
  • A quarter can be written as a percentage: 

\(\PalAa{25\%}\)
  • A quarter can be written as a decimal:

\(\PalAa{0\Ndp25}\)
  • A quarter can be written as a fraction:

\(\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{1}{4}}\)
