Farmer

A peanut farmer’s cost function in rand, is given by

\(\PalAa {C(\PalAb x)=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+100}\)

where \(\PalAb x\) represents the number of kilograms of peanuts produced.  The cost is in rand.

The revenue function is given by

\(\PalCd{R(\PalAb x)=31\PalAb x}\)

that is, each kilogram of peanuts produced is sold at \(\Cur31\Cdp00\).

His profit is the difference between income earned by selling (revenue) and the cost incurred for the production of the peanuts.

The profit is now given by

\(\begin{align}\PalBc{P(\PalAb x)}&=\PalCd{R(\PalAb x)}-\PalCd{C(\PalAb x)}\\ &=\PalCd{31}\PalAb x-\PalAa{\left(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+100\right)}\\ &=31\PalAb x-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}-100\\ &=-\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb x^2}{100}+31\PalAb x-100. \end{align}\)
