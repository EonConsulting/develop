Method 3

It compares the quarterly level of the real GDP in a specific
quarter with the same quarter of the previous year.
  • The quarters in this method are not seasonally adjusted or annualised.
  • It removes the impact of seasonal variations.
  • It looks at what happened to the economy over the entire previous year and not only at the last \(3\) months.
  • This is not considered as the official method to calculate the economy's growth rate, but remains a very useful measurement.

The formula used to calculated the unadjusted year-on-year quarterly percentage change is:

\(\text{(Y/Y) growth rate}= \left( \displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP}}_{\PalAa{\text{specific quarter in current year}}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP}}_{\PalAb{\text{specific quarter in previous year}}}}-1 \right) \times 100\)
