Sometimes it is difficult to simplify a mathematical expression.  

Consider the expression \(5+2\times4\) .      
Is the answer \(\PalAa{5+2}\times4\) \(=\) \(\PalAa{7}\times4\) \(=\) \(28\)
or \(5+\PalAb{2\times4}\) \(=\) \(5+\PalAb{8}\) \(=\) \(13\)?

In mathematics the exact same problem cannot have two or more different answers.
To eliminate this confusion a common technique for remembering the order of operations, namely BEMDAS was established.
