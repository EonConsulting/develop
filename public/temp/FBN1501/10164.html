Definitions

The words are a mouthful, but the ideas are simple.

Commutative law of addition

The sum of two numbers is unique.  It does not matter which number
is placed first and which is placed second, the result is the same.

Suppose we use variables \(\PalAa A\) and \(\PalAb B\).  Then this law can be written as:

\(\begin{align}\PalAa A+\PalAb B&=\PalAb B+\PalAa A\\\PalAa 5+\PalAb 2&=\PalAb 2+\PalAa 5\end{align}\)

Commutative law of multiplication

The product of two numbers is unique.

For variables \(\PalAa A\) and \(\PalAb B\) this law can be written as:

\(\begin{align}\PalAa A\times\PalAb B&=\PalAb B\times\PalAa A\\\PalAa 2\times\PalAb 3&=\PalAb 3\times\PalAa 2\end{align}\)
