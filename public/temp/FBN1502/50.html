Recap

The equation for a straight line can be determined by using two points on the line, or by using changes in the \(x\)- and \(y\)-values.  

Using changes in the \(x\)- and \(y\)-values is the easier way.

Method using changes in \(x\)- and \(y\)-values

Step 1

Start with the general equation for a straight line \(y=ax+b\).

Determine the value of \(b\), that is the \(y\)-intercept of the line, where \(x=0\).

Step 2

Determine whether the slope \(a\) has a positive or negative value.  The slope is positive if the line ascends and negative if the line descends.

Step 3

Choose any two points on the line.  Calculate the slope as the change in y-value divided by the change in x-value.

So, \(a=\displaystyle\frac{\text {change in }y\text{-value}}{\text {change in }x\text{-value}}\).

Step 4

Substitute the values of \(a\) and \(b\) into the general equation for a straight line \(y=ax+b\) to obtain the specific equation for the line.

Example

Determine the equation for the straight line in the following graph:

Step 1

The general equation for the line is \(y = ax + b\).  Since the line goes through the origin, the \(y\)-intercept is \(0\), therefore \(b=0\).  

So in this case the equation becomes \(y=ax\).

Step 2

The slope will be negative because the line descends when you move from left to right on the \(x\)-axis.  

So, we know \(a\) will have a negative value.

Step 3

Take any two points on the line, such as \((-1;2)\) and \((-3;6)\) as indicated on the graph. Calculate the slope as the change in \(y\)-value divided by the change in \(x\)-value.  

So, \(a=\displaystyle\frac{\text {change in }y\text{-value}}{\text {change in }x\text{-value}} = \displaystyle\frac{6-2}{-3-(-1)}=\displaystyle\frac{4}{-3+1}=\displaystyle\frac{4}{-2}=-2\)

Step 4

Substitute \(a=-2\) and \(b=0\) into the general equation for a straight line \(y = ax + b\).

The equation for the straight line is therefore \(y=-2x\).
