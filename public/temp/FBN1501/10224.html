Negative exponent

If a power has a negative exponent, it is equal to one divided
by the base raised to the corresponding positive exponent.

\(\begin{align}a^{\PalAb{-n}}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb1}{a^\PalAb n}&&a\neq0\end{align}\)

Consider:

\(a^{\PalAb{-1}}=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb1}{a^\PalAb 1}\)

The number \(a^{\PalAb{-1}}\) is now a symbol for the reciprocal, or multiplicative inverse, of any number \(a\).

Therefore:

\(a\times a^{\PalAb{-1}}=a \times \displaystyle\frac{\PalAb1}{a}=1\)
