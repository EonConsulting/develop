Phrases

Many times mathematical signs are not given, but only words like "...at least...", "...no more than..." and
"...at the most…".  How can we write these using only signs?

  • At least \(\PalAb2\) means two or more, two included.  We write it as \(\PalAb{x\ge2}\).

  • No more than \(\PalAa3\) and \(\PalAa3\) at the most both mean everything less than \(\PalAa3\) and \(\PalAa3\) included.  We write it as \(\PalAa{x\le3}\).

  • Less than \(\PalAa3\) means everything less than \(\PalAa3\) and \(\PalAa3\) excluded.  We write it as \(\PalAa{x<3}\).

Note the open circle above the \(3\) on the number line, because \(3\) is excluded in this case.

  • The words "...between \(2\) and \(5\)..." are often confusing.  It must be stated clearly whether the endpoints \(2\) and \(5\) are included or not.

The number \(x\) is less than \(5\) and greater than or equal to \(0\).  It is written as \(0\PalAa\le x\PalAa<5\).
