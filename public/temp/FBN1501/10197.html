Three types

Non-terminating decimal

Convert the fraction \(\displaystyle\frac{22}{7}\) to a decimal number and round off your answer to three decimal places.

 

The fraction

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{22}{7}\) converted to a decimal number is \(3\Ndp1428571\dots\)
 

and

  

\(3\Ndp1428571\dots\approx3\Ndp143\).

The symbol \(\approx\) is read as "approximately equal to".

The decimal \(3\Ndp1428571\dots\) is an example of a non-terminating decimal because the answer goes on and on and is never completed.

Terminating decimal

Convert the fraction \(\displaystyle\frac{3}{8}\) to a decimal number and round off your answer to one decimal place.

 

The fraction

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{3}{8}\) converted to a decimal number is \(0\Ndp375\)
 

and

  

\(0\Ndp375\approx0\Ndp4\).

The decimal \(0\Ndp375\) is an example of a terminating decimal because it is exact and complete.

Recurring decimal

Convert the fraction \(\displaystyle\frac{7}{11}\) to a decimal number and round off your answer to two decimal places.

 

The fraction

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{7}{11}\) converted to a decimal number is \(0\Ndp636363\dots\)
 

and

  

\(0\Ndp636363\dots\approx0\Ndp64\).

The decimal \(0\Ndp636363\dots\) is an example of a recurring decimal because there are repeating digits.

This decimal \(0\Ndp636363\dots\) can also be written as \(0\Ndp\dot{6}\dot{3}\) and is pronounced as "naught comma six three recurring".  The dots on the \(6\) and the \(3\) tell us that the \(6\) and the \(3\) are recurring.

The above recurring decimal can also be written as \(0\Ndp\overline{63}\).
