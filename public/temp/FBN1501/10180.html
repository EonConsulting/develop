Example

A fraction is reduced to its lowest terms, or simplified, when its numerator and denominator have no common factors.  It is easier to multiply, divide, add and subtract fractions when they are simplified.  To simplify a fraction we find an equivalent fraction where the numerator and denominator have no common factors.
To do this we need to use prime factors.

Prime factors

The prime factors of a number are the smallest divisors of that number.

For example, the prime factors of \(12\) are:

\(12=\PalAa2\times\PalAa2\times\PalAa3\)

By smallest divisors we mean that a prime factor has no divisors other than itself and one:
  \(\PalAa2\) can only be divided by itself, namely two, and one.
  \(\PalAa3\) can only be divided by itself, namely three, and one.

Method to simplify fractions

Follow these steps to simplify a fraction:

Step 1

Rewrite both the numerator and denominator as the product of its prime factors, respectively.

Step 2

Find the factors common to both the numerator and denominator (that is the greatest common factor), then divide by all the common factors, called cancelling.

Step 3

Find the products of the remaining factors, if more than one, in both the numerator and the denominator, respectively.

Example

Reduce \(\displaystyle\frac{6}{8}\) to its lowest terms.

 

Simplify the fraction:

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{6}{8}\)

Step 1

Rewrite each of the numerator and denominator as the product of its prime factors.

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa2\times\PalAa3}{\PalAa2\times\PalAa2\times\PalAa2}\)

Step 2

Divide by, or cancel, the factor of two that is common to both the numerator and denominator, that is the GCF.  Since two divided by two equals one, the two's cancel each other out.

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAb\not\PalAb2\times\PalAa3}{\PalAb\not\PalAb2\times\PalAa2\times\PalAa2}\)

Step 3

Left with only a \(3\) in the numerator.  Multiply the remaining factors in the denominator to get \(4\).

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}\)
