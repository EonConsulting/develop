Summary

  • For a discount loan the interest and any other related charges are calculated and paid at the time the loan is granted.
     
  • The borrower receives the reduced amount, called the present value, and must repay the face value or future value of the discount loan.
     
  • The formulas used for simple discount are:

 

 

\(\begin{align} D&=S\PalBc d\PalAb n\\ P&=S-D&\text{and}\\ P&=S\times(1-\PalBc d\PalAb n) \end{align}\)
where \(D\) is the simple discount (in rand) paid for the use of the money at the beginning of the term,
  \(P\) is the present value \((PV)\) of the loan,
  \(S\) is the face or future value \((FV)\) of the loan,
  \(\PalBc d\) is the discount rate, that is the fraction of the future value to be paid at the beginning of the period, and
  \(\PalAb n\) is the term of the loan.
