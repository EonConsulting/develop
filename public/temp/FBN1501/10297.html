Measures surface

The area is a measurement of the surface of a shape.  The surface area is the area that describes the material that will be used to cover a geometric solid.

The SI unit most often used for area is square metre or \(\text{m}^2\).

To find the area of a rectangle or a square you need to multiply the length and the width of the rectangle or the square. In this case, illustrated below,  \(l\) is the length while \(w\) is the width of the rectangle.

The area \(A\) is:

\(\begin{align}A&=l\times w\\ &=lw \end{align}\)

One square metre \((1\text{m}^2)\) is the area size of a square where each side of the square is one metre in length.
Note that \(\text{m}\times\text{m}=\text{m}^2\).

Example

The sketch below shows a rectangular metal plate.  The plate has a length of \(5\:\text m\) and a width of \(3\:\text m\).

The area of the plate is the number of squares, each \(1\:\text{m}^2\), that can fit into the plate.

The area \(A\) is:

\(\begin{align}A&=l\times w\\ &=5\:\text m \times 3\:\text m\\ &=15\:\text m^2 \end{align}\)

The area of the plate is \(15\:\text m^2 \).

That means the number of \(1\:\text{m}^2\) squares that fit into the plate is \(15\).  You can count them to check.
