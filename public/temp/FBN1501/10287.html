Example

Another way of representing data is a pie chart.  Look at this record of traffic travelling down a particular road.

Traffic survey 31 January 2012

Type of vehicle Number of vehicles
Cars  \(140\)
Motorbikes  \(70\)
Vans  \(55\)
Buses  \(5\)
Total Vehicles \(270\)

To draw a pie chart, we need to represent each part of the data as a proportion of \(360\), because there are \(360\)degrees in a circle.

Calculate the degrees that each type of vehicle will take up.  For example, if \(\PalBc{55}\) out of \(270\) vehicles are vans, we will represent this on the circle as a segment with an angle of:

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc{55}}{270} \times360=\PalBc{73}\)

Therefore, \(\PalBc{73}\) degrees out of \(360\), will be represented by the vans.  This will give the following results:
