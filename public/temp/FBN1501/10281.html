Convert large to small

We can describe length as being one dimensional and area as two dimensional.

How would you write square kilometres?  Easy, we write it as \(\text{km}^2\).  How do we convert units of area?  The process involves a little more thinking.
When converting from a large unit \((\text{m})\) to a smaller unit \((\text{cm})\) the number of units will increase.

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{cm}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}}=\:\PalAb{100\:\text{cm}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{m}} \times \PalAa{\text{m}}=\PalAa{\text{m}^2} )\\ \PalAb{100}\times\PalAb{100}&=\PalAb{10\Tsp000}&&(\PalAb{\text{cm}} \times \PalAb{\text{cm}}=\PalAb{\text{cm}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{10\Tsp000\:\text{cm}^2}\).
 

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{mm}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}}=\:\PalAb{1\Tsp000\:\text{mm}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{m}} \times \PalAa{\text{m}}=\PalAa{\text{m}^2} )\\ \PalAb{1\Tsp000}\times\PalAb{1\Tsp000}&=\PalAb{1\Tsp000\Tsp000}&&(\PalAb{\text{mm}} \times \PalAb{\text{mm}}=\PalAb{\text{mm}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{m}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{1\Tsp000\Tsp000\:\text{mm}^2}\) or \(\PalAb{10^6\:\text{mm}^2}\).
 

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{mm}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}}=\:\PalAb{10\:\text{mm}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{cm}} \times \PalAa{\text{cm}}=\PalAa{\text{cm}^2} )\\ \PalAb{10}\times\PalAb{10}&=\PalAb{100}&&(\PalAb{\text{mm}} \times \PalAb{\text{mm}}=\PalAb{\text{mm}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{cm}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{100\:\text{mm}^2}\).
 

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{km}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{cm}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{km}}=\PalAb{100\Tsp000\:\text{cm}}=\PalAb{10^5\:\text{cm}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{km}} \times \PalAa{\text{km}}=\PalAa{\text{km}^2} )\\ \PalAb{10^5}\times\PalAb{10^5}&=\PalAb{10^{10}}&&(\PalAb{\text{cm}} \times \PalAb{\text{cm}}=\PalAb{\text{cm}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{km}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{10^{10}\:\text{cm}^2}\).
 

  • Convert \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{km}^2}\) to \(\PalAb{\text{m}^2}\) \((\:\PalAa{1\:\text{km}}=\:\PalAb{1\Tsp000\:\text{m}})\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa1\times\PalAa1&=\PalAa1&&(\PalAa{\text{km}} \times \PalAa{\text{km}}=\PalAa{\text{km}^2} )\\ \PalAb{1\Tsp000}\times\PalAb{1\Tsp000}&=\PalAb{1\Tsp000\Tsp000}&&(\PalAb{\text{m}} \times \PalAb{\text{m}}=\PalAb{\text{m}^2} ) \end{align}\)

Therefore \(\:\PalAa{1\:\text{km}^2}\) equals \(\PalAb{1\Tsp000\Tsp000\:\text{m}^2}\) or \(\PalAb{10^6\:\text{m}^2}\).
 

Notice that the use of the exponential form for writing large numbers improves readability:

\(1\Tsp000\Tsp000=10^\PalAb6\) and \(10\Tsp000\Tsp000\Tsp000=10^\PalAb{10}\)
