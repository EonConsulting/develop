Case 2

\(x\) percent of \( y\) is \(z\)

What percentage is one number of another?  To determine one number as the percentage of another number, we write "\(\PalAa x\) percent of \(\PalAb y\) is \(\PalBc z\)" as:

\(\PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y=\PalBc z\)

And solve for \(\PalAa x\):

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}\times \PalAb y&=\PalBc z\\ \PalAa{\displaystyle\frac{x}{100}}&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc z}{ \PalAb y}\\ \PalAa{x}&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc z}{ \PalAb y}\times \PalAa{100} \end{align}\)

What percent is \({44}\) of \({11}\)?  In this case \(\)\(\PalAb y\) equals \(\PalAb{44}\), \(\PalBc z\) equals \(\PalBc{11}\) and \(\PalAa x\) is unknown.  So, the answer is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa{x}&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc z}{ \PalAb y}\times \PalAa{100} \\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc {15}}{ \PalAb {44}}\times \PalAa{100}\\ &=0\Ndp625\times\PalAa{100}\\ &=\PalAa{62\Ndp5\%} \end{align}\)

Example

Pumi invited \(24\) people to his birthday party.  Only \(15\) people showed up.
Calculate the percentage of people that showed up.

So, we need to calculate the number of people that showed up as a percentage of the total number invited.  In this case \(\PalAb y\) equals \(\PalAb{24}\), \(\PalBc z\) equals \(\PalBc{15}\) and \(\PalAa x\) is the unknown percentage.  The percentage is calculated as:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa x&=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc z}{ \PalAb y}\times \PalAa{100} \\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\PalBc {15}}{ \PalAb {24}}\times \PalAa{100}\\ &=0\Ndp625\times \PalAa{100}\\ &=\PalAa{62\Ndp5} \end{align}\)

The percentage of people that showed up at Pumi's birthday party is \(62\Ndp5\%\).

Alternatively, we can say that the ratio that showed up is \(15:24\) which reduces to:

\(\displaystyle\frac{15}{24}:\displaystyle\frac{24}{24}\) or \(0\Ndp625:1\)

Multiplying by \(100\) gives \(62\Ndp5\%\).
