Differences between simple interest and simple discount

  \(I=P \PalAb{i}\PalAb{n}\)   \(D=S \PalBa d \PalAa n\)
  \(S=P+I\)   \(P=S-D\)
  \(S=P \times(1+\PalAb i \PalAb n)\)   \(P=S \times(1- \PalAa d \PalAa n)\)

A simple interest rate of \(\PalAa{10\%}\) (calculated on the discounted/present value) is not the same as a simple discount rate of \(\PalAb{10\%}\) (calculated on the face/future value).

Similarities between simple interest and simple discount

  Borrower receives money at the beginning of the term.
  Repaid with a single payment at the end of the term.
