\(\geq\) and \(\leq\)

Sometimes the symbol \(=\) is combined with \(>\) or \(<\) as follows:

\(b\PalAa{\geq} a\) means \(b-a \PalAa{\geq} 0\)   \(b\) is greater than or equal to \(a\),  
\(b\PalAb{\leq} a\) means \(b-a \PalAb{\leq} 0\)   \(b\) is less than or equal to \(a\).  

If we replace the \(=\) sign in any equation, or system of equations, with a \(>\), \(<\), \(\geq\) or \(\leq\), then we obtain an inequality or system of inequalities.

The inequality, or system of inequalities, is linear or non-linear depending on whether the functions involved are linear or non-linear.
