Examples of simple interest and simple discount

Simple interest

Simple discount
 

Interest is calculated on the present value or principal amount.

    

Interest is calculated on the future/face value.

  
 

The loan is an add-on loan in which the interest is added to the principal:

    

The interest, called the discount or discounted amount, is subtracted from the future value and only the proceeds are received by the borrower:

  
 

\(S=P+I\)

    

\(P=S-D\)

  

Example

Happy Homes furniture store wants to borrow \(\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\) for two years.  Community Bank offers them a \(10\%\) simple interest loan.  National Bank offers them an \(8\%\) simple discount loan.  The store wants to compare the implications of these two loans.

Community Bank offers a 

\(\PalAa {10\%}\) simple interest loan.

The following is given:

\(P=\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\)
\(\PalAa{i=0\Ndp10\text{ per year}} \)
\(\PalAb{n=2\text{ years}}\)

The interest Happy Homes will have to pay on the loan is:

\(\begin{align} I&=P\PalAa i\PalAb n\\ &=\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\times \PalAa{0\Ndp10}\times \PalAb 2\\ &=\Cur7\Tsp600\Cdp00 \end{align}\)

The amount Happy Homes will have to repay after two years is:

\(\begin{align} S&=P+I\\ &=\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00+\Cur7\Tsp600\Cdp00\\ &=\Cur45\Tsp600\Cdp00 \end{align}\)

 

National Bank offers an 

\(\PalBc {8\%}\) simple discount loan.

The following is given:

\(P=\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\)
\(\PalBc{d=0\Ndp08\text{ per year}} \)
\(\PalAb{n=2\text{ years}}\)

The amount Happy Homes must borrow in order to receive proceeds of \(\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\) now, is:

\(\begin{align} P&=S(1-\PalBc d\PalAb n)\\ S&=\frac{P}{1-\PalBc d\PalAb n}\\ &=\frac{\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00}{1-\PalBc {0\Ndp08}\times\PalAb2}\\ &=\Cur45\Tsp238\Cdp10 \end{align}\)

The discount subtracted from the future value of the loan is:

\(\begin{align} D&=S-P\\ &=\Cur45\Tsp238\Cdp10-\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\\ &=\Cur7\Tsp238\Cdp10 \end{align}\)

  We now have that:

\(P=\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\)
\(I=\Cur7\Tsp238\Cdp10\)
\(\PalAb{n=2\text{ years}}\)

The equivalent simple interest rate of this loan can now be determined:

\(\begin{align} \PalAa i&=\frac{I}{P\times \PalAb n}\\ \PalAa i&=\frac{\Cur7\Tsp238\Cdp10}{\Cur38\Tsp000\Cdp00\times\PalAb2}\\ &=0\Ndp0952\\ &=\PalAa{9\Ndp5\%} \end{align}\)
