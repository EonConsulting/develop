The formula used to calculate the annual real growth rate between consecutive years is:

\(\text{Annual growth rate}= \left(\displaystyle \frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP}_{\text{current year}}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP}_{\text{previous year}}}}-1 \right) \times 100\)

So, the annual real growth rate from 2007 to 2008 is calculated as:

Annual growth rate

\(= \left(\displaystyle \frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP}_{\text{2008}}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP}_{\text{2007}}}}-1 \right) \times 100\)
 

\(=\left(\displaystyle \frac{\PalAa{\Cur1\Tsp619\Tsp802}}{\PalAb{\Cur1\Tsp561\Tsp076}}-1 \right) \times 100\)
 

\(=\PalBb{3\Ndp8}\% \)

The formula used to calculate the unadjusted year-on-year quarterly percent change is:

\(\text{(Y/Y) growth rate}= \left(\displaystyle \frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP}_{\text{specific quarter in current year}}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP}_{\text{specific quarter in previous year}}}}-1 \right) \times 100\)

The year-on-year quarterly percentage change between quarter 4 of 2011 and quarter 4 of 2012 is calculated as follows:

(Y/Y) growth rate

\(= \left(\displaystyle \frac{\PalAa{\text{GDP}_{\text{quarter 4 of 2012}}}}{\PalAb{\text{GDP}_{\text{quarter 4 of 2011}}}}-1 \right) \times 100\)
