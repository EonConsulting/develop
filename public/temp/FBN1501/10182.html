Recap

Reduce \(\displaystyle\frac{30}{45}\) to its lowest terms. 

 

Simplify the fraction:

  

\(\displaystyle\frac{30}{45}\)

Step 1

Rewrite each of the numerator and denominator as the product of its prime factors.  The prime factors of \(30\) are \(\PalAa2\), \(\PalAa3\) and \(\PalAa5\).  The prime factors of \(45\) are \(\PalAa3\), \(\PalAa3\) and \(\PalAa5\).

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa2\times\PalAa3\times\PalAa5}{\PalAa3\times\PalAa3\times\PalAa5}\)

Step 2

Find the factors common to both the numerator and denominator, that is the greatest common factor, then divide by all these common factors, called cancelling.  The greatest common factor of \(30\) and \(45\) is \(\PalAb{15}\).

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac {\PalAa2\times{\PalAb\not}{\PalAb3} \times{\PalAb\not}\PalAb5} {\PalAa3\times{\PalAb\not}{\PalAb3}\times{\PalAb\not}\PalAb5}\)

Step 3

Find the products of the remaining factors, if more than one, in both the numerator and the denominator, respectively.

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{2}{3}\)

So, to simplify a fraction, we rewrite each of the numerator and denominator as the product of its prime factors.  The prime factors of \(30\) are \(2\), \(3\) and \(5\).  The prime factors of \(45\) are \(3\), \(3\) and \(5\).  

Then we divide the numerator and denominator by the greatest common factor (GCF).  The greatest common factor, that is the largest integer that divides exactly into the numbers \(30\) and \(45\), is \(15\).  We can also say that we cancel out the \(3\) and \(5\) on top of and below the line.
