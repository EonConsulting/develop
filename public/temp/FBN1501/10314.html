Definition

The factorial (denoted by \(!\)) of a natural number is the product of
all natural numbers that are less than or equal to that number.

In other words, the factorial of a non-negative integer \(\PalBc n\), denoted by \(\PalBc n!\), is the product of all positive integers less than or equal to \(\PalBc n\):

\(\PalBc n!=\PalBc n\times(\PalBc n-1)\times(\PalBc n-2)\times\ldots\times3\times2\times1\)

Examples

\(\PalBc3!\) is shorthand for \(\PalBc3\times2\times1\).  \(\PalBc 3!\) is usually pronounced "\(\PalBc3\) factorial".

 \(\begin{align} \PalBc3!&=\PalBc3\times2\times1=6\\ \PalBc2!&=\PalBc2\times1=2\\ \PalBc5!&=\PalBc5\times4\times3\times2\times1=120 \end{align}\)

You can easily calculate the factorial of a natural number from the factorial of the preceding natural number:

\(\PalBc n\) \(\PalBc n!\)        
\(\PalBc1\) \(\PalBc1\)   \(1\)   \(1\)
\(\PalBc2\) \(\PalBc2\times1\) \(=\) \(\PalBc2\times1!\) \(=\) \(2\)
\(\PalBc3\) \(\PalBc3\times2\times1\) \(=\) \(\PalBc3\times2!\) \(=\) \(6\)
\(\PalBc4\) \(\PalBc4\times3\times2\times1\) \(=\) \(\PalBc4\times3!\) \(=\) \(24\)
\(\PalBc5\) \(\PalBc5\times4\times3\times2\times1\)\(\) \(=\)\(\) \(\PalBc5\times4!\) \(=\) \(120\)

Example

What is \(\PalBc9!\) if you know that \(8!=40\Tsp320\)?

\(\begin{align} \PalBc9!&=\PalBc9\times8!\\ \PalBc9!&=\PalBc9\times40\Tsp320\\ &=362\Tsp880 \end{align}\)

So the rule is:

\(\PalBc n!=\PalBc n\times(\PalBc n-1)!\)

This just means that the factorial of any natural number is that number times the factorial of one smaller than that number.  Therefore \(\PalBc9!=\PalBc9\times8!\) 
and \(\PalBc{130}!=\PalBc{130}\times129!\).
