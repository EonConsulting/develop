SI System

It is difficult to imagine a world without a system for measuring things.  You would not know the distance to another town, the capacity in litres of a fuel tank or whether your body mass is within limits, and so forth.

The earliest measuring systems originated in the barter system and units corresponded with things like the length or size of a hand or foot.  Many countries had their own measuring system.  In the modern world which is characterised by international trade and extended industrial and technological development, it became necessary to have a common system.  A modern international system, the Système Internationale d’Unités, also known as SI, was developed.  South Africa was one of the first countries to accept this system.
