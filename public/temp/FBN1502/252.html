Example

The following example demonstrates setting up your own linear equation.  A linear equation can be formed by converting a word statement to a linear equation:

"The first number is three less than two times the second number.  If their sum is increased by seven, the result is \(37\).  Find the numbers."

Determine the second number

Let \(\PalAb x\) be the second number. \(\PalAb x\)    

"The first number is three less than two times the second number."
So, the first number is three less than two times \(\PalAb x\), giving the first number as: \(\PalAa2\PalAb x\PalAa{-3}\)    

"If their sum..."
The sum of the first and second number is: \((\PalAa2\PalAb x\PalAa{-3})+\PalAb x\) \(=\) \(2\PalAb x-3+\PalAb x\)
    \(=\) \(3\PalAb x-3\)

"If their sum is increased by seven, the result is \(37\)."
This gives the equation: \((3\PalAb x-3)+7\) \(=\) \(37\)
  \(3\PalAb x-3+7\) \(=\) \(37\)
  \(3\PalAb x+4\) \(=\) \(37\)
  \(3\PalAb x+4-4\) \(=\) \(37-4\)
  \(3\PalAb x\) \(=\) \(33\)
  \(\displaystyle\frac{3\PalAb x}{3}\) \(=\) \(\displaystyle\frac{33}{3}\)
  \(\PalAb x\) \(=\) \(11\)
So, the second number is \(\PalAb{11}\).      

Determine the first number

The first number is: \(\PalAa2\PalAb x\PalAa{-3}\) \(=\) \(\PalAa2(\PalAb {11})\PalAa{-3}\)
    \(=\) \(2\times11-3\)
    \(=\) \(22-3\)
    \(=\) \(19\)
So, the first number is \(\PalAa{19}\).      

The numbers are \(\PalAa{19}\) and \(\PalAb{11}\).

Check the numbers

These numbers can be checked as follows:

It is true that \(\PalAa{19}=2(\PalAb{11})-3\),

\(\to\)

The first requirement, "the first number is three less than two times the second", is satisfied.
and    

it is true that \(\PalAa{19}+\PalAb{11}+7=37\).

\(\to\)

The second requirement, "if their sum is increased by seven, the result is \(37\)", is satisfied.
