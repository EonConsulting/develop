The intercepts on the \(x\)-axis

The \(x\)-intercepts are the points where the parabola crosses the \(x\)-axis.
These are the values of \(x\) for which the quadratic function is zero: 

\(y=ax^2+bx+c=0\).

The values of the \(x\)-intercepts are difficult to calculate, so they are given.

The \(x\)-intercepts are \(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\) and \(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\).

Example

In our example there are two intercepts on the \(x\)-axis.

For the parabola

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb x^2+2\PalAb x-3\)

with

\(a\)

\(=\)

\(1\), \(b=2\) and \(c=-3\),

the \(x\)-intercepts are where

\(\PalAa y\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAa0\).

Substitute \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) in the formulas

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb{\displaystyle\frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}}\)

and

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb{\displaystyle\frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}}\).

Substitute  \(\PalBa {a=1}\), \(\PalBb {b=2}\) and \(\PalBc {c=-3}\) into the given formulas:

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-\PalBb b-\sqrt{\PalBb b^2-4\PalBa a\PalBc c}}{2\PalBa a}\)

and

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-\PalBb b+\sqrt{\PalBb b^2-4\PalBa a\PalBc c}}{2\PalBa a}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-(\PalBb2)-\sqrt{(\PalBb2)^2-4(\PalBa1)(\PalBc{-3})}}{2(\PalBa 1)}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-(\PalBb2)+\sqrt{(\PalBb2)^2-4(\PalBa1)(\PalBc{-3})}}{2(\PalBa 1)}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\frac{-\PalBb2-\sqrt{\PalBb2^2-(4\times\PalBa1\times\PalBc{-3})}}{2\times\PalBa 1}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-\PalBb2+\sqrt{\PalBb2^2-(4\times\PalBa1\times\PalBc{-3})}}{2\times\PalBa 1}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2-\sqrt{4-(-12)}}{2}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2+\sqrt{4-(-12)}}{2}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2-\sqrt{4+12}}{2}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2+\sqrt{4+12}}{2}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2-\sqrt{16}}{2}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2+\sqrt{16}}{2}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2-4}{2}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-2+4}{2}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{-6}{2}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\displaystyle\frac{2}{2}\)
 

\(=\)

\(\PalAb{-3}\)

    

\(=\)

\(\PalAb1\)

So, the \(x\)-intercepts are \((\PalAb{-3};\PalAa0)\) and \((\PalAb1;\PalAa0)\).
