Formula

A value index measures changes in both the price and quantities involved.

A value index is a ratio which indicates how much the value of expenditure has changed as a ratio of expenditure in the base year.

It gives the value of a basket of goods in a specific year as a percentage of the value of the same basket in the base year.

A value index, such as the index of department store sales, needs the original base year prices, the original base year quantities, the given year prices and the given year quantities for its construction.

The formula for the value index is:

\(V = \displaystyle\frac{\sum \PalBc p_\PalAa n \PalBc q_\PalAa n}{\PalBc p_\PalAb 0\PalBc q_\PalAb 0} \times100\)

where \(\PalBc p_\PalAa n\) is the price in the \(\PalAa{n^{\text{th}}}\) year,
  \(\PalBc q_\PalAa n\) is the quantity in the \(\PalAa{n^{\text{th}}}\) year, 
  \(\PalBc p_\PalAb 0\) is the price in the base year,
and \(\PalBc q_\PalAb 0\) is the quantity in the base year.
