Word problems

Straight line graphs can be used to describe how a lot of things behave in real life.  In many cases the two points which you require to draw the line are not given to you, but you must unravel them from the information given.

Example question

Betty's Phone Service has been calling you to join their service to enjoy low rates. With her plan, for \(60\) minutes of use per week, you pay \(\Cur90\Cdp00\).  If you use \(152\) minutes, your weekly charge is only \(\Cur159\Cdp00\). The weekly charge is a linear function of the number of minutes you use per week

Determine the equation for the straight line that describes this linear relationship.

Example solution

The weekly charge is a linear function of the number of minutes used per week. Therefore, the charge per week depends on the number of minutes used per week:

 \(\PalAa y =f (\PalAb x) = \PalBb a\PalAb x + \PalBc b\) 

where \(\PalAa y\) is the dependent variable
and \(\PalAb x\) is the independent variable.

 

Therefore, let \(\PalAb x\) represent the number of minutes you use per week
and \(\PalAa y\) represent the weekly charge in rands.

The following data for \(\PalAb x\) and \(\PalAa y\) is given:

Number of minutes \(\PalAb {=x}\)  Weekly charge \(\PalAa {=y}\)
  \(60\) minutes     \(\Cur90\Cdp00\)  
  \(152\) minutes     \(\Cur159\Cdp00\)  

Therefore, the two data points that satisfy the linear relationship are \((60; 90)\) and \((152; 159)\).  We need to determine the equation of the straight line that passes through these two points.

Taking \((\PalAb {x_1};\PalAa{y_1})= (\PalAb{60}; \PalAa{90})\) and \((\PalAb {x_2}; \PalAa{y_2}) =(\PalAb{152}; \PalAa{159})\), we can calculate the slope \(\PalBb a\) of the straight line as:

\( \begin{align} \PalBb a &=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{y_2-y_1}}{\PalAb{x_2-x_1}} \\ &=\displaystyle\frac{\PalAa{159-90}} {\PalAb{152-60}}\\ &=\displaystyle\frac{69}{92} & \left({\displaystyle\frac{\displaystyle\div23}{\displaystyle\div23}}\right) \\ &=\PalBb{\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}} \end{align}\)

The general equation thus reduces to: 

\(\PalAa y=\PalBb{\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}}\PalAb x+\PalBc b \).

Substitute the \(\PalAb x\) and \(\PalAa y\) values of the first point \((\PalAb{60}; \PalAa{90})\) in \(\PalAa y=\PalBb{\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}}\PalAb x+\PalBc b \). This gives:

  \(\begin{align} \PalAa y&=\PalBb{\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}}\PalAb x+\PalBc b \\ \PalAa{90}&=\PalBb{\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}}\times\PalAb{60}+\PalBc b \\ \PalAa{90}&=45+\PalBc b \\\PalAa{90}-45&=45-45+\PalBc b & \text{(subtract 45 both sides)} \\\PalBc b&=90-45 \\\PalBc b&=45 \end{align}\)

The equation for the line passing through the two points \((60; 90)\) and \((152; 159)\) is therefore \(\PalAa y=\PalBb{\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}}\PalAb x+\PalBc {45} \).

Answer

The equation for the straight line that describes the linear relationship is \( y={\displaystyle\frac{3}{4}} x+{45} \).
