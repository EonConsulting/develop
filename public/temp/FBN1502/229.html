The discriminant and one \(x\)-intercept

The \(x\)-coordinates of the \(x\)-intercepts of a parabola are:

\(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b-\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\) and \(x=\displaystyle\frac{-b+\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\).

The quantity \(\PalAa{b^2-4ac}\) in the equations above is known as the discriminant since it discriminates between the different types of \(x\)-intercepts.

Discriminant \(=b^2-4ac\)

There are three different types of \(x\)-intercepts, namely:

  • Parabola with two \(x\)-intercepts
  • Parabola with one \(x\)-intercept
  • Parabola with no \(x\)-intercepts

Parabola with one \(x\)-intercept

When the discriminant is equal to zero, the parabola has only one \(x\)-intercept, as is illustrated below.

\(b^2-4ac=0\)

We can determine the \(x\)-intercepts using the given equations, which could also be written as

\( x={\displaystyle\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}}\). 

If \(\PalAa{b^2-4ac}=\PalAa{0}\), the values of the \(\PalAb x\)-intercepts are calculated as:

\(\begin {align}\PalAb x&= \displaystyle\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a} \\ &=-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a} \pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa0}}{2a} \\ &=-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\pm0 \\ &=\PalAb{ -\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}}\\ \end{align}\)

Both expressions reduce to the same, namely

\(x = -\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\).

This is also the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex.  In other words, in this case the vertex of the parabola just touches the \(x\)-axis at this point.

Example

For the parabola

\(y\)

\(=\)

\(x^2-4x+4\),

 

\(a\)

\(=\)

\(1\), \(b=-4\) and \(c=4\).

In the equations

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\PalAb\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\)

the discriminant is

  

 

\(\PalAa{b^2-4ac}\)

 

  

\(=\)

\((-4)^2-4(1)(4)\)
   

\(=\)

\((-4)^2-4\times1\times4\)
   

\(=\)

\(16-16\)
   

\(=\)

\(\PalAa0\).

So, this discriminant is zero.

      

Note value of \(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\)

  

 

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}=-\displaystyle\frac{(-4)}{2(1)}=-\displaystyle\frac{-4}{2}=2\)

So, \(x\)-coordinate of vertex is

  

 

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}=2\)

There is only one \(\PalAb x\)-intercept

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{b^2-4ac}}}{2a}\)

Substitute the discriminant

  

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{b}{2a}\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{(\PalAa{0})}}{2a}\)

Substitute \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\)

  

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{(-4)}{2(1)}\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{0}}}{2(1)}\)
   

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{-4}{2\times1}\pm\displaystyle\frac{\sqrt{\PalAa{0}}}{2\times1}\)
   

\(=\)

\(-\displaystyle\frac{-4}{2}\pm\displaystyle\frac{0}{2}\)
   

\(=\)

\(2\pm0\)
   

\(=\)

\(\PalAb 2\).

So, the \(\PalAb x\)-coordinate is

\(\PalAb x\)

\(=\)

\(\PalAb 2\).

The \(\PalAb x\)-intercept is \((\PalAb2;0)\).  Note that the vertex just touches the \(x\)-axis.
