Interpretation

The following table gives a man's inflation adjusted/real salary below.  The base year is 2008 with a CPI of \(100\).

Year Yearly salary
in Rands		 CPI Inflation adjusted salary
2008 \(\PalAb{\Cur145\Tsp720\Cdp00}\)   \(100,0\)   \(\PalAa{\Cur145\Tsp720\Cdp00}\)
2011 \(\PalAb{\Cur160\Tsp920\Cdp00}\)   \(92,6\)   \(\PalAa{\Cur173\Tsp779\Cdp70}\)
2012 \(\PalAb{\Cur178\Tsp600\Cdp00}\)   \(97,8\)   \(\PalAa{\Cur182\Tsp617\Cdp59}\)

The difference between his nominal salary of 2011 and 2008 is:

\(\PalAb{\Cur160\Tsp920\Cdp00}-\PalAb{\Cur145\Tsp720\Cdp00}=\PalAb{\Cur15\Tsp200\Cdp00}\)

The difference between his real (inflation adjusted) salary of 2011 and 2008 is:

\(\PalAa{\Cur173\Tsp779\Cdp70}-\PalAa{\Cur145\Tsp720\Cdp00}=\PalAa{\Cur28\Tsp059\Cdp70}\)

The conclusion is that although he received a \(\PalAb{\Cur 15 \Tsp 200 \Cdp 00}\) increase in salary from 2008 to 2011, the purchasing power of this money increased by more than that.  The purchasing power of this money increased by \(\PalAa{\Cur 28 \Tsp 059 \Cdp 70}\).  
This increase was because the CPI of 2011 was less than \(100\).  The inflation rate dropped and the purchasing power of a rand increased.

The difference between his nominal salary of 2012 and 2008 is:

\(\PalAb{\Cur178\Tsp600\Cdp00}-\PalAb{\Cur145\Tsp720\Cdp00}=\PalAb{\Cur32\Tsp880\Cdp00}\)

The difference between his real salary of 2012 and 2008 is:

\(\PalAa{\Cur182\Tsp617\Cdp59}-\PalAa{\Cur145\Tsp720\Cdp00}=\PalAa{\Cur36\Tsp897\Cdp59}\)

The conclusion is that although he received a \(\PalAb{\Cur 32 \Tsp 880 \Cdp 00}\) nominal increase in salary from 2008 to 2012, the increase in real salary was \(\PalAa{\Cur 36 \Tsp 897 \Cdp 59}\).  
This increase was because the CPI of 2012 was less than \(100\).

Please note that this comparison of salaries or any other monetary value can only be done between a value in the base year and the same value in some other year.  In the first case the man's 2011 salary was adjusted to the rand value in 2008.  Then the comparison between the 2008 salary and the inflation adjusted 2011 salary was made.  

This comparison may never be done between any other two years.  One of the years has to be the base year.  This means, the difference between his real salary of 2012 and his real salary of 2011 does not represent the change in salary between 2011 and 2012.

We see that if the CPI is less than \(100\), then the increase in the real value of money is greater than the increase in the nominal value of the money.

\(\begin{align} \text{CPI} &< 100\\ \text{increase in } \PalAa{\text{real}}\text{ value}&>\text{increase in }\PalAb{ \text{nominal}}\text{ value} \end{align}\)
