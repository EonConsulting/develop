Recap

A peanut farmer’s cost function in rand, is given by \( {C(x)=\displaystyle\frac{x^2}{100}+100}\)
where \(x\) represents the number of kilograms of peanuts produced.
The revenue function is given by \(\PalBa{R(x)=31 x}\), that is each kilogram of peanuts produced is sold at \(\Cur31\Cdp00\).  The profit function is given by \({P( x)=-\displaystyle\frac{ x^2}{100}+31 x-100}\).

Therefore, the marginal cost is given by the derivative \(\PalAb{C'(x)=\displaystyle\frac{ x}{50}}\) and the marginal profit by the derivative \(\PalAa{P'( x)=-\displaystyle\frac{ x}{50}+31}\).

Marginal profit is the profit that would be made by producing and selling one additional unit of a product or service.  Therefore, the formula for the extra unit can be written as:

marginal profit \(=\) marginal revenue \(-\) marginal cost

So, in the case of the peanut farmer, if:

  marginal revenue \(>\) marginal cost, marginal profit is positive
  marginal revenue \(<\) marginal cost, marginal profit is negative
  marginal revenue \(=\) marginal cost, marginal profit is zero

In general we can say that since total profit increases when marginal profit is positive and total profit decreases when marginal profit is negative, total profit must reach a maximum where marginal profit is zero, or where marginal cost equals marginal revenue.

This is because the producer has collected positive profit up until the intersection of marginal revenue and marginal cost (where zero profit is collected and any further production will result in negative marginal profit, because marginal cost will be larger than marginal revenue).

A company should continue increasing production
and sales until the marginal profit falls to zero.
