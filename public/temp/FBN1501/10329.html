Definition

The mean is sensitive to extreme values and may often lead to misleading conclusions.  Therefore, the median is often preferred as a measure of locality.

The median \((Me)\) is the score value which cuts the distribution in half,
such that half the scores fall above the median and half fall below it.

Computation of the median is relatively straightforward.

We follow two different procedures depending on the number of data values in the sample:

  • Computing the median of an odd number of data values in the sample
  • Computing the median of an even number of data values in the sample

The first step in both procedures is the same, namely to order the data values from the smallest to largest.
