Method

Systematic sampling starts at a randomly chosen starting point in the population.
Then each subsequent \(\PalAb{k^{\text{th}}}\) element is chosen.

Step 1 Define the population.
Step 2 Determine the desired sample size.
Step 3 Obtain a list, preferably a randomised list of the population.
Step 4 Determine what \(\PalAb k\) is equal to by dividing the size of the population by the desired sample size.
Step 5 Start at some random place at the top of the population list.
Step 6 Starting at that point, take every \(\PalAb{k^{\text{th}}}\) name on the list until the desired sample size is reached.
Step 7 If the end of the list is reached before the desired sample is reached, go back to the top of the list.

 
In this case, every third item from the population is systematically selected.
