Examples

Roots can extend to a higher order than cube roots.

The fourth root

The fourth root of a number is a number that, when taken to the fourth power, is equal to the given number.

Example

\(\sqrt[4]{a^{20}}\)

Consider \(a^{20}\).  Twenty is a multiple of four.  The fourth root of \(a^{20}\) is \(a^{20\div4}\) and that is equal to \(a^5\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[\PalAb 4]{a^{\PalAa{20}}}=a^{\PalAa{20}\div\PalAb4}=a^5\)

Higher index roots

The fifth root of a number is a number that, when taken to the fifth power, is equal to the given number, and so forth.

Example

\(\sqrt[6]{b^{24}}\)

Consider \(b^{24}\).  Twenty four is a multiple of six.  The sixth root of \(b^{24}\) is \(b^{24\div6}\) and that is equal to \(b^4\).

This gives:

\(\sqrt[\PalAb6]{b^{\PalAa{24}}}=b^{\PalAa{24}\div\PalAb6}=b^4\)
